# Tarim-Shaiel - Story Skeleton

## Core Concept
A TTRPG where legendary heroes start at the peak of their power, achieve ultimate victory, then mysteriously fall from their earned paradise and must climb back.

---

## Confirmed Structure

### Episode 0: The Victory
- **One session only**
- Players create god-tier characters (max power, whatever they want)
- Final epic battle - they WIN decisively
- Bonds form under fire
- Immediate aftermath: "...now what?"
- They hear of Hero Heaven (their earned Valhalla)
- **End state**: Peak power, world saved, purpose fulfilled

### The Fall
- **Instant. No stops. Elevator to hell.**
- One moment: gods
- Next moment: nothing
- No memory of WHY
- No warning

### Episode 1+: The Climb
- Wake up as weak as they'll ever be (essentially level 1)
- **But they remember being gods** (psychological torment)
- Traditional RPG power curve (weak → strong)
- Inverted emotional arc (confident → humbled → rebuilding)

---

## Time & Place

### The Gap
- **200-500 years have passed**
- Long enough for memory to become less important than myth
- Everyone they knew is dead
- Nations changed, world evolved
- They are now LEGEND, not history

### Physical State
- **Same age** (preserved in Hero Heaven)
- World moved on without them
- They're walking anachronisms

### Their Legend
*Still to be determined:*
- Heroes who vanished mysteriously?
- Villains who were overthrown?
- The Cataclysm (blamed for both salvation AND what came after)?
- Largely forgotten (only scholars remember)?

---

## The Mystery

### What They Don't Know
- **WHY did they fall from Hero Heaven?**
- What happened in the gap?
- What is the truth of their legend?

### What They Vaguely Know
- **One person remembers "the way back"** (barely, fragments)
- Destination: Return to Hero Heaven
- Method: Unknown (door? ritual? trial? worthy death?)

### The Betrayal
*Nature still undecided - possibilities include:*
- Other heroes in Hero Heaven saw them as threats/wrong fit
- Hero Heaven itself is not what it seemed
- The powers/gods running it had other plans
- One of THEM did something that got them all expelled
- They were never meant to stay
- "Were we actually the villains?"

---

## Key Design Principles

### Asymmetry
- **At least one character affected differently** by the fall
- Could be PC or NPC
- Creates internal tension/mystery

### Motivation
- **Personal/Selfish**: They want THEIR paradise back
- NOT about saving the world again
- The current world is a means to an end
- "We earned this. Someone stole it from us."

### Episode 0 Character Sheets
- **Cyphers, not constraints**
- Show who they were
- Not required to match future progression
- Freedom to be ridiculous

### Memory Recovery
- **Piecemeal** throughout campaign
- Triggers TBD (time? locations? items? each other?)
- Key moments revealed through play
- Players learn the truth gradually

## Still Open Questions

### Story
- Exact nature of the betrayal
- What "the way back" actually requires
- Whether they CAN actually return (or if that's the wrong goal)
- The shape of their legend in the present

### Mechanics
- How do they awaken? (Together or separate?)
- What triggers memory returns?
- Do they regain OLD powers or discover NEW ones?
- Is progression same character sheets climbing back, or different?

### Campaign Structure
- How many episodes total?
- Key milestone revelations
- Episodic vs. serial balance
- How nonlinearity (if any) serves the mystery

---

## The Emotional Core

**Gap between memory and capability:**
- "I know I can do this... why can't I do this?"
- They remember the spell, the strength, the respect
- But they can't access it
- YET

**The hollowness of victory:**
- Purpose-built beings (heroes) struggling when purpose is fulfilled
- The retired athlete problem
- Post-war soldier problem
- For fantasy heroes

**Theft of earned rest:**
- They DID the work
- They WON
- Someone took their reward
- Quest for justice/revenge/understanding
