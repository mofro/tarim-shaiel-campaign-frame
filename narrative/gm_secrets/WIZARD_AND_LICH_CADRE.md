# The Wizard and Lich Cadre [CLASSIFIED - DM ONLY]

⚠️ **CLASSIFIED DOCUMENT - FOR DUNGEON MASTER ONLY**
**DO NOT SHARE WITH PLAYERS**

---

## Overview

This document contains **complete information** about the primary antagonist (the Wizard), the Lich Cadre commanders, and the Lich-Legion threat. This information is hidden from players and should be revealed gradually through campaign progression.

**Cosmological context:** The Wizard's plan is not merely a military or personal threat — it is an assault on the mythic ecosystem itself. His agents and the ecosystem's immune response are in active, ongoing conflict throughout the campaign, including at the heroes' very first moments in the mortal world. See [[STORY_ARC_SYNTHESIS]] for the full cosmological picture; this document focuses on the Wizard's side of that conflict.

### 2. The Lich Cadre: Commanders & Legions

**Composition (8-12 Liches):**
Each Lich should be:
- **Different species** (shows Wizard's manipulation across all cultures)
- **Recruited at different times** (~400-900 CE across the timeline)
- **Unaware of each other** (each believes they're pursuing individual project)
- **Positioned strategically** (geographic distribution for coordinated assault)

**Individual Lich Profiles:**
For each Lich, document:
- **Species and original identity** (name, background, ambitions)
- **Recruitment story** (how Wizard identified and cultivated them)
- **Transformation timing** (when they became a Lich)
- **Stronghold location** (geographic position, defensive capabilities)
- **Undead legion composition** (size, troop types, special capabilities)
- **Personality and tactics** (combat style, strategic approach)
- **Relationship to Wizard** (how much do they know? Loyal or manipulated?)

**Suggested Lich Types:**
- High Elf seeking to prevent "diminishing" (tragic, wants to preserve beauty)
- Dwarf archivist obsessed with preserving knowledge beyond mortality (scholarly)
- Human warlord's court mage fearing mortality (power-hungry)
- Orc shaman wanting to preserve liberation memory forever (well-intentioned)
- Goblin merchant-prince seeking ultimate power (ruthless pragmatist)
- Mortal Elf historian trying to record all history personally (obsessive)
- Human scholar investigating ancient magic (curiosity-driven)
- Faun artist wanting immortal time to create masterworks (artistic)

**Undead Legion Details:**
- Total forces: ~80,000-120,000 across all Liches
- Troop types: Zombies (frontline), skeletons (archers), wights (commanders), wraiths (special ops)
- Command structure: Lich → Wight lieutenants → Lesser undead
- Special capabilities: Some legions specialized (siege, cavalry, infiltration)
- Weaknesses: Coordinated control vulnerable to disruption

### 3. The Lich-Legion as Mongol Analog

**Tactical Parallels:**
- **Speed:** Undead never tire; can march continuously
- **Coordination:** Magical communication enables perfect synchronization
- **Terror:** Psychological warfare through undead horror
- **Logistics:** No supply lines needed (undead don't eat/sleep)
- **Adaptability:** Wizard can redirect forces instantly via scrying

**Strategic Differences:**
- **Not seeking to conquer/rule:** Wizard wants Hero Heaven access, not territory
- **Diversionary tactics:** Attacks create chaos to cover Wizard's true goal
- **Expendable forces:** Undead losses don't matter (can reanimate casualties)
- **Hidden until activated:** Unlike Mongols, Lich-Legion was secret for centuries

### 4. The 1,000-Year Plan: Timeline & Mechanics

**Phase 1: Survival & Research (~250-400 CE)**
- Escape and establish hidden sanctum
- Research Hero Heaven (what is it? How to access?)
- Develop necromancy (perfect binding magic on the dead)

**Phase 2: Recruitment & Positioning (~400-700 CE)**
- Identify and cultivate proto-Liches across species
- Establish multiple personas in different courts
- Build initial undead forces from battlefields and crypts

**Phase 3: Consolidation (~700-1000 CE)**
- Complete Lich transformations
- Stockpile corpses and create massive undead legions
- Infiltrate institutions and create dependencies
- Test coordination (Khorashar incident)

**Phase 4: Triggering the Gaes (~1000-1200 CE)**
- Locate Hero Heaven threshold
- Discover fallen teammate connection
- Enact gaes to expel heroes
- **Deploy agents to intercept heroes at their awakening sites** — the gaes forces expulsion but cannot control where or how the heroes land; the Wizard's hierarchy moves quickly to locate, surveil, and if possible capture or neutralize them before they can orient. This is an active operation, not passive waiting.
- The ecosystem's immune response contests this: the heroes' landing sites were not random — each was selected or shaped to provide cover, protection, or advantage. The forces arrayed against the heroes at their awakenings are the first visible edge of this larger conflict.
- Position for final assault

*See individual awakening GM notes for how this plays out at each site.*

**Phase 5: Endgame (~1200+ CE, Campaign Present)**
- Heroes survive awakening (unexpected — the interception failed)
- Heroes orient, converge, begin to understand their charge
- Initiate Lich-Legion attacks (distraction and pressure)
- Attempt to breach Hero Heaven threshold
- Final confrontation

### 5. The Gaes Mechanism

**How It Works:**
- Uses fallen teammate's sacrifice as spiritual anchor
- Twists noble death into binding compulsion through necromancy
- Creates paradox: Heroes earned reward but charge incomplete
- Forces expulsion from Hero Heaven back to mortal world

**Magical Components:**
- Connection to fallen teammate's essence (preserved through necromancy)
- Link to Hero Heaven threshold (discovered through research)
- Binding ritual requiring immense power (why Wizard waited so long)

**Effects on Heroes:**
- Experience as betrayal, confusion, devastating self-doubt
- Carry compulsion but don't understand its source initially
- Must discover gaes origin through investigation
- Breaking gaes might require confronting fallen teammate's shade/memory

### 7. Campaign Revelation Structure

**Early Campaign (Sessions 1-10):**

- Multiple possible outcomes:

  - Sacrifice to seal threshold permanently
  - Negotiate/redeem Wizard (very difficult)
  - Partial victory (defeat but threshold compromised)

### 8. DM Guidance: When to Reveal What

**Never Reveal Directly:**
- Wizard's complete 1,000-year plan (let players piece together)
- Exact number of Liches until late campaign
- Full extent of undead forces
- Wizard's true motivation (keep ambiguous until endgame)

**Reveal Through Investigation:**
- Individual Lich identities (after defeating each)
- Undead legion locations (through captured intelligence)
- Gaes mechanism (through magical research or NPC allies)
- Fallen teammate connection (emotional climax)

**Reveal Through Combat:**
- Lich capabilities and tactics (each fight shows different approach)
- Undead troop types and weaknesses
- Coordination mechanisms (scrying, sending stones)

**Reveal Through NPCs:**
- Elf elders notice patterns across centuries
- Dwarf archivists connect disappearances
- Survivors of Khorashar (if any found)
- Captured/turned Wizard agents

- **Plan has gaps heroes can exploit:** Not inevitable; player choices matter
- **Moral complexity:** Some Liches had good intentions (Orc shaman, Dwarf archivist)
- **Multiple endings possible:** Heroes' choices determine outcome
