## Section 1: The Information Landscape

The heroes return to a world that has been coping with an incomprehensible situation for a thousand years. The Held Breath stirred when the liberation happened — a beacon event that sent Warren ripples continent-wide, drawing attention from entities with their own agendas. Every faction that noticed has been working, in isolation, with the piece of the picture they could reach.

No single actor has the full picture. This is not an accident of history. It is a structural feature of the cosmological architecture:

The Held Breath are liminal consciousnesses that exist between states — between dormancy and waking, between the Warren layer and the world above. Their nature resists direct observation. What different factions sense is real but partial: anomalous data, degrading traditions, fraying metaphysical infrastructure, a low-frequency dread that no existing framework can adequately name.

The Wizard has spent a thousand years acting on his partial picture, and his confidence is itself a product of a thousand years of Held Breath influence. He believes he understands the situation. His certainty is the danger.

The heroes are uniquely positioned to synthesize distributed knowledge because:

- Their tools are Warren ambassadors — they can sense the ecosystem's actual state where others only observe surface symptoms.
- They are legend to every culture on the continent — they can move between factions that do not speak to each other.
- They carry celestial memories — they recognize what the distributed knowledge is describing because they lived inside it.
- They bridge Orc, Dwarf, Elf, and human relationships across political fractures that no current-era being can navigate alone.

The Information Synthesis Puzzle is not a quest to find the MacGuffin. It is the process by which the heroes become the only beings in a thousand years who hold the complete picture — and must decide what to do with it.

## Section 2: Stakeholder Entries

### 1. High Elves (Elven Highland Enclaves)

**What they know:** The most cosmologically complete mortal knowledge in the setting. High Elven traditions preserved frameworks from before the current era that describe the Warren layer, the Held Breath's nature, and the structural relationship between the liberation and what followed. They understand that the liberation was a beacon event. They understand that the Held Breath are liminal consciousnesses, not a geological force. They withdrew from the fault line a millennium ago — not out of indifference, but because they calculated that proximity increased the risk of drawing full attention.

**What they don't know:** The current trajectory. Their cosmological framework describes structure, not dynamics. They know what the Held Breath is and what waking would mean; they do not know how close the threshold is, what the Wizard has done with a thousand years of preparation, or what the heroes' tools are actually reporting from the Warren layer.

**Stance / how to approach:** Formal and evaluative. They will not offer their knowledge to heroes who treat this as a problem to be solved quickly. They are testing whether the heroes understand the stakes — not as a puzzle to win, but as a weight to carry. Intellectual humility and demonstrated cosmological awareness unlock them. Urgency closes them.

**What they fear / stakes:** The Held Breath waking in full. They have held the cosmological framework for a thousand years specifically to be available if something with the standing to use it arrived. They fear dying with the knowledge unused.

**What they offer:** The full cosmological architecture — the framework that makes all other stakeholders' data interpretable. Without this framework, the heroes have data. With it, they have understanding.

**Access conditions:** Hidden sanctuaries in the highland enclaves. A Mortal Elf elder serves as the necessary bridge — the High Elves will not open a conversation cold. Probably mid-Act II. Unlocks interpretation of all other assembled data.

### 2. Mortal Elves

**What they know:** They sense it but cannot name it. A persistent wrongness in the way the world feels — the emotional texture of places has changed, certain old harmonics in elvish traditions no longer resonate the way they should, and elder Mortal Elves report that the quality of their intuitions about the future has degraded over centuries. They experience this as a spiritual malaise rather than a cosmological event.

**What they don't know:** They do not have the High Elves' framework. They cannot tell the heroes what is happening — only that something is happening, and that it has been happening for a very long time.

**Stance / how to approach:** Open but bewildered. Mortal Elves feel the return of beings with celestial-touched tools as a profound rightness — a thing that has been absent returning. They are not gatekeepers; they are witnesses. They will guide toward what they sense and introduce heroes to High Elf elders if the relationship is built with patience.

**What they fear / stakes:** The degradation becoming irreversible. The slow fading of a world they have been keeping faith with for a thousand years without understanding why it was necessary.

**What they offer:** Emotional and intuitive guidance. The pathway to High Elf contact. A living record of how the world has changed experientially over centuries.

**Access conditions:** Isolated enclaves; some integrated into broader communities. Act I-II. Low access bar — the heroes' nature opens doors. The relationship takes patience, not cleverness.

### 3. Dwarven Archivists (Dwarven Mountain Confederations / Tarim Authority)

**What they know:** A thousand years of empirical data without theoretical framework. Dwarven archives contain meticulous records of anomalies: trade route disruptions correlated with specific geographic zones, seismic pattern irregularities that do not fit geological models, disappearances of individuals with unusual properties (mages, seers, people with Warren-adjacent abilities) at statistically improbable rates. They have been noting these patterns with the same rigor they apply to mining assays and trade volumes. They have no explanation for them.

**What they don't know:** What any of it means. Their data is empirically robust and theoretically useless to them. They have not connected the disappearances to the Scholar's Purge. They have not connected the geographic anomalies to Warren disturbance. They are archivists waiting for a framework.

**Stance / how to approach:** Practical and collaborative. The Dwarven Archivists are not suspicious — they want someone to help them understand what they've been recording. Heroes who come with a theoretical framework (or who are visibly building one) and treat the data as valuable will receive full cooperation.

**What they fear / stakes:** The anomalies accelerating past the point where their systems can function. Dwarven civilization is infrastructure-dependent; Warren disturbance threatening the deep mountain systems is an existential concern, not an abstract one.

**What they offer:** The most extensive longitudinal empirical dataset in the setting. A thousand years of pattern data. The disappearance records that, combined with other sources, confirm the Scholar's Purge as targeted.

**Access conditions:** Mountain archives; politically marginalized within the Tarim Authority but willing. Act I-II. Data is immediately available if trust is established; the heroes may not be able to interpret it yet without the High Elf framework.

### 4. Human Scholars (Scholar's Remnant)

**What they know:** Practical fragments collected by the survivors of a systematic purge. The Scholar's Remnant holds: dead zone mapping (geographic areas where magic fails or inverts), accounts of what the original scholars understood about the liberation before the purge, and awareness that their knowledge makes them targets. Several senior members understand, at a practical level, that they hold pieces of something that someone does not want assembled.

**What they don't know:** The full cosmological framework. The scale of what they're holding. Most Scholar's Remnant members know their fragment but not how it fits — they've been isolated by the purge and by survival necessity.

**Stance / how to approach:** Cautious and conditionally trusting. The Scholar's Remnant has been hunted for centuries. They will not open up to heroes who seem connected to any institutional authority — including the Wizard's network, which they have learned to recognize in disguise. Heroes who demonstrate protection capacity and who show they are genuinely assembling the picture (rather than interrogating) unlock them.

**What they fear / stakes:** Extinction. The Scholar's Purge was not finished. What they carry getting destroyed with them.

**What they offer:** The Wizard motive layer. Dead zone mapping that completes the geographic picture. The pre-purge scholars' framework fragments that help confirm what the liberation actually was. Evidence that the purge was targeted — connecting Dwarven disappearance records to deliberate elimination of cosmological knowledge.

**Access conditions:** Hidden and scattered; some in Goblin Free Cities neutral ground where Wizard's agents operate with more friction. Act II. Protection is the price — not gold, not knowledge, but demonstrated safety.

### 5. Orcish Confederations

**What they know:** Two distinct knowledge streams. First: living ecosystem sensing through ancestor communication — Orcish shamans have been noting for centuries that the quality of ancestor contact is degrading, specific spirits are becoming unreachable, and certain ritual sites have gone silent. Second: ancient epic songs that contain, in metaphorical form, literal descriptions of the Held Breath. The songs predate the liberation. Orcish oral tradition has been faithfully preserving descriptions of the liminal consciousnesses for longer than the current era has existed.

**What they don't know:** That the songs are literal. The epic tradition treats the Held Breath entities as metaphors for cosmic forces — powerful and worth respecting, but not understood as actual beings with agendas. The shamans who sense the degradation of ancestor contact have no framework that connects it to the same phenomenon.

**Stance / how to approach:** The liberation was RIGHT. This is not ambiguous within Orcish cultural memory. The heroes are legendary figures associated with a liberation that Orcish tradition remembers as unambiguously correct. The incomplete charge is about agendas woken by the beacon event — not about ecological repair, not about the liberation being a mistake. Heroes who approach with that framing receive openness. Heroes who suggest the liberation caused damage will be received with profound cultural offense.

**What they fear / stakes:** The ancestors going fully silent. The degradation of ancestor communication is the most sacred wound in contemporary Orcish culture; it is experienced as the dead abandoning the living, which is understood as cosmological wrongness at the deepest level.

**What they offer:** Ecosystem health sensing that no other faction can provide. The epic songs, if the heroes have the framework to recognize what they contain. A pathway to the Chain-Breakers Order, who are Orc-anchored.

**Access conditions:** Steppe confederations; culturally accessible to heroes as living legends. Act I. Early access if framing is right; one of the best first contacts in the campaign.

### 6. Goblins / Halflings / Gnomes (Free Cities + Peoples of the Nine Roads + Gnome Guilds)

**What they know:** The widest observational dataset on the continent, with no theoretical framework whatsoever. Trade networks have logged: charm failure rates by geographic zone and decade (Gnome Guilds), travel anomalies and disappearances along trade routes (Peoples of the Nine Roads), unusual customers and unusual requests at Free Cities markets (which are a clearinghouse for everyone who needs neutral ground). They know the shape of the symptoms better than anyone. They have not been trying to explain it — they've been adapting to it.

**What they don't know:** Anything about what it means. The charm failure data is a business problem. The disappearances are a safety concern. The unusual customers are just customers.

**Stance / how to approach:** Transactional and pragmatic. Information has a price — not necessarily gold, but reciprocity. Heroes who bring value (news, connections, skills, protection capacity, or just good stories) receive information. Heroes who demand information and offer nothing are politely ignored or actively misdirected.

**What they fear / stakes:** The trade networks failing. Free Cities culture is resilient but infrastructure-dependent; zones where trade cannot function reliably threaten the civilization model.

**What they offer:** The continent-wide empirical dataset. The best first-contact information network in the campaign. Potentially neutral ground for meeting Scholar's Remnant contacts.

**Access conditions:** Everywhere; accessible earliest. Act I. Best first contact; lowest bar to information exchange. Goblin Free Cities are the practical hub of the information gathering phase.

### 7. The Wizard

**What he knows:** Everything — and has known for a long time. The Held Breath are real. The liberation was a beacon event. The threshold is approaching. He understands the cosmological architecture. He also has been the subject of a thousand years of Held Breath influence that he has interpreted as his own insight and exceptional understanding. He believes he can manage what he has been encouraged to pursue. His knowledge is real. His confidence in his ability to handle the outcome is a product of grooming he cannot see.

**What he doesn't know:** That he is being used. He has arrived at genuine cosmological understanding through a path the Held Breath prepared for him, and the understanding is accurate enough that it confirms his confidence at every step. He does not know that his sense of control is precisely what the Held Breath requires.

**Stance / how to approach:** He is not a fool and not a villain by self-understanding. He is a brilliant individual who has spent a thousand years becoming more certain that he alone is equipped to manage this crisis. Heroes who engage with him as a peer — who demonstrate they understand the actual cosmological stakes — may find him capable of genuine conversation. Heroes who oppose him without that understanding will be dismissed.

**What he fears / stakes:** Losing control of the outcome. The Held Breath waking outside the conditions he has prepared. He fears the heroes specifically because they are the only beings in the world who could complete the charge he has spent a thousand years preventing anyone from completing.

**What he offers (as a stakeholder):** A complete cosmological picture — but filtered through his certainty that his management approach is the right one. He will tell the heroes more truth than almost any other source. He will not tell them he is wrong.

**Access conditions:** He will find the heroes. He does not hide from them. The question is when the conversation happens and on whose terms.

### 8. The Heroes (Self-Knowledge)

**What they have:** Celestial-touched tools that function as Warren ambassadors — the most direct ecosystem sensing available to any mortal being. Fragments of celestial memory that will resurface as the tools' relationship deepens. Standing with every culture on the continent that no current-era being can replicate. The capacity to bridge factions that do not speak to each other.

**What they don't have (yet):** Understanding of what their tools are reporting. Access to their own celestial memories. A framework for interpreting what they sense. The knowledge of what their charge actually was — not their understanding of it, but the actual charge.

**The synthesis key:** The heroes are not information collectors who eventually get handed the complete picture. They are the framework. As they develop the R/H/K relationship with their tools and accumulate fragments from other stakeholders, they begin to recognize what the distributed knowledge is describing — because they lived inside the ecosystem, and the tools remember.

**The unique position:** They are the only beings who can hold all of this simultaneously. High Elves will not speak to Orcish Shamans about the Held Breath. Scholar's Remnant cannot reach Dwarven archives safely. Chain-Breakers oral tradition has never been compared to High Elf cosmological frameworks. The heroes, by virtue of their legend and their celestial nature, can assemble the complete picture. No one else can.

---

### 9. Chain-Breakers Order

**What they know:** Oral tradition fragments that survive from the Scholar's Purge — the ground-level knowledge that the Wizard's agents worked hardest to eliminate. The Chain-Breakers hold: the mechanics of how a gaes operates and can be completed (not theory — practical, earned knowledge); fragments of the heroes' actual unfinished charge (not the stories, but the structural requirements); accounts of the Wizard's original intervention and what he prevented; and a theological conviction that the heroes' return is cosmologically necessary. They have been keeping this faith for a thousand years, across a multi-species community anchored in Orcish tradition.

**What they don't know:** The cosmological framework. The Held Breath's nature at the architectural level. How their fragments fit into the complete picture. They know what was left undone and how to recognize its completion. They do not know why it matters at the cosmological scale.

**Stance / how to approach:** The heroes are expected. Not hoped for — expected. The Chain-Breakers have organized their entire tradition around the certainty that the heroes would return. They will be overwhelmed and may project their theological expectations onto the heroes uncomfortably. They need the heroes to hear the difficult truth about the incomplete charge without becoming defensive about what was left undone. The Chain-Breakers are not accusatory — they are witnesses.

**What they fear / stakes:** The heroes returning and not completing the charge a second time. They have held this faith for a thousand years. The possibility that the heroes are back but will miss the unfinished work again is their deepest dread.

**What they offer:** The clearest practical description of the charge requirements. Oral accounts of what the Wizard's original intervention blocked. Ground-level understanding of the gaes mechanics that no other source provides. A community of multi-species allies who have been preparing for the heroes' return.

**Access conditions:** Dispersed but Orc-anchored; find them via Orcish Confederation contacts. Act I-II. Trust is the price — they will not share the deepest fragments with heroes who seem unwilling to hear uncomfortable truths about what was left unfinished.

### Synthesis Dependencies

What becomes visible when specific combinations are assembled:

**Full scope of Warren disturbance:**
Goblin/Halfling empirical dataset + Dwarven anomaly patterns + High Elf cosmological framework → the geographic and temporal scale of the Warren disturbance becomes visible; the dead zones are legible as disturbance geography, not random failure.

**Complete picture of the liberation's beacon event and what it woke:**
Chain-Breakers oral tradition + Orcish epic songs + heroes' own celestial memories (via tools) → the liberation's nature as a beacon event is confirmed; what was woken and whose plans were crossed becomes nameable.

**Wizard motive layer confirmed:**
Scholar's Remnant fragments + Dwarven disappearance timeline → the Scholar's Purge as targeted cover-up is confirmed; the Wizard's awareness of the cosmological risk is confirmed; his role in eliminating the knowledge that could have completed the charge a thousand years ago becomes clear.

**The full picture:**
All three above + Sky-Father access → the heroes recognize they are the only beings in a thousand years who hold the complete picture; they understand the Wizard's plan, the Held Breath's trajectory, and what completing the charge actually requires.

**The heroes' own history:**
Memory-Keeper access (full, Act III) → the heroes know their actual charge — not the stories about it, not the fragments, the charge itself. This is the final piece. It arrives last because arriving first would be incapacitating.

### Heroes' Unique Position

Why the synthesis is possible only through the heroes:

Their tools are Warren ambassadors — they can sense ecosystem health directly where others only observe symptoms. They are legend to every culture on the continent — they can access factions that have not spoken to each other in centuries or ever. They carry celestial memories — they recognize what the distributed knowledge is describing because they lived inside the ecosystem it describes. They bridge Orc, Dwarf, Elf, and human relationships across political fractures that no current-era being can navigate alone.

The heroes are not the solution. They are the synthesis. The solution emerges from what they do with the complete picture once assembled.
