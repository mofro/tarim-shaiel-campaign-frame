# Divine Names Across Cultures

---
### 1. The Sky-Father

**Central Asian (Turkic/Mongol)**
- Tengri-Ata *(Sky-Father — near-literal; the name most shamans use)*
- Kök Tengri *(Blue Heaven — the vault itself as person)*
- Uzak Tengri *(Distant Heaven — acknowledges his remove)*

**Persian/Zoroastrian**
- Ahura Dadar *(Lord-Creator — closely convergent with Tengri-Ata; priests of both traditions have noted the overlap and mostly ignored its implications)*
- Vahu Manah Bozorg *(The Great Good Mind — emphasizes his calculated principled-inaction as *wisdom*, not negligence)*

**Tang Chinese**
- Tiān Fù *(Heavenly Father — common usage)*
- Yuǎn Tiān *(The Far Heaven — convergent with Uzak Tengri; travelers who've crossed both traditions notice this)*

**Indian Subcontinent**
- Dyaus Pitar *(Sky-Father — ancient, pre-Vedic; scholars consider it archaic)*
- Antariksha-Swami *(Lord of the Between-Space — emphasizes his position above and apart)*

**Orcish**
- Keth-Varal *(He Who Holds the Oldest Bargain)*
- The First Creditor *(common usage in Bondkeeper oral tradition — he is owed, not worshipped)*

**Elvish**
- Aelon-Dûr *(The Old Jurisdiction — familiarity with disdain; implies his authority is inherited, not earned)*
- The Senior Arrangement *(informal; what Elves say when they don't want to use his name)*

**Dwarven**
- The Overburden *(the geological pressure of sky on stone — accurate, unsentimental)*
- The Arch-Load *(the force the whole structure is built to manage)*

**Wizard-Tradition (Arabic/Persian scholarly)**
- Al-Muqīm al-A'lā *(The Highest Sustainer — implies passive maintenance, which scholars consider theologically precise)*
- Rabb al-Tawāzun *(Lord of Balance — used in treatises on cosmological equilibrium)*

### 2. The Weighmaster

**Central Asian**
- Erlik-Tergüülegch *(not Erlik himself — the one who counts above Erlik; a distinction shamans are careful about)*
- Toktоо Ezen *(Lord of Settlement — debts resolved, accounts closed)*

**Persian/Zoroastrian**
- Rashnu *(already canonical in Zoroastrian tradition — the divine judge at the scales; this name barely needs inventing)*
- Dādvar-e Kohan *(The Ancient Adjudicator)*

**Tang Chinese**
- Tiāncáo Zhǔ *(Master of the Celestial Registry)*
- Dì Bù Wú Quàn *(He For Whom No Debt Goes Unrecorded — a saying that became a name)*

**Indian Subcontinent**
- Varuna-Chitra *(Varuna-the-Precise — Chitra means vivid/exact; this is the name used by astronomers and accountants)*
- Karma-Dhara *(Holder of the Flow of Consequence — less personal, more systemic)*

**Orcish**
- The Ledger Before the Ledger *(the record that predates Orcish chain-keeping)*
- Varath-Keth *(He Who Was Owed Before We Were Born)*

**Elvish**
- The Accountant *(blunt, mildly contemptuous — Elves find his precision without wisdom tedious)*
- Sulë-Mīr *(The Breath-Price — implies everything costs something, even existing)*

**Dwarven**
- The Yield Assessor *(what a vein of ore produces versus what was spent extracting it — applied cosmologically)*
- The Stress Ledger *(every force applied leaves a record in the stone)*

**Wizard-Tradition**
- Al-Ḥāsib al-Azali *(The Eternal Reckoner — one of the 99 names of God repurposed; scholars are divided on whether this is appropriate)*
- Mīzān al-Ghayb *(The Hidden Scale)*

### 3. The Shattered King

**Central Asian**
- Erlik Khan *(canonical underworld lord — this is his name here, taken directly)*
- Yer-Tanrı *(Earth-God — the one beneath, not above)*
- Kara Tengri *(Black Heaven — the inverse sky)*

**Persian/Zoroastrian**
- Ahriman-e Kohan *(The Ancient Ahriman — distinguished from Ahriman-as-active-evil; this is older, more geological)*
- Mard-e Shekasteh *(The Broken Man — used by those who pity him, which is dangerous)*

**Tang Chinese**
- Pò Wáng *(The Shattered King — direct translation, used by those who know)*
- Dì Xià Gǔ Zhǔ *(The Ancient Lord Below — polite, used in formal contexts to avoid drawing attention)*

**Indian Subcontinent**
- Vibhīṣaṇa-Purva *(Before-Vibhishana — the dignity without the redemption arc)*
- Kṣaya-Raja *(King of Diminishment/Decay)*

**Orcish**
- The Unchained One *(ironic — he was never in chains, but the Orc concept of liberation makes this a warning, not an honor)*
- Grath-Valon *(He Whose Domain Grows When Ours Ends)*

**Elvish**
- The Patient Landlord *(everyone eventually pays him rent)*
- Dûr-Sîn *(The Deep Tenure — he was here before the current order and they know it)*

**Dwarven**
- The Played-Out Seam *(a mine that has given everything — what remains is hollow and unstable)*
- The Subsidence *(ground collapse; what happens when the structure beneath fails)*

**Wizard-Tradition**
- Malik al-Kasr *(King of the Break/Fracture)*
- Al-Qadīm al-Asfal *(The Ancient Below)*

### 4. The Jade Illusionist

**Central Asian**
- Aldarchī *(The Deceiver — affectionate in tone, which is itself suspicious)*
- Köz Boyauchi *(The Eye-Painter — one who makes you see what isn't there)*

**Persian/Zoroastrian**
- Ayyār-e Āsmān *(The Celestial Trickster — ayyār has connotations of rogue, spy, lovable scoundrel)*
- Divāneh-ye Dānā *(The Wise Fool — the one whose foolishness is deliberate and cuts deeper than wisdom)*

**Tang Chinese**
- Yù Huàn Zhě *(The Jade Illusionist — direct; used formally)*
- Hú Xiān *(Fox Immortal — the elevation of the huli jing concept to divine scale)*
- Biàn Liǎn Shén *(The Face-Changing God)*

**Indian Subcontinent**
- Māyā-Siddha *(The Perfected Illusionist — one who has mastered illusion completely)*
- Vetāla-Deva *(The Divine Vetala — the trickster-spirit at celestial elevation)*

**Orcish**
- The Unbound Witness *(watches everything, owes nothing)*
- Rel-Ashkar *(He Who Remembers the Joke — Orc humor, but the name is old and means something)*

**Elvish**
- The Amusing One *(said with genuine warmth and deep wariness)*
- Ilë-Mith *(Starlight-Smoke — beautiful, insubstantial, goes where it likes)*

**Dwarven**
- The False Assay *(ore that tests as one thing and is another — the worst thing a Dwarf can encounter)*
- The Unstable Compound *(reacts unpredictably; handle with caution)*

**Wizard-Tradition**
- Al-Khayāl al-Ḥakīm *(The Wise Phantom/Illusion)*
- Ṣāḥib al-Wujūh *(Master of Faces)*

**Random Nicknames**
* "The Emerald" (*like a joke of a formal name or title — references their penchant for green as a "tell" that they are what they are*)
* "Spring Whisp" (*plays on both the colors of spring and the ephemeral nature of their humor*)

### 5. The Conquering Heaven

**Central Asian**
- Jayagchi Tengri *(Victorious Heaven)*
- Köк Noyon *(Blue Lord — territorial claim on the sky itself)*

**Persian/Zoroastrian**
- Bahram-e Āsmān *(Celestial Bahram — Bahram is already the Persian war-god/victory principle)*
- Azar-Khoda *(Fire-Lord — territorial, military, ancient claim)*

**Tang Chinese**
- Zhēngfú Tiān *(Conquering Heaven — direct)*
- Lóng Wáng Shàng *(The Dragon King Above — territorial, deal-making, ancient claims over specific domains)*

**Indian Subcontinent**
- Indra-Vijaya *(Victory-Indra — the martial aspect foregrounded)*
- Dyu-Pati *(Lord of the Bright Sky — territorial claim)*

**Orcish**
- The Heaven That Charges Rent *(blunt; Orcs have dealt with territorial divine claims before and are unimpressed)*
- Keth-Doral *(He Whose Bargains Have Borders)*

**Elvish**
- The Ambitious Neighbor *(said with the tone of someone who has watched this behavior for millennia)*
- Galad-Môr *(Bright-Dark — the light of military victory with shadow underneath)*

**Dwarven**
- The Fault Line *(pressure moving laterally — not the weight above, but the force that pushes sideways and causes shearing)*
- The Competing Load *(two structural forces that cannot both be fully expressed)*

**Wizard-Tradition**
- Al-Fātiḥ al-Samāwī *(The Celestial Conqueror)*
- Rabb al-Ḥudūd *(Lord of Borders/Boundaries)*

### 6. The Chaos Weaver
*Epithets only — not a person, not a presence. Something closer to a diagnosis.*

**Central Asian**
- Tog'ri Emes *(Not-Straight — the thing that makes paths curve wrong)*
- Bosh Tengri *(Empty Heaven — where the sky should be and isn't)*

**Persian/Zoroastrian**
- Bi-Surat *(Formlessness — the state before the world was shaped)*
- Hundun-Ahriman *(The Formless Evil — a syncretism that appears in border-region texts; scholars dispute whether this is accurate or category error)*

**Tang Chinese**
- Hùndùn *(Primordial Chaos — canonical; the thing that died when given apertures)*
- Wú Xíng Zhī Lì *(The Force Without Form)*

**Indian Subcontinent**
- Nirākāra-Śakti *(The Formless Force)*
- Pralaya-Mūla *(The Root of Dissolution — what is at the bottom of the end)*

**Orcish**
- The Unbargained Force *(cannot be negotiated with; the most frightening thing in the Orcish cosmological vocabulary)*
- Where the Chain Ends *(not a name — a location description that became a name)*

**Elvish**
- The Before-Silence *(what existed before even Elves; said rarely and quietly)*
- Ungol-Mīth *(Web-Smoke — something that spreads without substance)*

**Dwarven**
- The Void Pressure *(pressure from nothing — structurally impossible, which is exactly the problem)*
- Spall *(stone that fractures inward for no load-bearing reason — one word, said as a warning)*

**Wizard-Tradition**
- Al-Fawḍā al-Azalī *(The Eternal Chaos)*
- Al-Lā-Ṣūra *(The Formless — a philosophical term that became a proper noun in certain texts)*

---

### 7. The Memory-Keeper
*Epithets weighted toward grief, patience, fidelity.*

**Central Asian**
- Eske Sanaa *(The Old Memory — not ancient, exactly; *worn* with keeping)*
- Unut-Magan *(Cannot-Forget — structurally incapable of forgetting, which is its burden)*

**Persian/Zoroastrian**
- Yād-Negahbān *(Memory-Guardian)*
- Dāvar-e Kohneh *(The Old Witness — has seen everything and remembers all of it)*

**Tang Chinese**
- Jì Yì Zhī Shén *(The God of Memory)*
- Qiān Nián Dēng *(The Thousand-Year Lamp — the light kept burning until the people it was lit for came home)*

**Indian Subcontinent**
- Smṛti-Deva *(Memory-God — smṛti is specifically *remembered* tradition, passed down)*
- Sākṣī-Purāṇa *(The Ancient Witness)*

**Orcish**
- The Keeper Before the Keepers *(the Remembrancer tradition's source, whether they know it or not)*
- Varath-Olan *(The Debt of Return — what is owed to memory when you finally come back)*

**Elvish**
- The One Who Waited *(said with the specific Elvish register reserved for things that deserve reverence they won't get)*
- Sîr-Athan *(River-That-Holds — water that keeps what flows into it)*

**Dwarven**
- The Archive That Precedes the Archive *(the record before Dwarves started keeping records — and they know something is there)*
- The First Stratum *(the geological layer below all the others; everything above rests on it)*

**Wizard-Tradition**
- Ḥāfiẓ al-Azal *(The Eternal Preserver — Ḥāfiẓ also means one who has memorized the Quran in full; the resonance is intentional)*
- Shāhid al-Awwal *(The First Witness)*
