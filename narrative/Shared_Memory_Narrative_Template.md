# Shared Memory Narrative Imagery Template

## Purpose

This template provides a consistent structure for writing the **specific narrative scenes** that appear in shared memory flashbacks. Use this when:
- Drafting the 5 core shared memory events
- Creating additional events needed later in campaign development
- Ensuring sensory richness and emotional specificity across all memories

---

## Template Structure

### Event Header
```
## EVENT NAME

**Thematic Core:** [One sentence capturing the essential question/choice]

**Canonical Truths Revealed:**
- [Truth 1 - what they learn about their legendary selves]
- [Truth 2 - what emerges about undone work]
- [Truth 3 - what about aspirations/repercussions/failure becomes visible]
```

### Abstract Trigger
```
**When This Emerges:**
[Description of the narrative/choice condition that makes this memory surface]

Example: "When a character chooses restraint despite their archetype's natural impulse to act..."
```

### Narrative Imagery (The Scene)

**SENSORY ANCHOR (Ground them in the physical moment):**
- What do they see?
- What textures, temperatures, sounds surround them?
- What's the atmospheric weight?
- Length: 2-3 sentences maximum

**THE SETTING (Where are they? When?):**
- Location in Hero Heaven or the legendary past
- Historical moment (before/after what event?)
- Who else is present?
- What's at stake in this moment?
- Length: 3-4 sentences

**THE CHOICE (What decision did they face?):**
- What was the pressure/impulse?
- What was the restraint/alternative?
- Why was this choice difficult?
- What archetype is being tested?
- Length: 3-4 sentences

**THE RESONANCE (How does it mirror the present moment?):**
- What parallel do they see between then and now?
- Why does this feel relevant to what they just experienced?
- What doubt/question does it raise?
- Length: 2-3 sentences

**THE CONSEQUENCE/COST (What happened as a result?):**
- Did their choice have immediate consequences?
- Did the choice feel right or wrong?
- What lingered after?
- Does the character understand the full weight of what they chose?
- Length: 2-3 sentences

### Pairing Variations

```
**Variant: [ARCHETYPE 1] + [ARCHETYPE 2]**

**What This Pair Discovers:**
- [Emotional emphasis unique to this pairing]
- [Different aspect of the scene they notice/understand]
- [Personal resonance this has for each character type]

**Why This Pairing Works:**
- How does Archetype 1's nature connect to this scene?
- How does Archetype 2's nature connect to this scene?
- What do they learn about each other from this shared memory?

**GM Note:** [Any pairing-specific delivery notes or emphasis]
```

## Writing Guidelines

### Narrative Voice
- Write from the character's perspective (what they see/feel/remember)
- Use second-person ("You see...") during delivery, but draft in sensory detail
- Ground in physical sensation; avoid abstraction
- Emotional truth before explanation

### Sensory Specificity
- Don't describe everything—pick 2-3 vivid details that carry weight
- Favor concrete details over abstract concepts
- Let the setting do symbolic work (water = flow/change, stone = permanence, etc.)
- Avoid over-explaining symbolism; let players find meaning

### Dialogue (Optional)
- Can include brief snatches of what was said
- Never explain; let dialogue speak for itself
- Other characters' voices can be present but should feel distant or dreamlike
- Keep dialogue under 10 words per snippet

### Length
- Full event imagery: 400-600 words
- Should take 60-90 seconds to read aloud
- Pacing: sensory anchor (quick) → setting (medium) → choice (slow) → consequence (quick)

### What NOT to Do
- ✗ Don't explain what happened. Show it.
- ✗ Don't name the Wizard directly in the imagery.
- ✗ Don't resolve the ambiguity. Leave it open.
- ✗ Don't use the word "remember" (players experience it, don't narrate remembering)
- ✗ Don't make the character's interpretation explicit. Let them discover it.

---

## Checklist for Completed Event Imagery

- [ ] Sensory anchor grounds reader immediately
- [ ] Setting is clear but not over-described
- [ ] The choice is visceral, not abstract
- [ ] Consequence shows impact, doesn't explain meaning
- [ ] At least 2 pairing variations drafted
- [ ] No direct naming of Wizard or explicit explanations
- [ ] Ambiguity about rightness of choice is preserved
- [ ] Imagery connects to canonical truth(s) but doesn't state them
- [ ] Total length 400-600 words
- [ ] Reads naturally aloud in 60-90 seconds
