## Version 0.1 - Opening Segment Base
**Date:** 2025-01-21  
**Status:** Draft - Segment 1 complete  
**Conversation:** Initial Breaker development session

### Content Created
- **Segment 1: Waking in the Dark** - Complete opening sequence establishing:
  - Physical confinement in collapsed inn/tavern post-earthquake
  - Tool discovery (hammer - crude, heavy)
  - Five trapped individuals (including protagonist)
  - Core crisis: escape requires precision vs. power
  - Closing memory hook: *"Yes."* (suggesting past similar choice)

### Key Decisions

**Setting:**
- Collapsed inn/tavern after earthquake ("shifting earth")
- Multiple rubble pockets where survivors are trapped
- Protagonist could break free but would risk total collapse
- Mix of conscious/unconscious civilians (4-5 total)
- At least one child present for ethical weight

**Tool Choice:**
- Hammer selected as archetypal "breaking" implement
- Described as crude/heavy (post-driver or stone-breaker, not carpenter's tool)
- Note: May need variants for other classes (wand for mage, lockpick for thief)
- Tool presented as both salvation and threat

**Structural Parallel to Warrior:**
- Both wake in vulnerable situations
- Both have tools that want to be used
- Both face moment where using tool seems obvious
- Both must choose restraint despite capability
- Key difference: Warrior = "don't threaten others"; Breaker = "don't destroy escape route"

### Prose Techniques Applied

**Erikson-Style Elements:**
- Opening with sensory grounding: "Darkness, and the weight of stone"
- Fragmented memory emergence
- Visceral physical detail (dust/copper taste, breathing resistance)
- Ambiguity about protagonist identity
- Tool personification (hammer "patient and waiting")
- Atmospheric pressure over mechanical urgency

**Pacing Density:**
- Dense, compressed prose matching confined space
- Short paragraphs for disorientation phase
- Longer exploration once protagonist gains agency
- Tempo slows during civilian discovery (building dread)

**Sensory Specificity:**
- Sound: dripping water, breathing, child's whimper, debris skitter
- Touch: splintered wood, metal heft, pressure of rubble
- Taste: dust, old wine, copper
- Sight: gradations of darkness, shadows within shadows
- Proprioception: pinned arm, confined movement

### GM Guidance Notes

**Player Agency Approach:**
- Segment 1 is pure setup - no mechanical choices required yet
- Encourage exploration and questions
- Let atmospheric pressure build organically
- Don't rush to branching paths

**Ethical Stakes:**
- Child present but not manipulatively overwrought
- Man's trapped leg = ticking clock (pain, potential shock)
- Unconscious woman = urgency without dialogue
- Older person's shallow breathing = fragility
- Total collapse risk affects rescuers too (external consequence)

**Tool as Test:**
- Hammer weight increases throughout segment (metaphorically)
- From "possibility" → "consequence" → "accusation"
- Final line positions it as answer to unremembered question

### What's Next

**Segment 2 Requirements:**
- Branch architecture for player approach
- Path A: Precision/restraint (seeking careful solution)
- Path B: Power/risk (considering forced escape)
- Both paths must lead to alignment memory integration
- Need to develop specific structural challenge that rewards one approach

**Alignment Memory Needs:**
- Must echo "choosing NOT to break when breaking feels natural"
- Or: "choosing surgical precision over overwhelming force"
- Should crystallize as player makes critical choice
- Memory fragment format: brief, sensory, emotionally resonant

**Open Questions:**
1. Does protagonist remember being a hero at all during Segment 1?
2. How much structural engineering knowledge do they have?
3. What triggers the alignment memory - action or realization?
4. Should rescue voices be heard from outside, or is silence better?

## Version 0.2 - Crisis Architecture Revision
**Date:** 2025-01-21  
**Status:** Draft - Segment 1 revised  
**Conversation:** Continued Breaker development, architectural corrections

### Changes Made

**Crisis Restructure (Major):**
- **OLD:** Protagonist could break free with hammer but would risk collapse
- **NEW:** Hammer is wedged as structural support; escape requires abandoning it
- **Why:** Creates better archetype test about identity vs. survival
- Three-layer design:
  1. Hammer acts as load-bearing support (removing it = instability)
  2. Gap is only escape route (body fits, body + hammer doesn't)
  3. Leaving hammer allows escape; taking it risks everyone

**Prose Refinements:**
- Reduced "you <verb>" repetition through varied sentence structures
- Opening: "swaddled like a newborn" vs. "wake to pressure" (more visceral)
- Removed premature memory exposition (inn details)
- Changed to sensory inference: smells/sounds suggest public house
- Eliminated "copper taste" (overused in previous scenarios)
- Changed "blood" to "something wet and dark" (less explicit, more atmospheric)

**Discovery Sequence:**
- Hammer familiarity is instinctive, not remembered
- Location inference through smell: wine, food, bodies = inn
- No narrator exposition about what crushed them
- Protagonist learns through touch, not being told

**Improved Archetype Alignment:**
- Question shifts from "Can I use power carefully?" to "Am I anything without this power?"
- Breaker doubt becomes: "Do I need destructive capability to be myself?"
- Leaving hammer = accepting powerlessness to save others
- Taking/keeping hammer = prioritizing identity over lives

### Prose Technique Notes

**Sentence Variety Examples:**
- "Fingers flex" vs. "Your fingers flex"
- "Water drips somewhere" vs. "Somewhere water drips"
- "The air suggests answers" vs. "You realize from the air"
- "Moving through..." vs. "As you move..."

**Sensory Specificity Without Exposition:**
- "Sour-sweet reek of spilled wine" (not "you smell wine")
- "Ghost of roasted meat" (not "you remember the smell")
- "Particular musk of many bodies" (not "travelers were here")

**Atmospheric Details Enhanced:**
- "Canvas weighted with snow" (sagging ceiling metaphor)
- "Ghost-light" (barely-visible light through cracks)
- "Each inhalation a question mark" (older person's failing breath)
- "Geometry is merciless" (mathematical/structural framing)

### GM Guidance Updates

**Crisis Communication:**
- Don't explain the choice immediately
- Let player discover escape route through exploration
- Physical testing reveals hammer won't budge
- Trying the gap reveals it's too narrow with hammer
- Realization should dawn: "I can leave, but not with this"

**Emotional Stakes:**
- Others can potentially be rescued from outside if structure stays stable
- Protagonist's choice: save self now (without tool) or wait with others (keeping tool)
- Identity question: "Who am I without this?"
- Not selfishness vs. heroism—it's identity vs. pragmatism

### Open Questions (Still)
1. What memory surfaces when choice is made?
2. If hammer is abandoned, can it be recovered later?
3. What happens if player refuses both options?
4. Should external rescue sounds be heard, or maintain uncertainty?

### Token Budget
- **Used:** ~80,000 / 190,000 (42%)
- **Remaining:** ~110,000 (58%)
- Still sufficient for 2 more segments

6. **Secret Snippet (2-3 min):** Memory crystallization → TO BE WRITTEN
7. **The Rescue (3-5 min):** External help, extraction → TO BE WRITTEN
8. **Convergence (2-3 min):** Caravan joining → TO BE WRITTEN

**Total Duration:** 40-45 minutes (matching Warrior)

### Segment 1 Refinements (Continued from v0.2)

Additional line edits applied:

**Opening improvements:**
- "Awareness" vs "Waking" (more abstract)
- "urgent, raw, and with little understanding" (visceral expansion)
- "Not yet" as standalone fragment (rhythm break)
- "no angle neglected" vs "from every angle" (more absolute)

**Air/taste description:**
- Expanded with hesitation: "what seems like unwashed warmth"
- "Perhaps the echoes of long habitation" (poetic uncertainty)

**Breathing personification:**
- "hasn't decided whether to finish its crushing task"
- "Predatory, pressing down to pin while it relishes the moment"
- Gives weight agency and menace

**Water sound:**
- "suffuses rather than echoes" (better spatial description)
- "like the darkness weeping" (simpler, stronger)

**Wood discovery:**
- "Your hand flexes to greet wood in return"
- "handshake and a warning" (active personification)
- "creak like an admonition" vs just "crack"

**Confinement exploration:**
- "Tentative, probing movements" (shows character state)
- "limb or makeshift pillar" (uncertainty about what's pinning)
- "permits no extraction without risking" (clearer stakes)

**Hammer discovery:**
- Two-part discovery: tactile qualities first, then tracing upward
- "its partner" (pairing metal/wood)
- "heft, craft, shape all speak" (sensory trilogy)
- "fractionally more liberty" vs "freedom"

### Editorial Principles Established

**Sentence variety to reduce "you" overuse:**
- Lead with sensory details: "Water drips" vs "You hear water"
- Use implied subjects: "Fingers flex" vs "Your fingers flex"  
- Active objects: "Wood meets them" vs "They find wood"
- Atmospheric subjects: "The air suggests" vs "You realize from the air"

**Discovery through sensation, not exposition:**
- No narrator telling what crushed them
- Inference from smells/sounds (wine, bodies = public house)
- Physical exploration reveals geometry
- Character learns alongside player

**New structure:**
- Campaign Context section (mirrors Warrior)
- Setup Notes for GM (dual-truth framework)
- 8 segment placeholders with key beats outlined
- "Remaining Content" section holding old prose for mining
- Clear GM guidance per segment
- Player agency prompts

**Remaining work:**

- Write new prose for segments 6-8  
- Apply editorial rigor throughout
- Create Secret Snippet text
- Develop branching consequences

### Next Version Plan

**v0.3 criteria:**

### Segment 5 Design Choices

**Core Architecture:**
- Opens with gap description: physical possibility of escape
- Reiterates hammer's load-bearing function (structural constraint)
- Introduces dialogue with trapped man ("How long?" / "You could get out. Get help.")
- Surfaces the identity question: *And what are you without this?*
- Names the archetype: "Breaker" - surfaces from beneath conscious thought
- Frames hammer as proof/evidence/identity, not just tool
- Presents the binary: being yourself (with hammer, trapped) vs. being free (without hammer, powerless)
- Updates on deteriorating conditions (child quiet, older person's breathing worse, man in pain)

**Prose Techniques:**
- Question-answer rhythm builds tension: "Could you? The hammer's weight in your hands asks..."
- Identity unpacking: "Not a name. A *definition*." (philosophical weight)
- Physical description of wear: "worn-smooth wood speaks of use, of purpose"
- Stark choice framing: "what matters more: being yourself, or being free"
- Atmospheric pressure through others' suffering (non-manipulative)

**GM Guidance Added:**
- Explicit instruction: "This is the archetype test. Let it breathe."
- FAQ section for common player questions with honest answers
- Structural reality reinforced: geometry is merciless, no easy outs
- Both paths validated as character moments
- Permission for player creativity, with realistic constraints
- Time boundary: don't drag past 12 minutes
- Clear branching paths: Segment 6a (leave hammer) vs 6b (keep hammer)
- Connection to "Yes" moment from Segment 4

**Player Agency Framework:**
- Three explicit options: Leave / Keep / Creative solution
- GM instructed not to proceed until choice is made
- Space for genuine deliberation
- No guilt-tripping despite others' suffering

### Structural Notes

**Why This Works:**
- Builds naturally from Segments 1-4 (discovery → understanding → connection → hint)
- The "Yes" from Segment 4 creates haunting echo: "You've done this before"
- Identity vs. survival mirrors Breaker archetype doubt
- Hammer as load-bearing = perfect metaphor for "I hold things together by destroying them"
- Gap as escape = freedom requires powerlessness
- Both choices are meaningful, neither is "wrong"
