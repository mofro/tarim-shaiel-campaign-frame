# Party Composition & Player Accommodation

⚠️ **GM PLANNING DOCUMENT — Contains spoiler-level design thinking.**

This document captures analysis of which archetypes are narratively essential, minimum viable party size, and strategies for accommodating remote or intermittent players. Derived from February 2026 planning sessions.

---

## ARCHETYPE NARRATIVE FUNCTIONS

### Tier 1 — Essential (Story Cannot Function Without)

**WARRIOR**
- **Narrative function:** Physical anchor, protection instinct, force vs. understanding axis
- **Campaign role:** The one whose instinct is "I will stand between danger and the people behind me"
- **Doubt drives:** Can strength solve this, or is it the blindness that let the Wizard escape?
- **Why essential:** Every party needs a physical presence. The Warrior's restraint-vs-dominance theme mirrors the campaign's macro-question. Their doubt is immediately legible and anchors the party in physical stakes.
- **Player status:** ✅ Committed player

**SEEKER**
- **Narrative function:** Investigative spine, truth-uncovering, knowledge vs. deception
- **Campaign role:** The one who will uncover the Wizard's survival, question the tool's revelations, drive discovery
- **Doubt drives:** "Who is answering when I look?" — can the tool of clarity itself be a deception?
- **Why essential:** The campaign is fundamentally about incomplete knowledge. The heroes failed because they didn't SEE the Wizard's survival. Without a Seeker, the party has no one whose METHOD is discovery. The Wizard's patient deception specifically targets the Seeker's vulnerability.

**BREAKER**
- **Narrative function:** Moral fulcrum, consequence-bearer, liberation vs. destruction
- **Campaign role:** The one who broke the Empire's chains — and might have broken the Wizard's containment
- **Doubt drives:** Was liberation itself the problem? Did freedom cause the seventy years of chaos?
- **Why essential:** This is the archetype that forces the party (and the table) to grapple with the unintended consequences theme. Without the Breaker, the campaign loses its most uncomfortable question: did our heroism cause this?

### Tier 2 — Important (Story Significantly Enriched By)

**VISIONARY**
- **Narrative function:** Foresight, direction, temporal dimension (seeing futures, not just uncovering pasts)
- **Campaign role:** Guidance and path-finding; the one who sees where to go
- **Doubt drives:** "Was my sight compromised from the start?" — paranoia about whether the party's direction is genuine or Wizard-manipulated
- **Why important:** Adds the "are we being guided toward the Wizard's plan?" tension. Overlaps somewhat with Seeker's investigative function but adds the temporal/future axis.
- **Flexibility:** Could be absent from core party; their function (seeing ahead) can be partially distributed through environmental clues and NPC guidance. Best candidate for a recurring/intermittent player.

**BRIDGE (Diplomat)**
- **Narrative function:** Political complexity, alliance-building, unity vs. subtle control
- **Campaign role:** The one who negotiates, builds coalitions, works within systems
- **Doubt drives:** "Is my gift for unity genuine wisdom, or unconscious control?" — did diplomatic networks become the Wizard's networks?
- **Why important:** In a Silk Road campaign about trade, cultural intersection, and trust across difference, this archetype is deeply thematic. But their FUNCTION (social negotiation) can be partially carried by any charismatic character.
- **Flexibility:** Strong candidate for Model B (stationary anchor point) or Model C (late introduction).

### Tier 3 — Enriching (Story Deepened But Not Dependent On)

**SACRIFICER**
- **Narrative function:** Cost, willingness to pay it, receiving vs. giving
- **Campaign role:** The emotional texture; the one who asks what this is all worth
- **Doubt drives:** "Was the defining sacrifice orchestrated?" — the most personal doubt
- **Why enriching:** Enormously deepens the party's emotional register. But the doubt doesn't drive the external plot the way Seeker's discoveries or Breaker's consequences do.
- **Flexibility:** Most flexible archetype. Strong candidate for the "already on caravan" structural innovation. Could be an NPC if no player fills it.

## MINIMUM VIABLE PARTY

**Three players:** Warrior + Seeker + Breaker

This covers:
- **Protection** (Warrior) — the physical anchor
- **Discovery** (Seeker) — the investigative engine
- **Consequence** (Breaker) — the moral weight

Together: force, knowledge, and destruction. That's a complete narrative engine.

**Four is better.** Add Visionary (paranoia about direction, future-sight) or Bridge (political complexity, alliance dynamics) depending on what the table wants.

**Five or six is the full experience** — every archetype's doubt creates a different facet of the central crisis, and the interplay between them is where the deepest moments live.

## ACCOMMODATING REMOTE/INTERMITTENT PLAYERS

### Model A — The Recurring Wanderer

The character is on the same road but not always with the caravan. They appear, travel together, then diverge. When the player is available, the character is present. When not, they're "elsewhere on the road."

**Best for:** Visionary (following a path they must walk alone), Seeker (chasing a thread of knowledge off the main route)

**Strengths:** Narratively organic. Arrivals and departures can be story moments.
**Weaknesses:** Requires GM improvisation to explain absences. Character misses party bonding.

### Model B — The Anchor Point

The character is stationary — a contact the party returns to. Based in a city, caravanserai, or sacred site.

**Best for:** Bridge (embedded in political/trade networks), Sacrificer (tending a community)

**Strengths:** The character builds deep relationships with a specific place. Party has a "home base" contact.
**Weaknesses:** Player only participates when party is at that location. Requires routing the campaign through the anchor.

### Model C — The Convergence Player

They weren't at Session 0. They join at a specific narrative moment. Their awakening happens IN PLAY — the party encounters someone clearly going through what they went through.

**Best for:** Any archetype. Especially powerful for Visionary or Bridge.

**Strengths:** Their first appearance becomes a major story moment. Dramatic irony — the party recognizes what's happening before the new character does. Turns the "we all woke up alone" reveal into a recurring campaign beat.
**Weaknesses:** Player misses Session 0 shared experience. Must be handled so the new player feels welcomed, not like a latecomer.

### Key Cosmological Principle

**Every hero was expelled, but not necessarily at the same moment.** The expulsion is a process, not a single event. Some heroes wake earlier, some later. Some might have been walking the world for weeks before the party finds them. This gives complete flexibility on timing without breaking the cosmological rules.

### Recommendations

- Build Session 0 for the committed players (2-3 awakenings)
- Design the remote player as Model A (Wanderer) or Model C (Convergence)
- Keep all six archetypes available as design space — don't close doors
- The graduated timing model makes adding players straightforward (15-20 min awakenings are repeatable)
- The "already on caravan" model (Sacrificer) could accommodate additional players beyond six if needed
