# Session 0 Secret Mythical-Tier Snippets

## Purpose

Each archetype receives a **personalized, secret Mythical-tier memory fragment** during their awakening scenario at the moment of highest emotional resonance. These snippets ground player investment in their character's past while preserving mystery about what actually happened.

**Delivery:** Written snippet pushed across table to player silently, or whispered during the relevant moment.

---

## Design Guidelines

### The Register: Emotional & Sensory (NOT Explanatory)

✓ **DO:** Sensory details grounded in the present crisis
✓ **DO:** Emotional weight specific to the archetype's core doubt
✓ **DO:** Implicit connections ("I felt this before")
✓ **DO:** Physical sensations that link past to present

✗ **DON'T:** Explain what happened
✗ **DON'T:** State "you were a god"
✗ **DON'T:** Reveal the actual cause of expulsion
✗ **DON'T:** Name the Wizard or the charge directly

### The Structure

```
[SENSORY ANCHOR - Ground in present crisis]

[EMOTIONAL RESONANCE - What this felt like when you had power]

[CONNECTION - The parallel between then and now]

[PHYSICAL SENSATION - Link past to present]
```

### Archetype-Specific Focal Points

**Breaker:** Focus on the weight of restraint. What does it cost to hold back?

**Bridge:** Focus on the complexity of connection. How do you hold others together?

**Seeker:** Focus on the limits of knowledge. What can't you see, even with cosmic sight?

**Sacrificer:** Focus on the burden of gift. What does it mean to be given to?

**Warrior:** Focus on the isolation of strength. What do you protect, and at what cost?

**Visionary:** Focus on the paralysis of sight. What good is foresight if you can't act?

## Snippet Templates (Archetype-Specific)

### BREAKER: Moment of Restraint

**Trigger Condition:**
- When Breaker faces overwhelming temptation to use force/destruction to solve problem
- But doing so would cause collateral harm
- **Snippet arrives in moment of choosing restraint**

**Template:**

> [SENSORY ANCHOR]
> Cold stone beneath you / dark space around you / weight pressing from above. The instinct is overwhelming—simply *break* your way free. One gesture. One exertion of will.
>
> [EMOTIONAL RESONANCE]
> You've felt this before. The crushing weight of your decisions. Faced with the charge and power to deliver devastating change with a single stroke. To END one thing that deserves an end, and begin another in its place. You COULD. Every fiber knows it.
>
> [CONNECTION]
> But you paused then too. What is the price of wielding such power to destroy? What lives will be touched and changed? 
>
> [PHYSICAL SENSATION]
> You feel the power coursing through your limbs, only to be met with the clammy sweat of momentary uncertainty. Of concern. The world around you trembles, as if in concert to your motions. Or is it the silt and stones of your current entombment, filtering down around you, whispering of consequence.

---

### BRIDGE: Moment of Connection

**Trigger Condition:**
- When Bridge must negotiate/connect with someone who seems fundamentally opposed to agreement
- In the moment where words actually work—where impossible compromise happens
- **Snippet arrives as Bridge recognizes the power of connection itself**

**Template:**

> [SENSORY ANCHOR]
> [Prison bars, or chains, or the distance across which words must travel. The weight of what separates you from freedom. The voice of your captor/opponent across the divide.]
>
> [EMOTIONAL RESONANCE]
> You remember the weight of bringing together what refused to be joined. People who hated each other. Ideologies that seemed irreconcilable. Worlds that shouldn't touch. And yet, somehow, you found the thread. The resonance. The point where opposing things recognized they needed each other.
>
> [CONNECTION]
> That memory sits in your chest now. In this moment, as your words bridge the gap between you and [NPC], you feel that same resonance. Not manipulation. Not trick. Just... understanding. Recognition. *Connection*.
>
> [PHYSICAL SENSATION]
> It feels warm. Dangerous. Like standing at the center of something much larger than yourself. Your voice doesn't shake. Your hands don't tremble. You speak with the certainty of someone who has already done impossible things.

---

### SEEKER: Moment of Blindness

**Trigger Condition:**
- When Seeker faces situation where cosmic knowledge/perception means nothing
- In moment of accepting they must navigate *without* their greatest strength
- **Snippet arrives as Seeker confronts the limits of all-seeing sight**

**Template:**

> [SENSORY ANCHOR]
> Desert expanse stretching in all directions. Wind carrying sand into eyes, mouth, ears. The horizon so vast it becomes meaningless. Or crowded darkness with no direction. Or mist that obscures everything.
>
> [EMOTIONAL RESONANCE]
> You remember the horror of realizing that even cosmic sight has limits. That there are things—important things—you simply cannot see. You stood at the edge of understanding and looked outward, only to find a darkness deeper than any light you could summon. And you had to *choose* to step into it anyway.
>
> [CONNECTION]
> That moment arrives again, now. In this moment of being truly blind—stripped of perception, forced to navigate by instinct alone—you remember what it felt like to know that your knowing had limits. That the most important answers might come from *not* seeing.
>
> [PHYSICAL SENSATION]
> There's a strange peace in it, beneath the fear. A settling. Like putting down a weight you've carried so long you forgot it was heavy. You can't see. That's... okay. You don't need to see everything. You never did.

---

### SACRIFICER: Moment of Receiving

**Trigger Condition:**
- When Sacrificer must accept help/sacrifice from someone else instead of providing it
- In moment of recognizing they are *being cared for* rather than *caring*
- **Snippet arrives as Sacrificer experiences the other side of giving**

**Template:**

> [SENSORY ANCHOR]
> Soft bed. Warm hands. Someone else's resources being spent on you. The weight of being cared for when you're accustomed to being the carer. The strangeness of being held, tended, given to.
>
> [EMOTIONAL RESONANCE]
> You remember the moment you realized that sacrifice isn't only giving. It's also *receiving*. That the greatest strength sometimes means accepting what others give you. That being tended by others is not weakness—it's completion. You stood in a moment where the world gave to you instead of demanding you give to the world.
>
> [CONNECTION]
> This moment echoes that. Lying here, accepted into this family's home despite your strangeness. Being given to. Being cared for. Their sacrifice for you mirrors something you'd forgotten: that you too were once cared for in return.
>
> [PHYSICAL SENSATION]
> There's gratitude in it, but also fear. What if you disappoint them? What if you can't repay what they're giving? And underneath that—a recognition. A memory. You've felt held like this before. Trusted like this. And somehow, you survived it. You even thrived.

---

### WARRIOR: Moment of Protection

**Trigger Condition:**
- When Warrior must protect others while being perceived as threat
- In moment where strength must be expressed as *restraint* rather than *power*
- **Snippet arrives as Warrior recognizes the paradox of protective force**

**Template:**

> [SENSORY ANCHOR]
> People flinching from you. The weight of your blade. The fear in their eyes when they see what you carry. The terrible power in your hands, made terrible only by their vulnerability.
>
> [EMOTIONAL RESONANCE]
> You remember the exhaustion of being strong in a world of fragile things. The strange loneliness of being capable of ending, when all you want to do is *protect*. You stood among people who needed you, and your very presence was a threat to them. You had to choose to be less than you were to make them feel safe.
>
> [CONNECTION]
> That choice arrives again, now. In this camp full of refugees who've seen too much violence. Who flinch from strength because strength has harmed them. And you understand: your greatest power isn't in the blade. It's in *choosing* not to use it. In standing guard while remaining somehow... gentle.
>
> [PHYSICAL SENSATION]
> Your shoulders ease. Your grip softens. There's a kind of peace in accepted limitation—in choosing restraint not from fear or weakness, but from love. You remember this. You knew how to do this. You're still the same person who chose to protect the fragile.

---

### VISIONARY: Moment of Immediacy

**Trigger Condition:**
- When Visionary faces crisis requiring *instant* action with no time for foresight
- In moment of choosing action *without* seeing the path forward
- **Snippet arrives as Visionary is forced to act blind**

**Template:**

> [SENSORY ANCHOR]
> Chaos. Danger descending. No time. Avalanche/fire/bandits approaching NOW. Your oracle device showing flashes of futures that might be, but no time to interpret, no time to understand.
>
> [EMOTIONAL RESONANCE]
> You remember the terror of foresight failing you. Of standing at a moment where every possible future diverged and you couldn't see which was right. And having to *choose anyway*. Having to act on intuition instead of knowledge. Trusting something deeper than sight.
>
> [CONNECTION]
> That moment is now. You *could* try to see. You *could* look for the safest path. But there is no time. The people around you are in danger. This caravan, these people—they need you to act *now*, not to see all possibilities.
>
> [PHYSICAL SENSATION]
> It's strangely liberating. No weight of choice-between-futures. No paralysis of infinite possibility. Just this: danger, people, action. Your hands move without consultation. Your body remembers what your mind had forgotten—that sometimes the most powerful sight isn't foresight. It's just presence. It's just showing up.

## Delivery Instructions

### Before Session 0

1. **Write out all six snippets** in full before the session (customize to your party's specific characters if desired)
2. **Print or prepare digitally** for quiet delivery
3. **Know when each trigger occurs** in your planning notes
4. **Have them accessible** during play without needing to search

### During Session 0

**When the Moment Arrives:**
1. Let the roleplay/challenge proceed naturally
2. Recognize the moment when the archetype faces their core doubt during restraint/choice
3. **Without breaking the scene,** slide the written snippet across the table, OR whisper it to the player
4. **Let them read silently** while others play—don't interrupt overall flow
5. **Continue the scenario** — the snippet ends, play resumes

### After Session 0

**In Early Downtime:**
- Players may choose to share what they felt/saw, or keep it private
- Don't push them to discuss—let it emerge organically
- If they share, listen. Don't over-explain or confirm/deny their interpretation.
- Their interpretation of what they felt is *their truth*, even if different from the broader narrative

## Customization Notes

### Personalizing to Your Party

These templates assume certain archetype expressions. If your players interpret their archetypes differently:
- Adjust the sensory anchors to match their specific scenario
- Keep the emotional core the same
- Let the physical sensation remain grounded in *their* moment

### Optional: Player-Specific Customization

If you know details about a player's character before Session 0:
- You could include their character's actual name or a specific detail
- Example: [Breaker] might reference a specific wall they're escaping from
- But this adds prep work—templates are fine as-is
