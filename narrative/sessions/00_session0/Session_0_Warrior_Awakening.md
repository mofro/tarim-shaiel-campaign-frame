# Session 0: Warrior Archetype Awakening

## Overview

**Archetype:** Warrior  
**Thematic Challenge:** Protection without power; strength without threat  
**Core Test:** Can a warrior be present and capable without dominating through force?  
**Tool Discovery:** Iron blade with wrapped bone grip (crude form, bound to wrist)  
**Setting:** Refugee camp of ~30 displaced people, hiding in a ravine  
**Duration:** 40-45 minutes total play time (refined prose adds atmospheric density)  
**Tone:** Sensory disorientation → slow context-building → trust challenge → restrained power moment

---

## Campaign Context

This awakening serves the larger Session 0 architecture:
- **Individual crisis** specific to Warrior archetype (protection through restraint, not dominance)
- **Secret snippet moment** integrated into the crisis (restraint as choice, not weakness)
- **Convergence path** to caravan (refugees joining merchant caravan westward)
- **Established trust** creates first meaningful relationship outside immediate party

The Warrior enters the convergence scene as the *only one who has already protected others*. This shapes their entry into the larger group differently than heroes who arrive through accident or chance.

---

## Setup Notes (For GM)

**What the GM knows that the player doesn't:**

- The Warrior's arrival in the camp was instantaneous and inexplicable
- The muck where they woke was empty moments before
- No one in the camp saw them arrive—they simply appeared
- The camp guards have no explanation
- The camp itself is a loose confederation of people displaced by regional conflicts they didn't invite
- This camp has been hiding for weeks, afraid of attracting military attention
- The woman with military bearing is former soldier (discipline, threat assessment)
- The old woman has seen this before (in a way she doesn't articulate yet)
- The soldiers who approach are real, patrolling, but not specifically hunting this camp

**Contested-site context (see [[WIZARD_AND_LICH_CADRE]] §Phase 4 and [[STORY_ARC_SYNTHESIS]]):**
The refugee camp and the battle that created it were not coincidental. The Wizard's agents descended on the town and its soldiers alike — not out of military interest, but to locate the Warrior before they could orient. The remnant soldiers the camp fears are themselves survivors of that dark-force assault, fleeing and dangerous, unaware of what actually destroyed them. The immune system's counter: the camp itself. Thirty frightened people provided cover, noise, and moral complexity that slowed the search. The Warrior woke inside a human shield they didn't choose and couldn't yet understand. The agents may still be in the region.

**Passive hook:** The old woman already knows something — she's written as having "seen this before." If she travels with the caravan or the party returns to her, she can describe what she saw in the sky the night before the Warrior appeared. Separately, the remnant soldiers' account of what destroyed their company doesn't match any known military force — careful interrogation reveals a gap where an explanation should be.

**Dual-truth preservation:**
- Storyteller knows: The Warrior arrived with purpose, called to this moment — and something was already looking for them
- Player knows: They woke up in mud with no memory and a blade they don't remember choosing

## Segment 1: The Waking (3-5 minutes)

### Opening Narration

**[Start with pure sensation. No context. No explanation. Player should feel disorientation physically.]**

![[Cold-comes-first_ElevenLabs_2026-01-16T01_18_30_Julian - Deep, Rich and Mature_pvc_sp100_s95_sb96_f2-5_EDITED.mp3]]
![[Cold Comes First-ElevenLabs_2026-01-16T01_18_30_Julian - Deep, Rich and Mature_pvc_sp100_s95_sb96_f2-5-EDITED_2.mp3]]
![[Cold Comes First - Warrior MUSIC.mp3]]

Cold comes first—not the clean bite of winter air, but something more claustrophobic and cloying. Something that's settled into your bones like sediment, patient and aching. Your left arm is numb where it's been crushed beneath you dully aching from the weight, and when you try to flex fingers, they move like foreign things, more like distant relatives or belonging to a stranger. How long have you been lying like this?

The smells come next, deep and unbidden but a necessary evil of your haggard breathing. They are layered like a burial: unwashed bodies pressed too close. Smoke—cooking fires, low and abashed, nothing eager about them. Lower still, grain gone sour, rotting from the inside out. And underneath *everything*, sharp and copper-bright: fear-sweat. That particular reek of people who understand, deeply, the necessity of caution, and the peerless value of being small. What it means to be hunted.

The wind, because of course, there is a breeze you realize, carries with it sounds in fragments. You can feel it as much as hear it, touching your skin and tickling your ears. A child crying somewhere distant—not the thin wail of hunger, but something quieter. Self-aware enough at whatever young age to somehow know it shouldn't cry too loud. Fire crackling, but subdued, speaking in whispers. Voices urgent and careful. Footsteps on packed earth, deliberately slow. The shuffle of people who have learned to move as though apology itself has weight.

You open your eyes.

Darkness greets you—not absence of light but a thickness to the air itself, like looking through cloth. Shapes emerge slowly: angles that might be shelters, humps that could be sleeping bodies or discarded bundles. And beneath you, pressing against your cheek, the mud.

This isn't the honest mud of a riverbank that dries clean in sun. This is earth that's given up. Degraded past soil into something closer to rot—compacted by years of feet that had nowhere else to stand, stained by blood washed thin until it forgot it was ever red, host to whatever got dropped and never retrieved *because retrieval meant admitting you'd lost something worth keeping*. Your left arm sinks into it past the wrist, skin bonded to cold wetness like an unwelcome embrace from a cold lover, a pact you don't remember making. Something solid rests against the pulse point.

You move to lift your arm, half in recoil and half for balance and the movement costs you. As the muscles pull and the slow sucking sound of muck challenging your release from its intimate embrace you feel the solid thing's weight shift and for a moment—just a moment—you feel it resist. A shy partner, unconvinced of the wooing. But move it does.

---

Iron.

A blade emerges with your arm, trailing streamers of muck. Simple and dark—the kind of crude iron that was never meant to catch light and throw it back. No shine of care, no gleam of pride. Just function hammered into shape by someone who knew what they were doing but didn't waste time making it pretty. The grip is bone wrapped in leather gone the color of old bruises, the kind that yellow at the edges when they're trying to heal. A leather thong cinches it to your wrist, tight enough to announce itself with every pulse but not quite tight enough to numb. The blade itself carries stains that might be rust. Or might be something that *was* rust before it decided to become a question.

You look at it—this... this tool. This rudimentary expression of violence and mis-repair, that you apparently brought to whatever this place is—and the first thought that surfaces isn't relief. Not recognition either, though something about the weight feels *known* in a way that bypasses memory entirely. Just a certainty that arrives simple and terrible and absolute:

--This is my chain--

### GM Notes

- Let sensory details land before moving forward
- Don't rush through the waking sequence
- The disorientation is the point; player should feel genuinely confused
- The tool's presence is *weight*, not comfort
- The mud is specific—degrading, unpleasant—it speaks to what this place is
- Allow pause after "This is my chain" for the player to orient

### Player Agency

The player is now awake and aware of:
- Their own body (weak, stiff, or both)
- The tool (bound to them, heavy)
- The location (shadows, shapes, incomplete awareness)
- The presence of people around them

They can act. Prompt them: "What do you do?"

## Segment 2: Becoming Aware (5-8 minutes)

### The Camp Reveals Itself

**[Context emerges through observation, not explanation. The player discovers the setting by looking.]**

As the player rises to sitting (or standing, depending on their choice):

![[Movement Brings the World In Focus - Warrior.mp3]]

Your movement brings the world into focus piece by piece, each detail arriving like testimony at an inquest. Shapes resolve into shelters—no, not shelters, but a more dolorous architecture. Cloth draped over frames that were never meant to bear weight for this long. The camp nestles into a wound-like depression in the landscape that people have decided to make home. Half-sunk below grade, using the land itself as walls. Whether it's a ravine worn by old water or a drainage cut by someone's labor years ago doesn't matter—what matters is it hides them. From wind, yes. But mostly from predation's gaze. The hunters.

---

Thirty people, maybe? Hard to count when they're all trying to be so unseen. Fires burning so low they're barely confession, just enough heat to justify the smoke. Tents patched past the point where you could call it repair—this is archaeological layering, strata of fabric and desperation. Some people wrapped in blankets so threadbare they're mere suggestions, light filtering through the weave like broken promises.

Near the closest fire, an old woman worries a pot with movements that refuse to be hurried. Each stir deliberate, patient, as though time itself has agreed to leave her be. She doesn't look up. Doesn't need to—her awareness spreads through posture alone, the way she holds her shoulders, how her stirring never quite falls into rhythm. Listening without seeming to.

---

Farther out, a woman sits with her back to one of the cloth walls, but she's not resting. Mid-thirties, perhaps. Or maybe younger and aged past her years by whatever put those scars on her forearms. She watches the camp's edges with the kind of systematic scan that comes from training, not fear. Every few seconds her eyes complete a circuit—perimeter, people, perimeter, people: Professional. A soldier's habits wearing civilian clothes.

---

A child stares in your direction. Five years old, maybe less. Small in the way children are small when they haven't been eating enough, all angles and wide eyes. But those eyes carry something they shouldn't—a wariness that belongs to adults who've learned the world has teeth.

---

The moment the child registers your consciousness—that shift from still to aware—the child bolts. Not toward safety. Not toward anything. Just *away*, legs pumping, bare feet finding purchase in the muck that tried to keep you. Running deeper into camp, closer to the woman with the soldier's eyes...

### The Reaction

As the child reaches the woman:

Voices spike. Not panic. Commands. Urgency without the pretense of calm. The woman rises in one motion. Hand finds spear. She doesn't point it.

Doesn't need to.

Around the fires, bodies shift. One man stands, gathers his family—wife, two children—moves them back. Not running. Adjusting distance. The way you step away from something unpredictable.

The old woman keeps stirring. Doesn't look. But her shoulders have angled toward you. Listening.

### The Pressure

The weight settles. You're the problem. The thing that doesn't belong.

The blade is in your hand. When did you stand? When did your grip tighten? The movement happened without thought. Muscle memory.

It feels right there. Natural. Your body knows this weight.

Instinct screams. Not words. Pressure behind your eyes. The kind that builds when you need to—

To what?

### GM Notes

- This is the setup for the challenge
- The Warrior is the *threat* by default (they are armed, capable, unknown)
- The weapon feels natural (archetypal memory bleeding through)
- The camp's reaction makes clear: drawing it, using it, or even *appearing* too capable will destroy any trust they might build
- The woman with military bearing is the *one who might know more*
- The old woman is watching (will have significance later)
- The child is innocent of the fear others carry (will become important)

### Visual Reference

**Key NPCs in Camp:**
- **Woman with Military Bearing:** ~40 years old, scarred, moves like someone who's fought. Hand on spear. Eyes that assess threats professionally. She's the de facto leader. She's afraid, but not of combat—she's afraid of *responsibility for others*.
- **Old Woman:** ~70+, moves slowly but deliberately. Stirring soup with perfect patience. Has seen violence before. Chooses grace anyway.
- **The Child:** ~5 years old. Afraid. But curious beneath the fear.
- **The Scout (appears later):** Female, fit, bleeding from shoulder wound. Brings news of soldiers.

## Segment 3: The Voices (3-4 minutes)

### The Approach

The woman approaches. Not fast. Not slow. Measured. Each step deliberate, weight tested before commitment. Hand still on the spear. Eyes locked.

"Don't move."

Two words. Not a threat. Not a request. Just information. Here's what happens next if you do.

### The Whispers

From deeper in camp, voices. Fragments. They're not trying to whisper—they're trying to decide.

"...where did they..."
"...just there, no warning..."
"...armed..."

One voice, older, cuts through: "Keep the children back."

### The Crouching

Twenty feet away, the woman drops into a crouch. Combat stance. Ready to spring, ready to dodge. Weight on the balls of her feet.

She waits. Doesn't advance. Doesn't retreat. Watching.

"I'm going to ask you something," she says. Her voice is level. Controlled. "Think before you answer."

The whole camp watches. Even the old woman has stopped stirring.

"What do you remember?"

### GM Notes

- This is the player's first real moment of agency
- How they answer determines everything that follows
- The crouching stance is professional, not hostile—she's assessing, not attacking
- The silence from the camp is heavy; everyone is waiting
- This question is deliberately open-ended

### Branching (Player's Choice)

**If honest about confusion:** Woman relaxes slightly. She's looking for *honesty*, not coherence. Proceed to Segment 4.

**If reaching for weapon:** Everyone backs away. They become the threat they fear. Redirect to vulnerability or reset to honest answer.

**If standing too fast:** Woman moves to intercept (not violently, but blocking movement). Requires de-escalation.

**If staying still and vulnerable:** Woman approaches closer. The old woman watches. Proceed to Segment 4.

## Segment 4: The Trust Architecture (8-12 minutes, Variable)

### GM Framework: What's Being Tested

The Warrior must *protect* this camp—that's their nature. But they cannot do it through force or even just readiness. They must do it by being *non-threatening*. By being the strong one who chooses vulnerability.

**This is the core test for a Warrior archetype:** Can you be a warrior without being a threat?

### The Woman's Assessment

She's evaluating whether this person is:
- Dangerous and unpredictable
- Like them: displaced, confused, trying to survive
- Something else entirely

**She will offer limited information:**
- Who she is (never given, but established through action)
- What the camp is (refugees, hiding)
- Why they fear armed strangers (context emerges)
- Whether this person can be trusted (her judgment call)

**She will not:**
- Explain where the Warrior came from (she doesn't know)
- Provide history or origin story
- Welcome them immediately
- Force them to participate

### How Trust Builds

**Trust comes through:**
- Answering her questions honestly
- Not moving suddenly
- Showing interest in others' safety, not just survival
- Listening more than speaking
- Accepting the old woman's gesture (food)
- Being present without dominating

**Trust breaks through:**
- Reaching for weapon
- Standing aggressively
- Making demands
- Dismissing their fear
- Lying or being evasive
- Moving toward the child

### The Dynamics

**The Woman (Soldier):**
- Professional. Assessing threats.
- Will gradually relax as minutes pass without incident
- Once the old woman brings food, she nods—signal: "Maybe we wait before deciding"
- If Warrior builds trust with child or old woman, she becomes *ally*

**The Old Woman:**
- After first few minutes of watching, will approach
- Old enough not to fear much anymore
- Will bring food (thin soup, probably)
- Won't ask questions
- Will listen if Warrior talks
- Her presence is *calming*—suggests she's *chosen* to trust them

**The Child:**
- Hides behind the woman
- Won't come close at first
- Will watch them
- If no sudden movements, no reaching for weapon, no assertion of dominance... fear will slowly give way to curiosity
- Eventually asks: "Are you like the ones who burned the village?"
- This is the moment where the Warrior's answer—and how they say it—*matters most*

### GM Guidance

This segment should take time. Don't rush. The discomfort is intentional. Let the player sit in the awkwardness of being unwanted despite being capable of protecting the people.

**Encourage play that:**
- Shows vulnerability
- Asks questions about the camp
- Notices the child's fear
- Accepts help from the old woman
- Responds honestly to the woman's assessment

**Don't force:**
- Immediate acceptance
- Emotional breakthroughs
- Premature trust
- Alliance with specific NPCs

Let the player choose how they respond. The Warrior's choices shape their relationships going forward.

## Segment 5: The Child & The Old Woman (3-5 minutes)

### Emergence Through Player Choice

These moments emerge naturally from the player's approach in Segment 4. Don't force them; allow them to develop.

### The Child's Fear

The child watches from behind the woman. Minutes pass. Maybe longer. Time moves differently when you're being evaluated. The child's eyes never quite leave you, tracking movement, cataloging threat.

Eventually—slowly—curiosity starts winning against fear.

The child doesn't approach. Stays behind the woman. But the voice carries across the space between you:

"Are you like the ones who burned the village?"

Not accusation. Just need. The real question underneath: *Are you what happened to us?*

**The Warrior's response to this question sets everything that follows.**

>*How you answer—the words, the tone, whether you make yourself smaller—determines whether the child believes you're safe. And through the child, whether the woman does too.*

### The Old Woman's Trust

She doesn't announce herself. One moment you're sitting with the soldier's eyes on you, next there's a bowl. Thin soup. Barely more than water with grain's ghost in it. But hot.

The old woman settles nearby. Not close. Not far. Doesn't ask permission. Doesn't ask questions. Her movements carry the unhurried quality of someone who's made a decision that doesn't require proof.

If you speak, she listens. If you don't, she doesn't press.

Her trust—because that's what it is—arrives like weather. It simply is.

The soldier watches this. Eyes narrow slightly. Not disapproval. Assessment. The old woman's judgment carries weight.

### The Woman's Shift

Once the old woman brings food, the woman with the spear sits. Still watching. But the readiness has shifted. Less coiled.

She nods. Small. A signal the camp understands: "The old woman trusts. Maybe we wait."

>*If the Warrior has built genuine connection by this point—with the child, with the old woman—the soldier becomes something different. Not threat. Ally.*

## Segment 6: The Crisis (5-7 minutes)

### Timing

This should happen once the Warrior has begun establishing some trust. The crisis should feel like a *test* of that trust, not a betrayal of it.

### The Scout's Arrival

Movement at the camp's edge. A scout runs in. Not panicked. Urgent. Blood soaking through her shoulder—not deep but worrying.

"Soldiers," she says. "Imperial patrol. Moving through. Maybe an hour."

### The Camp's Response

The camp moves. Practiced. No panic.

Tents folding. Fires dying. Possessions gathered. Children pulled close. Valuables secured. The choreography of people who've done this before. Who know what happens if they don't.

### The Ask

The woman with the spear looks at you directly.

"We can't fight them. Eight soldiers. We're thirty—most children, most old. Fight, and everyone dies. Run, maybe they let us go. But we need to be ready. Need to be quiet."

She pauses.

"And we need someone watching the perimeter. In case they come from a direction we're not expecting."

Another pause. Eyes assessing.

"You can do that. Can't you?"

Not a question. Assessment. She's asking if you're capable. But also: will you protect through restraint? Or will you be the violence they fear?

### GM Notes

- The soldiers are *real*. They will come.
- The camp *will* hide and try to move quietly.
- The Warrior is being asked to do what they do best: protect
- But the method is constrained: watch, stay alert, hold back *unless absolutely necessary*
- This is the transition moment from "stranger" to "protector"
- The Warrior's choice here shapes everything in later segments

## Segment 7: The Secret Snippet Moment (2-3 minutes)

### Trigger Condition

The snippet arrives when:
- The Warrior is positioned at the camp's perimeter
- They're on watch, alert and present
- Soldiers approach and begin to come *close*
- The moment of real danger becomes palpable

### What Happens

> As the camp finishes moving and settles into hiding positions, a sound comes from the perimeter the Warrior is watching: footsteps. Voices. Imperial soldiers, getting closer.
>
> Instinct *screams* to draw the blade, to intercept them, to solve this the warrior way.
>
> But they can't. If they move, if they fight, the camp is exposed.
>
> So they stand still. Blade sheathed. Body tense with readiness but *restrained*.
>
> Holding back the power that surges through them.

### This is the Moment

**[The snippet arrives here. Silently. The player reads it while the camp is hidden, while danger passes nearby, while they are alone at the perimeter with the weight of restraint.]**

Deliver the snippet by:
- Quietly sliding it across the table or handing it directly
- Letting them read in silence
- Continuing the scene naturally once they've finished
- Not asking them to share or explain
- Letting them sit with it

## Snippet Text (Warrior Archetype - Restraint & Duty)

![[Stone Beneath Your Feet - Warrior MUSIC.mp3]]

Stone beneath your feet. Worn smooth by years of standing watch, or perhaps just worn by your memory of standing watch—hard to tell which came first. The darkness around you isn't empty. It's full of breathing. Thirty people hiding in the ravine behind you, trusting you to be the thing between them and what hunts.

Not soldiers. Not warriors who chose this. Refugees. The old woman who brought you soup without asking questions. The child with eyes too old, who wanted to know if you're like the ones who burned the village. People who didn't ask for this war, this hunt, this fear. They just want to survive another night.

Your duty is simple. Clear. Keep them safe.

But the blade—

The soldiers are close now. You can hear boots testing ground, voices conferring in low tones. Your hand knows the grip. Your body knows the draw. The blade at your hip doesn't sing—it *howls*. One motion and you could end this. One stroke and the threat dissolves.

Because the blade is *there* and the threat is *there* and your body is screaming that the space between them should be closed. Now. Fast. Final.

Whose voice is this? Whose thirst?

The blade howls but your gut whispers: what about the people behind you? What begins when steel meets steel and thirty terrified souls realize the armed stranger they're hiding with just became the violence they were fleeing?

So you stand. Muscles coiled. Breath controlled. Power surging through you like water behind a dam, and you—you are the dam. You are the choice to hold.

The boots pass. The voices fade. They never knew you were there.

You're still standing. Still holding the flood inside. Still choosing restraint over release.

And somehow—despite every instinct, despite the blade's hunger, despite the screaming need to ACT—

This feels like victory.

## Segment 8: Convergence (3-5 minutes)

### The Morning After

Dawn breaks. The woman with the spear approaches.

The soldiers passed. The camp survived. The Warrior protected through restraint, not force.

### The Conversation

She looks at you differently now. Not with fear. Something else entirely.

"Scouts say there's a caravan. Two hours west. Merchants heading for the coastal cities. We're going with them—safer in numbers than out here alone."

Pause. Weighing something.

"You can come. If you want."

Another pause. Eyes reading you.

"You can fight, can't you? I mean... *really* fight."

This is the moment. She's registering what you are. What you're capable of. But she's not afraid anymore. She's asking if you'll fight *for* them.

### The Caravan Path

The camp joins the merchant caravan heading west. You're integrated—hired as protection, or invited, or simply absorbed into the group through the old woman's quiet insistence.

You're given a place. A role.

More importantly: you're traveling with people who chose to trust you despite knowing what you carry.

### The Larger Convergence

As the caravan settles for evening travel or makes camp:

The other heroes are there. All of them. Same caravan. Together.

Strangers. But something about them feels *familiar* in a way you can't name.

When your eyes meet—across the fire, along the caravan route—something resonates.

Not memory. Not yet.

Just recognition.

## GM Notes: Pacing & Flexibility

### Time Breakdown

- **Segment 1 (Waking):** 3-5 minutes
- **Segment 2 (Awareness):** 5-8 minutes
- **Segment 3 (Voices):** 3-4 minutes
- **Segment 4 (Trust):** 8-12 minutes *(variable—let it breathe)*
- **Segment 5 (Child/Old Woman):** 3-5 minutes
- **Segment 6 (Crisis):** 5-7 minutes
- **Segment 7 (Snippet):** 2-3 minutes
- **Segment 8 (Convergence):** 3-5 minutes

**Total: 40-45 minutes** (refined prose in Segments 1-3 adds ~5-10 minutes)

**Note:** Original estimate was 30-35 minutes with functional prose. Erikson-grade refinement increases atmospheric density and requires more time for sensory details to land. If playtesting shows this drags, Segments 6-8 can be tightened (Option C: speed up crisis/resolution sections while preserving opening atmosphere).

### Pacing Tips

- Don't rush the middle sections
- The player's discomfort is intentional and productive
- The child's fear is real; don't soften it too quickly
- The old woman's quiet trust is powerful because it contrasts with everyone else's fear
- The snippet moment should feel like a *gift* after the tension

### Handling Deviation: If the Player Tries to Fight

- Let them try
- Show immediate consequences: camp scatters, child is hurt in chaos, woman fights back
- Offer the reality: "You can win this fight. But everyone you were trying to protect dies."
- Offer reset: "You hold back. You lower the weapon."
- The snippet still comes—but tinged with near-tragedy and guilt

### Handling Deviation: If the Player Tries to Leave

- Let them leave
- But they'll encounter the caravan shortly after
- The other heroes are there
- Something about them will make staying matter

### Handling Deviation: If the Player Builds Deep Trust Quickly

- Excellent
- Run with it
- The woman becomes an ally throughout early campaign
- The old woman recognizes something (will have significance later)
- The child begs to come on the caravan (creates ongoing connection)

## Connection to Larger Architecture

### Session 0 Framework

This awakening is one of six (Breaker, Bridge, Seeker, Sacrificer, Warrior, Visionary). Each happens in parallel, with the GM managing multiple scenes and pacing.

### The Secret Snippet

This moment (restraint while watching, as danger passes) is the Warrior's personal revelation. It's theirs alone until they choose to share it. It reinforces their archetype's core doubt: *Can I be strong without being dangerous? Can I protect without controlling?*

### The Caravan Convergence

The Warrior arrives at the caravan as the *only one who has already protected others*. This shapes their relationships with the other heroes. They enter the group with established capability and trust with strangers—a different dynamic than the other archetypes.

### The Recognition Climax

Later in Session 0, all six heroes will share a vision at the campfire (Hero Heaven moment). The Warrior's personal relationship with restraint and protection will inform how they interpret that shared vision.
