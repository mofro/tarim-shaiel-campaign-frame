# Tarim-Shaiel Weapons - Silk Road Arsenal

**Status:** Draft v1.0  
**Scope:** Jerusalem → Changzhou weapon types mapped to Daggerheart mechanics  
**Tiers Covered:** Primarily Tier 1, with scaling guidance

---

## DESIGN NOTES

**Differentiation Strategy:**
- Weapons with identical stats are differentiated primarily through **cultural flavor text** and **regional availability**
- Minor tag variations used sparingly to represent unique fighting styles
- Features applied conservatively per Daggerheart design principles

**Enhancement Potential:**
- **Mythic Candidate** = Historically legendary associations OR unique mechanics OR cultural symbolism
- **Standard** = Common weapon, typical enhancement path

**NPC Archetype Pairings:**
- **Combat Roles:** Duelist, Cavalry Raider, Heavy Infantry, Skirmisher, Archer
- **Cultural Roles:** Steppe Nomad, Silk Road Merchant Guard, Temple Warrior, Desert Scout, Mountain Clansman

## ANCESTRY-SPECIFIC WEAPON TRADITIONS

### ORCS - Clan Diversity

**Cultural Principle:** "Every clan's blade tells where they've been, who they've fought beside, who they've overcome."

**Historical Context:** 1,000 years post-liberation (~40 generations), Orc clans scattered across the Silk Road and developed diverse martial traditions based on:
- Where they settled
- Who sheltered them during the chaos period
- Which peoples they allied with
- What weapons they seized and mastered

**Weapon Philosophy:** Orcs are **weapon-agnostic**. An Orc warband might carry shamshirs, dao, qiang spears, composite bows, and tabar axes simultaneously - each weapon representing a different clan heritage or regional alliance.

**Signature Characteristic:** NOT a specific weapon, but **variety itself**. An all-Orc warband with 10 different weapon types is iconic.

**Mechanical Implication:** Orcs can justify proficiency with ANY weapon on this list by explaining which clan/region/tradition it comes from:
- "My grandmother's clan fled east during the chaos - we fight with dao like the steppe warriors who sheltered us"
- "My people settled in the mountain passes - we learned the khanda from Rajput traders turned friends"
- "Our clan? We take weapons from those who deserved to lose them"

**Cultural Role:** Orcs are the most cosmopolitan weapon merchants and smiths on the Silk Road, with 1,000 years of accumulated expertise. Orc martial academies teach multiple weapon traditions and attract students from across the known world. Heroes seeking training will find Orc weapon-masters who have perfected techniques from a dozen or more cultures—knowledge passed down across 40 generations.

**Common Orc Arsenal by Settlement Region:**
- **Persian Diaspora Clans:** Shamshir, Pesh-kabz, Gurz
- **Central Asian Clans:** Dao, Composite Recurve, Kilij
- **Chinese Settlement Clans:** Qiang, Jian, Guan Dao
- **Indian Subcontinent Clans:** Talwar, Khanda, Katar
- **Mountain Clans:** Bardiche, Pulwar, throwing axes
- **Scavenger Clans:** Eclectic mix of seized/improvised weapons

**Rare Memorial Weapons:** Some Orcs carry "Breaker's Chains" (weaponized shackles) or mining picks as ceremonial remembrance of captivity, though these are rarely used in actual combat.

### OTHER ANCESTRIES - Weapon Affinities

**DRAKONA (Dragon People)**
- **Strong Fit:** Glaive/Guan Dao (reach + power), Fire Lance (breath weapon synergy), Tabar, Hook Swords (reimagined as dragon talons)
- **Variants:** "Scaled Glaive" (dragon scale-wrapped), "Breath Lance" (channels elemental breath)

**DWARVES**
- **Strong Fit:** Warhammer, Gurz, Shishpar, Battleaxe/Tabar, Crossbow (engineering), Short Axes (Fu)
- **Variants:** "Rune Hammer" (etched runes), "Deep-Forged Axe" (mountain clan signature)

**FAUNS (Goat People)**
- **Strong Fit:** Spear/Qiang (shepherd origins), Composite Recurve (mountain agility), Rope Dart (sure-footedness)
- **Variants:** "Horn-tipped Spear" (shed horn caps), "Shepherd's Bow"

**FIRBOLG (Cow People)**
- **Strong Fit:** Quarterstaff (gentle giant), Glaive (reach + power), Bardiche (agricultural origins), Meteor Hammer
- **Variants:** "Grove Guardian Staff" (living wood), "Harvest Blade" (peaceful tool turned weapon)

**GIANTS**
- **Strong Fit:** Two-handed weapons become one-handed; Glaive, Sledge Axes, Lance (as spear), Meteor Hammer (scaled up)
- **Variants:** All weapons gain "Giant-Forged" (+size, +reach category)

**GOBLINS**
- **Strong Fit:** Khanjar/Jambiya, Hook Swords (dirty fighting), Rope Dart (hit-and-run), Repeating Crossbow (ingenuity), Thunder Crash Bomb
- **Variants:** "Scrap-Forged Blade" (improvised materials), "Trickster's Hook"

**HALFLINGS**
- **Strong Fit:** Sling (classic), Shortbow/Crossbow, Dagger/Khanjar, Chakram (finesse over strength)
- **Variants:** "Lucky Blade" (reroll feature), "Hearthguard Sling"

**INFERNIS (Demon-Folk)**
- **Strong Fit:** Fire Lance (flame association), Chain Whip (infernal chains), Katar, Scimitar/Shamshir (hellblade aesthetic), Bagh Nakh
- **Variants:** "Hellchain Whip" (burns on strike), "Ember Blade" (glows with inner heat)

**KATARI (Cat People)**
- **Strong Fit:** Bagh Nakh/Tiger Claws (PERFECT), Hook Swords, Butterfly Swords, Rope Dart, Chakram
- **Variants:** "Pride Claws" (ceremonial), "Stalker's Hook"

**HUMANS**
- **Strong Fit:** All weapons (cultural diversity mirrors real Silk Road)
- **Principle:** Human weapon choice reflects specific cultural origin/background

**MIXED ANCESTRY**
- **Philosophy:** Can use ANY weapon from either parent ancestry's cultural tradition, creating hybrid fighting styles
