## CHARM SYSTEM HYBRID RESOURCE DECISION

### Background
Previous iterations of Tarim-Shaiel's Charm system struggled with resource management and narrative progression. Initial designs were too complex, drawing from multiple systems without clear coherence.

### Decision: Hybrid FP + Hope Resource System

#### Core Components
- **Fatigue Points (FP):** Tactical, frequent Charm use
- **Will Points:** Strategic, high-impact Charm activation
- **Hope:** Narrative progression, mythic awakening signal

#### Key Design Principles
1. Preserve narrative agency
2. Create meaningful character progression
3. Provide resource management depth
4. Ensure each Tier feels distinctly powerful

### Rejected Alternatives

#### Option A: Pure Hope Economy
- **Pros:** Simplified resource tracking
- **Cons:** Limited Charm activation frequency
- **Decision:** Rejected due to potential player frustration

#### Option B: Exalted Mote System
- **Pros:** Proven complex resource management
- **Cons:** Too intricate for Tarim-Shaiel's narrative focus
- **Decision:** Rejected to maintain narrative clarity

#### Option C: Daggerheart Direct Adaptation
- **Pros:** Integrated Hope/Fear mechanics
- **Cons:** Didn't support heroic progression narrative
- **Decision:** Partially adopted, significantly modified

### Implementation Details

#### Hope Gain Mechanisms
- Duality dice rolls
- Resisting tool whispers
- Mythic Tier bonuses
- Ally interactions

#### Charm Activation Costs
- Tier 1: 2 FP or 1 Hope
- Tier 2: 3 FP or 1 Hope + 1 Will
- Tier 3: 2-3 Hope exclusively

### Competency Area Integration
- Narrative-driven Charm access
- XP and skill investment expansion
- Preserves fictional coherence

## FUTURE EXPLORATION

## SURRENDERED-LAYER FRAMEWORK — AWAKENING DESIGN PRINCIPLE

**Date:** 2026-03-20
**Status:** Proposed — not yet locked. Pending review and confirmation against character files.

### Decision

The **Surrendered-Layer Framework** is adopted as the governing design principle for all awakening scenarios.

**Core principle:** Each tool is the specific attribute surrendered at the threshold. The tool's behavior — its voice, its resistance, its hunger — is the exiled self trying to reclaim primacy. The awakening's crisis is always the same question: *will the hero let the surrendered thing come back?*

### Rationale

Emerged from comparative analysis of three independently-written awakening scenarios (Warrior, Seeker, Breaker). The pattern was already present in all three without having been explicitly designed for. Making it explicit:

- Provides a design checklist for the remaining seven awakenings
- Ensures the expanded archetype list (Trickster, Crafter, Sentinel, Healer) has comparable narrative weight to the core three
- Explains why the tools feel like they have agency — they do; they're the exiled self
- Gives the R/H/K mechanic a cleaner rationale: Resist/Hunger/Know are the surrendered attribute lobbying to return, not neutral tool-states

### The Surrendered Layers (Proposed, Pending Lock)

| Archetype | Layer | What they gave up |
|---|---|---|
| Seeker | Epistemic | Certainty as operating system |
| Warrior | Tactical | Force as the clean answer |
| Breaker | Ontological | Identity defined by what they can destroy |
| Bridge | Relational | The right to have a side |
| Sacrificer | Volitional | The right to refuse |
| Visionary | Perceptual | The right to look away |
| Trickster | Authenticating | The right to be believed when telling the truth |
| Crafter | Receptive | The experience of the world as finished |
| Sentinel | Doxastic | The ability to trust without evidence |
| Healer | Restorative | The certainty that wholeness is possible |
