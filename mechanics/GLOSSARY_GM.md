# GM Glossary — Tarim-Shaiel

**GM Reference — Canonical vocabulary for design and prep work.**
Player-facing terms belong in `world/GLOSSARY_PLAYER.md`. When a term here has a player-facing name, that alias is noted.

Entry format:
- **Domain:** narrative | mechanical | structural
- **Lock status:** locked | provisional | open
- **Definition:** plain-language definition
- **Cross-refs:** linked source docs
- **First defined:** where the term was established

---

## Beacon Event
**Domain:** narrative
**Lock status:** locked
**Definition:** The liberation performed by the heroes 1,000 years ago. Cosmologically significant not because it was wrong, but because its scale sent Warren ripples continent-wide, stirring the Held Breath and drawing the attention of entities with their own agendas. "Beacon Event" is the precise term; "ecosystem damage" is explicitly wrong framing.
**Cross-refs:** → `DECISION_LOG.md` (liberation framing decision); `narrative/gm_secrets/STAKEHOLDER_KNOWLEDGE_DISTRIBUTION.md`
**First defined:** CLAUDE.md hard constraints; DECISION_LOG 2026-03-08

## Convergence Point (CP)
**Domain:** structural
**Lock status:** locked
**Definition:** A moment of maximum gravitational pull where multiple independent world-forces arrive at the same location simultaneously. Convergence Points are not quests or plot waypoints — they are inevitable collisions between forces already in motion. Heroes cannot dodge them indefinitely. Six CPs are defined for the mid-campaign arc (Sessions 3–26), each labeled CP1–CP6.
**Cross-refs:** → `narrative/gm_secrets/MID_CAMPAIGN_CONVERGENCE_ARCHITECTURE.md`
**First defined:** Mid-Campaign Progressive Stakes Architecture (2026-03-20)

## Gaes
**Domain:** narrative / mechanical
**Lock status:** locked
**Definition:** The incomplete charge the heroes carry — the unfinished work that caused them to be expelled from paradise. Not a curse; the structural shape of what was left undone. The Chain-Breakers Order holds the most precise practical description of the gaes requirements.
**Cross-refs:** → `narrative/gm_secrets/STAKEHOLDER_KNOWLEDGE_DISTRIBUTION.md` (Chain-Breakers entry)

## Held Breath
**Domain:** narrative
**Lock status:** locked
**Definition:** The liminal consciousnesses that exist between dormancy and waking, between the Warren layer and the world above. Their nature resists direct observation. Stirred by the Beacon Event 1,000 years ago; their full waking is the cosmological endgame threat. **Do not use:** "sleeping entity" or "sleeping entities" — these terms are prohibited.
**Cross-refs:** → `narrative/gm_secrets/STAKEHOLDER_KNOWLEDGE_DISTRIBUTION.md`; `narrative/gm_secrets/DIVINE_PLAYERS.md`
**First defined:** CLAUDE.md hard constraints

## Layer 1 / Layer 2 / Layer 3 Revelations
**Domain:** structural
**Lock status:** locked
**Definition:** The Three-Layer Revelation structure governing how cosmological truth is disclosed to heroes and players. Layer 1: the incomplete charge named and confirmed. Layer 2: the Wizard's plan and its fatal flaw revealed. Layer 3: the full charge — not just stopping the Wizard but repairing what the beacon woke. Endgame scenarios must not be written without this structure.
**Cross-refs:** → `DECISION_LOG.md`; `narrative/gm_secrets/MID_CAMPAIGN_CONVERGENCE_ARCHITECTURE.md`
**First defined:** DECISION_LOG cosmological architecture lock (2026-03-08)

## R/H/K (Resist / Hunger / Know)
**Domain:** mechanical
**Lock status:** locked
**Definition:** The three behavioral modes of a hero's tool. **Resist:** the Warren protecting its investment, or protecting itself from what the hero is about to do. **Hunger:** the Warren (or its denizens) calling for energy use, for reasons the hero may not understand. **Know:** Warren-mediated revelation — what the Warren *wants* the hero to know (may be true, partial, or strategically framed). This is NOT the tool being protective of the hero. NOT neutral truth-delivery.
**Cross-refs:** → `CLAUDE.md` (R/H/K system); `DECISION_LOG.md` (reframe 2026-03-08)
**First defined:** DECISION_LOG R/H/K reframe (2026-03-08)

## Scholar's Purge
**Domain:** narrative
**Lock status:** locked
**Definition:** The Wizard's deliberate, targeted elimination of scholars who understood the cosmological situation — specifically those who held knowledge that could have allowed the heroes' incomplete charge to be completed. Confirmed as targeted (not incidental) by cross-referencing Dwarven disappearance records with Scholar's Remnant fragments.

## Shared Memory Event (SME)
**Domain:** structural / mechanical
**Lock status:** locked
**Definition:** One of five designed flashback events that surface collective heroic memory during play. Not pair-specific. Event-centric design; flexible pairing. Locked titles: The Warrior's Choice, The Question of Sight, The Weight of Choice, Aspirations Undone, The Wizard's Shadow. Count is locked at 5 — do not expand.
**Cross-refs:** → `narrative/Shared_Memory_Events.md`; `CLAUDE.md` (locked decisions)

## Threshold
**Domain:** narrative
**Lock status:** locked
**Definition:** The point at which the Held Breath move from stirring to waking. The cosmological endgame condition. The Wizard's plan would breach containment in a way that accelerates threshold approach; the heroes' charge is to prevent this while also completing the deeper repair work.
**Cross-refs:** → `narrative/gm_secrets/STAKEHOLDER_KNOWLEDGE_DISTRIBUTION.md`

## Tool Evolution (Stage 1 / 2 / 3)
**Domain:** mechanical
**Lock status:** provisional
**Definition:** The three stages of deepening relationship between a hero and their tool (animistic divine mark). Stage 1: tool resonance becomes active. Stage 2: R/H/K behaviors differentiate and deepen. Stage 3: K behavior reaches final form — surrendered self returned as choice. Evolution is tied to R/H/K consistency, not session count.
**Cross-refs:** → `mechanics/` (character progression files)
**First defined:** Tools-as-animistic-divine-marks decision (DECISION_LOG)

## The Warren
**Domain:** narrative / mechanical
**Lock status:** locked
**Definition:** The cosmological layer beneath the world — the substrate through which heroes' tools operate as ambassadors. Not a place heroes visit; a layer they sense through tools. Warren disturbance (not "ecosystem damage") is the correct framing for Held Breath effects on the world.
**Player-facing alias:** No direct player-facing name established yet. See `world/GLOSSARY_PLAYER.md`.
**Cross-refs:** → `CLAUDE.md` (hard constraints); `narrative/gm_secrets/STAKEHOLDER_KNOWLEDGE_DISTRIBUTION.md`

## The Wrongness
**Domain:** narrative / mechanical
**Lock status:** provisional
**Definition:** The environmental escalation of Warren disturbance across the mid-campaign arc. Three tiers: (1) Localized dead zones — magic fails, preservation fails; (2) Dead zones go "alive-wrong" — cause and effect locally suspend, heroes' virtues become traps; (3) A specific significant location simply stops being a location — not destroyed, ended. Always framed as Warren disturbance, never ecosystem damage.
**Cross-refs:** → `narrative/gm_secrets/MID_CAMPAIGN_CONVERGENCE_ARCHITECTURE.md`
**First defined:** Mid-Campaign Progressive Stakes Architecture (2026-03-20)

## Animistic Divine Mark
**Domain:** mechanical / narrative
**Lock status:** locked
**Definition:** The design-level framing of what a hero's tool is. Tools are not equipment — they are the hero's own divine nature externalized and made into an object. They possess consciousness. They remember the hero's past (including what the hero does not yet remember). They have genuine agency — they can refuse, resist, and redirect. They function as mirrors of the hero's R/H/K alignment. This is the locked cosmological reason that tools evolve rather than upgrade.
**Cross-refs:** → `CLAUDE.md` (locked decision 4); `mechanics/` (TOOL_EVOLUTION_FRAMEWORK.md)
**First defined:** CLAUDE.md locked decisions

## Celestial Peak

**Lock status:** open *(definition pending — no source file yet)*

Its cosmological significance is not controlled by any single faction; the competition for it is part of what makes it dangerous. ## Celestial Layer
**Domain:** narrative
**Lock status:** locked
**Definition:** The cosmological stratum above the Warren layer, inhabited by self-interested divine players rather than a unified pantheon. Entities here are cosmologically aware, politically independent, and have been observing the Held Breath crisis from their own angles. The Celestial Layer is not heaven in any redemptive sense — it is where power of sufficient magnitude operates. Four of the seven canonical divine players are rooted here; the Shattered King and Chaos Weaver occupy the pre-cosmic layer below the Warren system.
**Cross-refs:** → `narrative/gm_secrets/DIVINE_PLAYERS.md`
**First defined:** DIVINE_PLAYERS.md (2026-03-19)

## Celestial Memory
**Domain:** mechanical / narrative
**Lock status:** provisional
**Definition:** Fragments of the heroes' experience in paradise (Svarga) that resurface as the tool relationship deepens. Not remembered consciously — surfaced through the tool's K (Know) behavior, triggered by specific encounters, locations, and synthesis events. Celestial memory is how heroes gradually recognize what the distributed stakeholder knowledge is describing: they lived inside the ecosystem, and the tools remember. The heroes do not arrive in the world with access to these memories; they recover them.

## The Fallen Companion
**Domain:** narrative
**Lock status:** open *(definition pending — WIZARD_AND_LICH_CADRE.md is planned)*
**Definition:** A ghost companion — a figure who died connected to the heroes' original charge and who now functions as an ambiguous guide. The Fallen Companion is not a reliable narrator: they have their own perspective on the incomplete charge and what it cost. Their relationship to the Wizard's original intervention is unclear until mid-campaign. Role in play: structural ambiguity — comfort that may mislead, guidance that may be incomplete.

## Information Synthesis Puzzle
**Domain:** structural
**Lock status:** locked
**Definition:** The core campaign mechanic by which heroes become the only beings holding the complete cosmological picture. Not a quest to find a MacGuffin — the heroes are the synthesis mechanism. No single stakeholder has the full picture; the Held Breath's nature resists direct observation; the distributed knowledge requires celestial-touched tools and cross-faction standing to assemble. The puzzle resolves gradually across Acts I-III, with each stakeholder faction contributing a layer that requires others to interpret. See `STAKEHOLDER_KNOWLEDGE_DISTRIBUTION.md` for the full synthesis table and dependencies.
**Cross-refs:** → `narrative/gm_secrets/STAKEHOLDER_KNOWLEDGE_DISTRIBUTION.md` (Section 3)

## Lich Cadre
**Domain:** narrative
**Lock status:** provisional *(WIZARD_AND_LICH_CADRE.md is planned; entry reflects current design intent)*
**Definition:** The Wizard's inner command structure — 8-12 Liches drawn from multiple species, each representing a culture the Wizard has penetrated and subverted. The Cadre is the Wizard's century-spanning administrative apparatus: they maintain personas, infiltrate institutions, and execute his long-term plan while he operates at remove. Each Lich has genuine depth — not simply corrupted, but beings who accepted the Wizard's offer for reasons that made sense from inside their own story.
**Player-facing alias:** The Lich Cadre (player-accessible name; scope of subversion is GM-layer)

## Lich-Legion
**Domain:** narrative
**Lock status:** provisional *(full spec in WIZARD_AND_LICH_CADRE.md, planned)*
**Definition:** The Wizard's military and coercive apparatus, commanded by the Lich Cadre. Not merely a standing army — a force that has been assembling, infiltrating, and positioning across the continent for centuries. The Lich-Legion is the visible edge of the Wizard's plan; most mortal factions are aware of it as a threat but do not understand its true scale or that it serves a cosmological agenda rather than a territorial one.

## Memory Fragment
**Domain:** mechanical
**Lock status:** locked
**Definition:** A discrete unit of celestial memory — a specific recovered piece of the hero's experience in Svarga or during the original charge. Memory Fragments surface through the tool's K (Know) behavior and through Shared Memory Events. They are not explained; they are experienced. A fragment surfacing is not the hero being told something — it is the hero briefly becoming who they were. The accumulation of fragments is how heroes move from "stop the Wizard" (Layer 1) toward the full cosmological picture (Layer 3).
**Cross-refs:** → `mechanics/` (TOOL_EVOLUTION_FRAMEWORK.md); `narrative/Shared_Memory_Events.md`
**First defined:** CLAUDE.md (locked decisions — Vestiges/Memory Fragments/The Wrongness)

## Pre-Cosmic Layer
**Domain:** narrative
**Lock status:** provisional
**Definition:** The cosmological stratum below the Warren system's base — the exhausted, fallen-energy spaces that predate the current order. The Shattered King has occupied it since before the Silk Road existed. The Chaos Weaver manifests structurally at the boundary between it and the active Warrens. The pre-cosmic layer is not accessible to heroes through normal Warren interaction; its adjacency to the Held Breath makes it cosmologically significant — if the Threshold breaks, the boundary between the active Warrens and this layer may dissolve.
**Cross-refs:** → `narrative/gm_secrets/DIVINE_PLAYERS.md` (The Shattered King, The Chaos Weaver)
**First defined:** DIVINE_PLAYERS.md (2026-03-19)

## Surrendered Layer
**Domain:** mechanical / narrative
**Lock status:** locked
**Definition:** The specific attribute each hero surrendered at the threshold when they crossed into paradise — the aspect of themselves externalized as their tool. Each archetype has a distinct surrendered layer: ontological (identity), epistemic (knowing), volitional (the right to refuse), perceptual (the right to look away), relational (the right to have a side), and so on. The tool's voice is the exiled self trying to reclaim primacy. Tool evolution is the process of the hero deciding whether to reclaim that layer or integrate it differently. This is the design-level reason that all tools have distinct voice characters and crisis tests.
**Cross-refs:** → `narrative/gm_secrets/Session_0_Awakening_Design_Notes.md` (§THE SURRENDERED-LAYER FRAMEWORK); `mechanics/` (TOOL_EVOLUTION_FRAMEWORK.md)
**First defined:** Surrendered-Layer Framework (DECISION_LOG 2026-03-20)

## Svarga / Hero Heaven / Paradise
**Domain:** narrative
**Lock status:** locked
**Definition:** The cosmological location from which the heroes were expelled — a genuine paradise, earned through the original charge's completion, not a metaphor or false reward. The heroes' expulsion is not punishment; it is recognition that something was left unfinished. The term "Hero Heaven" is the working project title and common shorthand. "Svarga" is the in-world term, drawn from Vedic cosmology adapted for the setting. Players are not told they are the heroes; they discover this through play. The existence and nature of Svarga is GM-layer knowledge until Layer 1 revelation.
**Player-facing alias:** No direct player-facing name until Layer 1 revelation; referred to obliquely in legend as "the rest the heroes earned"
**Cross-refs:** → `CLAUDE.md` (core premise); `narrative/gm_secrets/WHAT_THIS_CAMPAIGN_IS.md`
**First defined:** CLAUDE.md (campaign premise)

## The Wizard
**Domain:** narrative
**Lock status:** locked (motivation/awareness locked 2026-03-17 as Decision 4 — B+C)
**Definition:** The primary antagonist. A being of genuine intelligence and real cosmological understanding who has spent a thousand years becoming certain he is the one person equipped to manage the Held Breath crisis. His certainty is itself a product of Held Breath influence that groomed him for five hundred years before the liberation. He is not a fool, not purely a villain by his own lights, and his cosmological knowledge is largely accurate. He is wrong in the specific way that matters: his confidence that he can control the outcome. He knows the heroes are the only beings who could complete the charge he has spent a thousand years preventing. He has been the primary force preventing it.
**Player-facing alias:** The Wizard (player-accessible; identity/history GM-layer)
**Cross-refs:** → `narrative/gm_secrets/WIZARD_AND_LICH_CADRE.md` (planned); `narrative/gm_secrets/STAKEHOLDER_KNOWLEDGE_DISTRIBUTION.md` (The Wizard entry); `CLAUDE.md` (Decision 4 lock)

## Warren Ambassador
**Domain:** narrative / mechanical
**Lock status:** locked
**Definition:** What a hero's tool actually is, from the Warren's perspective: not an object the hero possesses, but the Warren's own representative attached to the hero. The tool senses the Warren layer directly where all other mortal beings observe only symptoms. This is why heroes are uniquely positioned to synthesize the information puzzle — their tools are Warren-native in a way no other sensing instrument can replicate. The term is used in design and GM prep; it is not a player-facing term until the K (Know) behavior deepens sufficiently to make the explanation land.

## Warren Disturbance
**Domain:** narrative
**Lock status:** locked
**Definition:** The correct framing for the effects the Held Breath's stirring produces in the world — and for the beacon event's ongoing consequences. Warren disturbance is not ecosystem damage (prohibited framing). It is a signal propagating through the Warren layer: dead zones, degrading ancestor communication, charm failure rates, and eventually The Wrongness. Different factions observe different surface symptoms; Warren disturbance is the unified underlying cause. The distinction matters because "ecosystem damage" implies repair; Warren disturbance implies reckoning.
**Cross-refs:** → `CLAUDE.md` (hard constraints, liberation framing); `narrative/gm_secrets/STAKEHOLDER_KNOWLEDGE_DISTRIBUTION.md`
**First defined:** CLAUDE.md hard constraints; DECISION_LOG 2026-03-08

## Warren Intelligence
**Domain:** narrative
**Lock status:** provisional
**Definition:** A Warren-layer entity with sufficient coherence to be considered an agent — not the same as a divine player, but not a mindless force either. Warren Intelligences have arrangements with Celestial Layer entities (particularly the Conquering Heaven). Their relationship to the heroes' tools is structurally significant: the tools are Warren ambassadors attached to heroes, which means Warren Intelligences have something like a standing interest in what the heroes do.
