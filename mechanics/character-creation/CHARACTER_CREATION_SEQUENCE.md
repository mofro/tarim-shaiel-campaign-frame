# CHARACTER CREATION SEQUENCE

**Date:** 2026-01-11  
**Status:** Core Design Document  
**Purpose:** Define the complete character creation flow from Session -1 through Session 0, including questionnaire structure and collaborative elements

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Character creation spans two phases:**
1. **Session -1 (Pre-Game)**: Trait-based questionnaire + Class collaboration (async)
2. **Session 0 (Awakening)**: Individual awakening scenes + first meeting (actual play)

**Key Design Principle:** Players discover their **mythic Archetype** through play, but choose their **mortal Class** collaboratively upfront.

---

## PART 1: SESSION -1 (PRE-GAME QUESTIONNAIRE)

### **Overview**

**Format:** Asynchronous (email, shared document, Discord)  
**Timing:** 1-2 weeks before Session 0  
**Duration:** Players complete individually (~30 minutes), then group discussion (~1 hour)

**Goals:**
1. Players choose Daggerheart Class (open/collaborative)
2. GM secretly maps players to Archetypes (trait-based)
3. Players establish mortal backgrounds
4. Party composition balanced through Class discussion

---

### **QUESTIONNAIRE STRUCTURE**

**Part 1: Mortal Identity (Known to Players)**

```
DAGGERHEART CLASS SELECTION

Choose the class that represents how you act in the world:
- Bard: Master of performance and social influence
- Druid: Nature-bonded shapeshifter and wild magic
- Guardian: Steadfast protector and defensive bulwark
- Ranger: Wilderness expert and tactical hunter
- Rogue: Cunning infiltrator and shadow operator
- Seraph: Divine champion and radiant warrior
- Sorcerer: Innate magic wielder and elemental force
- Warrior: Martial expert and weapon master
- Wizard: Scholarly mage and arcane researcher

Your Class: _______________

MORTAL BACKGROUND

Answer briefly (2-3 sentences each):

1. Where are you from? (Region, culture, environment)
2. What skills did you develop? (Training, profession, expertise)
3. What defines your personality? (Traits, mannerisms, values)
4. What are you known for among those who know you? (Reputation, deeds)
```

---

**Part 2: Mythic Nature (Secret—GM Maps to Archetype)**

```
CORE NATURE QUESTIONS

Answer instinctively. There are no wrong answers.

1. When faced with an insurmountable obstacle, you tend to:
   A) Break through it by force or will
   B) Find a way around by connecting with others
   C) Study it until you understand its nature
   D) Protect others from it while finding a solution
   E) Look beyond the immediate problem to see larger patterns
   F) Confront it directly with courage and strength

2. What drives you more deeply:
   A) Liberation—freeing yourself and others from constraints
   B) Connection—building and maintaining meaningful bonds
   C) Truth—uncovering what is hidden or unknown
   D) Service—giving of yourself for others' benefit
   E) Vision—seeing what others cannot see
   F) Honor—upholding principles and proving worth

3. Your greatest fear is:
   A) Being controlled or constrained against your will
   B) Being isolated or cut off from others
   C) Being ignorant of important truths
   D) Being selfish or failing those who depend on you
   E) Being blind to danger or unable to see clearly
   F) Being weak or proven unworthy

4. When you succeed at something important, you feel:
   A) Free—constraints lifted, possibilities opened
   B) Connected—bonds strengthened, relationships deepened
   C) Enlightened—understanding expanded, mysteries solved
   D) Fulfilled—others benefited, needs met
   E) Certain—future clarified, path revealed
   F) Vindicated—worth proven, honor upheld

5. If you could change one thing about the world:
   A) Remove systems that bind and oppress
   B) Help people understand each other better
   C) Make all knowledge accessible to everyone
   D) Ensure no one suffers needlessly
   E) Give people sight beyond immediate concerns
   F) Establish clear codes of honor and justice

6. Your role in a group naturally becomes:
   A) The one who challenges limitations
   B) The one who maintains relationships
   C) The one who uncovers information
   D) The one who supports others' needs
   E) The one who sees what's coming
   F) The one who faces threats directly

7. When making difficult decisions, you prioritize:
   A) Freedom and autonomy
   B) Relationships and connections
   C) Understanding and truth
   D) Others' wellbeing and needs
   E) Long-term consequences and patterns
   F) Principles and honor

8. The phrase that resonates most with you:
   A) "Break the chains"
   B) "We are stronger together"
   C) "Seek and you shall find"
   D) "What I have is yours"
   E) "I see what others miss"
   F) "Stand and be counted"
```

**GM SCORING GUIDE (Not Shared with Players):**

```
Tally responses:
Mostly A → BREAKER archetype
Mostly B → BRIDGE archetype
Mostly C → SEEKER archetype
Mostly D → SACRIFICER archetype
Mostly E → VISIONARY archetype
Mostly F → WARRIOR archetype

Mixed results: Choose dominant pattern or blend thematically
```

---

**Part 3: Collaborative Class Discussion**

After individual completion, players share (in group channel/call):

```
CLASS COORDINATION DISCUSSION

Share with the group:
1. Your chosen Class
2. Brief description of your character concept
3. What role you'd enjoy playing (combat, support, social, etc.)

Discuss:
- Are there overlaps? (Not a problem, but worth noting)
- Are there gaps? (Missing healer, damage-dealer, etc.)
- Do concepts complement each other?
- Would anyone like to adjust their Class choice?

Final Decisions:
Record each player's confirmed Class selection.
```

**GM privately notes:** Archetype assignments based on questionnaires (players DON'T know these yet)

## PART 2: SESSION 0 (AWAKENING & FIRST MEETING)

### **Overview**

**Format:** Actual play session  
**Duration:** 3-4 hours  
**Structure:** Individual scenes → Convergence → Shared threat

---

### **PHASE 1: INDIVIDUAL AWAKENING SCENES (30-45 min total)**

**Run each player through solo awakening (5-10 minutes each):**

```
AWAKENING SCRIPT TEMPLATE

1. DISORIENTATION
   "You wake. Darkness. Cold. The smell of... [filth/decay/earth].
    You don't remember falling asleep.
    You don't remember... much of anything clearly."

2. THE BINDING
   "Your wrist burns—leather thong biting into skin.
    You reach for it and feel... [describe crude tool].
    It's bound to you. You can't remove it."

3. FIRST WHISPER
   [Archetype-specific whisper - soft, insistent]
   
   BREAKER: "Break free. Shatter your chains."
   BRIDGE: "Reach out. You are not alone."
   SEEKER: "Look closely. Truth is here."
   SACRIFICER: "Give of yourself. Others need you."
   VISIONARY: "See beyond. This is not all there is."
   WARRIOR: "Stand. Face what comes."

4. FIRST CHALLENGE
   [Immediate threat appropriate to location]
   - Forces tool use
   - Reveals power beyond normal capability
   - Creates confusion: "How did I do that?"

5. THE QUESTION
   "As the threat fades, you look at the [tool] in your hand.
    It feels... familiar somehow. Like something you've held before.
    But you don't remember when. Or why.
    
    What do you do?"
```

**GM Notes:**
- Customize location to player's background (urban, wilderness, etc.)
- First whisper introduces Archetype flavor without naming it
- Challenge should be survive-able but frightening
- End with player mobile, tool bound, questions unanswered

---

### **PHASE 2: CONVERGENCE (30-45 min)**

**Narrative Hook Options:**

**Option A: Common Destination**
- All heroes drawn to same landmark/location
- Natural meeting point (oasis, crossroads, caravanserai)
- Each arrived separately, don't know why

**Option B: Shared Threat**
- Danger threatens area where heroes awakened
- Forces cooperation to survive
- "Enemy of my enemy" temporary alliance

**Option C: Mysterious Summons**
- All received same vision/dream/compulsion
- Converge on location seeking answers
- Meet as fellow seekers

**Convergence Scene:**

```
FIRST MEETING SCRIPT

1. CAUTIOUS APPROACH
   "You see others approaching [location].
    They move cautiously, hands on... [tools].
    They look as confused as you feel."

2. MUTUAL RECOGNITION
   "Something about them feels... familiar?
    No. That's not right. You don't know them.
    But maybe you share something?"

3. TOOL REACTIONS
   "Your [tool] grows warm. Theirs do too.
    A low hum—felt more than heard—
    connects you all for a moment."

4. BREAKING THE ICE
   [Let players roleplay first contact]
   Prompt: "What do you say? Or do?"

5. ESTABLISHING ALLIANCE
   "Whatever brought you here,
    you're clearly not ordinary travelers.
    And you clearly need answers.
    
    Maybe you need each other?"
```

---

### **PHASE 3: SHARED THREAT (60-90 min)**

**Structure:** Combat or challenge that requires cooperation

**Purpose:**
- Demonstrate how different Classes complement each other
- Show tools working in concert (visual/narrative connection)
- Create bonds through shared survival
- End with "We should stay together... for now"

**Example Encounter:**

```
BANDITS + CORRUPTED ELEMENT

Setup:
- Road bandits ambush the group
- But bandits are fleeing something worse
- Corrupted creature/environment emerges
- Forces heroes + bandits to cooperate or die

Mechanical Goals:
- Each Class gets spotlight moment
- Tools demonstrate power beyond normal
- Whispers provide guidance (follow or resist?)
- Hope economy introduced through actual use

Narrative Goals:
- Heroes prove capable (but don't understand why)
- Tools reveal partial nature (not full identity)
- Shared survival creates foundation for trust
- Questions multiply: "What ARE these things?"

Resolution:
- Threat overcome (barely)
- Survivors scatter or express awe/fear
- Heroes left standing together
- Tools pulse with satisfied warmth
```

---

### **PHASE 4: SESSION 0 WRAP-UP (15-30 min)**

**Establish Forward Motion:**

```
CLOSING SCENE

1. IMMEDIATE AFTERMATH
   "The [threat] is gone. You're alive.
    Your [tools] seem... pleased? Is that possible?"

2. SHARED CONFUSION
   Invite players to share character thoughts:
   - "Did anyone else hear whispers?"
   - "How did we do that?"
   - "What are these things bound to us?"

3. PRACTICAL DECISIONS
   - Where do you go from here?
   - Do you stay together?
   - What do you need (supplies, shelter, answers)?

4. HOOK FOR SESSION 1
   [Plant mystery seed]
   Examples:
   - NPC recognizes one hero's tool (but shouldn't)
   - Vision/dream of place none have been
   - News of similar "awakened" causing trouble
   - Tool whispers about somewhere you "must" go

5. FINAL MOMENT
   "As you [make camp/enter town/set watch],
    your tools rest within reach.
    Still bound. Still mysterious.
    Still yours.
    
    What you don't know yet:
    They remember everything you've forgotten."
```

## PART 3: POST-SESSION 0

### **Between Sessions 0 and 1**

**GM Actions:**
- Document tool descriptions as customized by players
- Prepare Charm reveals for Session 1
- Note which Archetype clues players responded to
- Plan first flashback/memory trigger

**Player Actions:**
- Reflect on Session 0 events
- Decide how character feels about tools
- Consider relationships with other heroes
- (Optional) Begin journaling character thoughts

## PART 4: DESIGN NOTES

### **Why This Structure Works**

**Preserves Mystery:**
- Players don't know Archetypes = genuine surprise when revealed
- Questionnaire feels like personality test, not mechanical choice
- Awakening confusion mirrors character confusion

**Enables Collaboration:**
- Class selection is open/transparent
- Party composition discussed before play
- First meeting creates shared investment

**Creates Investment:**
- Individual awakening = personal connection to tool
- Shared threat = bonds through adversity
- Mystery = hooks for continued play

### **Common Pitfalls to Avoid**

**DON'T:**
- Tell players their Archetype names in Session 0
- Force specific party compositions
- Railroad first meeting (let it emerge)
- Skip individual awakening scenes (essential for investment)
- Resolve tool mysteries too quickly

**DO:**
- Let players customize tool appearance gradually
- Encourage questions (but don't answer everything)
- Make tools feel alive (warm/cool reactions, whispers)
- End Session 0 with forward momentum
- Celebrate survival and cooperation

## PART 5: QUESTIONNAIRE TEMPLATES

### **Email Template for Session -1**

```
Subject: Tarim-Shaiel Character Creation - Session -1

Hello [Player Name],

Welcome to Tarim-Shaiel! Our Session 0 is scheduled for [date/time], but before then, please complete this questionnaire to help shape your character.

This has three parts:

1. MORTAL IDENTITY (20 min)
   - Choose your Daggerheart Class
   - Answer background questions
   
2. CORE NATURE (10 min)
   - Answer instinctively, no wrong answers
   - These help me understand your character's essence

3. CLASS DISCUSSION (after individual completion)
   - We'll discuss Class choices as a group
   - Opportunity to adjust for party balance

Please complete parts 1-2 by [deadline].
Then join [group channel/call] on [date/time] for part 3.

[Include questionnaire or link to form]

Looking forward to seeing your hero awaken!

[GM Name]
```

---

### **Group Discussion Facilitation Guide**

```
CLASS DISCUSSION AGENDA (60 minutes)

1. INTRODUCTIONS (15 min)
   Each player shares:
   - Chosen Class
   - 2-sentence character concept
   - What excites them about this choice

2. PARTY COMPOSITION (15 min)
   Discuss:
   - What roles are covered?
   - Any notable overlaps?
   - Any gaps that concern anyone?

3. ADJUSTMENT OPPORTUNITY (15 min)
   - Does anyone want to change Class?
   - Would tweaks improve party synergy?
   - Encourage but don't pressure

4. FINAL CONFIRMATION (10 min)
   - Lock in Class selections
   - Confirm everyone feels good about choices
   - Brief preview of Session 0 structure

5. Q&A (5 min)
   - Address any concerns
   - Clarify expectations
   - Build excitement
```
