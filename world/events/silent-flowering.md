## What Is Known (Canon)

The elves withdrew from active engagement with the world across a 300-year period. This coincides with:
- The Mediator's Peace (318–398) — the post-Liberation stabilization
- The Merchant's Rise (398–647) — Orc trade expansion fills the political vacuum
- The Goblin Free Cities (418–698) — opportunistic city-states proliferate

The withdrawal was gradual, not sudden. Something changed — or was decided — at the start of this period that set the elves on an inward course for three centuries.

## Design Notes

- **Model:** Ming dynasty withdrawal — deliberate policy choice after an expansionist moment, NOT decline or tragedy. The elves *chose* this.
- **The Zheng He question:** What was the elven equivalent of Zheng He's voyages — the brief expansionist moment that the conservative faction won the argument against? This is the origin story of the withdrawal.
- **The Japan question:** Which faction in Tarim-Shaiel refuses to acknowledge elven cultural gravity entirely? (Japan refused the Ming tributary system; someone refused the elves.)
- **"The Whispered Warning" (1048–1050 HB):** The elves briefly re-emerge at this late date — a 2-year Declaration just before the Gathering Storm. This is a crack in the withdrawal policy. What did they see?
- **Faction hook:** `elven-highland-enclaves` — Priority 1 faction file not yet built. This event is their defining historical arc.
- **Cosmological hook:** "Elven Cosmology: Celestial Refugees" concept (BACKLOG — Major Decision) may reframe the withdrawal entirely. Resolve that decision before treating this stub as design-stable.
