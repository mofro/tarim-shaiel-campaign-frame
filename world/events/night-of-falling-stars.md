## What Is Known (Canon)

A singular astronomical event at 1048 HB, observed across the known world. Coincides exactly with "The Whispered Warning (Elf Watchers)" 1048–1050 HB — the moment the Elf Watchers broke 350 years of silence.

The Night of Falling Stars is the **first domino** in the causal chain leading to campaign present. Every event that follows traces back to this night.

## Design Notes

- **The Elf Watchers connection is structural, not coincidental.** They had been watching since 698 HB — 350 years. They didn't begin watching because of this event. The event *answered* what they were watching for. What question had they been holding for three and a half centuries?
- **Warren framing:** The stars didn't fall. The world *moved*. What observers interpreted as a celestial event was the Held Breath stirring — the first visible tremor of the ecosystem disturbance that the entire campaign traces back to.
- **Islamic cosmological resonance:** Meteors as angels repelling djinn who eavesdrop on divine conversation — something below was listening to something above. In Tarim-Shaiel terms: the Held Breath pressed against the membrane, and the sky pushed back visibly.
- **Public vs. GM knowledge:** The Night of Falling Stars is a known historical event — legends, folk memory, scholarly records. What it *meant* is GM-only. Players may encounter references to it without knowing it's the inciting incident.
