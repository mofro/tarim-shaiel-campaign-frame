# Historical Timeline: The Thousands Years Arc

## Overview & Dating Conventions

The Tarim-Shaiel campaign spans almost **2,000 years of recorded history**, from the age of mythic heroes' legendary deeds to the campaign present. This timeline uses **HB (Tutulan Nefes "The Held Breath") equivalents** anchored to real-world Silk Road historical periods for reference, but represents a fantasy world with its own internal chronology.

> **Notes on the confusing state of date-keeping and usage:**
> 
> Dates can be represented as 
> * "*...the 3198th Year of The Held Breath (Tutulan Nefes)*", 
> * a local "political" time (e.g., "*Year 780 of Împeratoriya Nû (the New Empire)*": *TN 200*, or "*...the 200th Year of The Held Breath*"  )
> * a hyper-local convention (e.g., *Orc Migration, Year 25* or *TN: 278*)
>
>  

**Key Dating Anchor:**
- **Prehistory:** ~2000-0 HJ **(Jibîrkirina Hêdî — "The Slow Forgetting")** - Equivalent to "BC"
- **The Held Breath:** ~0-Present **HB (Tutulan Nefes — "The Held Breath")**
- **Heroes' Era:** ~200-250 HB equivalent (Late Antiquity parallel)
- **Campaign Present:** ~1200-1250 HB equivalent (High Medieval parallel)
- **Time Span:** 1,000 years between heroic deeds and return

**Historical Grounding:**
This timeline mirrors real Silk Road history (Han Dynasty → Medieval Islamic/Mongol period) but diverges through fantasy elements: magic, multiple sapient species with varying lifespans, and the Wizard's millennium-long conspiracy.

**Real-World Parallel Reference:**
- **200 HB Real:** Late Han Dynasty, early Sassanid Persia, Roman Empire height
- **1200 HB Real:** Song Dynasty China, Islamic Golden Age remnants, pre-Mongol invasion Central Asia

## Era 0: The Heroes' Time (~200-250 HB equivalent)
**"The Age of Chains"** (50 years)

### Dominant Powers

**Human Empire (name TBD):**
- Aggressive expansionist state controlling major Silk Road trade hubs
- Military power partially dependent on conquered peoples, including enslaved Orc legions via binding magic. Some of the binding has become banal and pragmatic through time
- Bureaucratic administration; tribute-based economic model [!]
- Controls: Major caravan routes, key oasis cities, mountain passes [!]

**Northern Dwarven Kingdoms:**
- Mountain strongholds in isolated high-altitude regions
- Master craftspeople and record-keepers
- Trade partners with empire but maintain independence through geography
- Neutral stance in imperial conflicts. “They are but visitors here, after all…”

**High Elf Enclaves:**
- Ancient, aloof, beginning slow withdrawal from mortal politics
- Concentrated in hidden forest sanctuaries and remote coastal regions
- Possess deep magical knowledge but rarely share it
- View mortal empires as "brief flickers" in their long existence

**Subjugated Orcs:**
- Enslaved through binding spells (combination magical/psychological control)
- Serve as military backbone of human empire (shock troops, garrison forces)
- No autonomous culture; generational trauma and enforced amnesia of pre-enslavement history
- Scattered across empire as controlled populations

**Other Peoples:**
- **Goblins:** Marginal city-states in borderlands; pragmatic survivors
- **Halflings/Gnomes:** Dispersed communities; traders and craftspeople within empire
- **Fauns/Katari:** Small independent settlements; avoid imperial attention
- **Mortal Elves:** Beginning to retreat from cosmopolitan centers toward isolation

### What the Heroes Did

**The Liberation:**
- Discovered the binding spell mechanism
- Systematically broke the magical chains enslaving Orc populations
- Freed tens of thousands across multiple regions simultaneously

**The Military Victory:**
- Defeated the empire's leadership through strategic and tactical brilliance
- Destroyed key imperial infrastructure (administrative centers, military depots)
- Allied with regional powers to fragment empire's control

**The Sacrifice:**
- A team member (name TBD, charismatic and upbeat) died in the final push
- Their death became the rallying cry that secured victory
- This sacrifice created the spiritual/emotional bond that later enables the gaes

**The Failure:**
- **Did not finish the Wizard:** He survived, retreated, and was presumed defeated or fled
- **Did not recognize their incomplete charge:** Believed liberation + empire collapse = complete work
- **Accepted reward prematurely:** Departed for Hero Heaven (the Celestial Peak) without ensuring long-term stability

### World State at Heroes' Departure

**Political Collapse:**
- Empire fragmenting into dozens of warlord states
- No clear successor authority; regional powers negotiate ad-hoc arrangements
- Trade routes collapsing as imperial enforcement fails

**Orc Liberation Crisis:**
- Freed Orcs suddenly autonomous with no cultural memory of self-governance
- Traumatized, directionless, armed populations across the continent
- No leadership structures; tribal/warband organization emerging chaotically

**The Wizard's Retreat:**
- Survives heroes' assault; realizes he was underestimated
- Withdraws to hidden sanctum (location TBD)
- Begins 1,000-year plan to access Hero Heaven and claim its power for himself

**Real-World Parallel:** Late Han Dynasty collapse → Three Kingdoms chaos; fall of Western Roman Empire

## Era 1: The Scattering (~250-400 HB equivalent)
**"The Age of Blood and Ash"** (150 years)

### Phase 1: The Orc Eruption (~250-320 HB, ~70 years)

**Immediate Post-Liberation Chaos:**
- Freed Orcs, with no cultural infrastructure, erupt into violence
- Raids on former imperial cities and settlements (revenge, resources, territorial claims)
- Inter-Orc warfare as warbands compete for dominance and territory
- Casualties mount on all sides; entire regions depopulated

**Human Response:**
- Former imperial cities fortify; become isolated city-states
- Warlord states compete for legitimacy and survival
- Some attempt to re-enslave Orcs (fail catastrophically)
- Migration away from contested zones; refugee crises

**Dwarven Adaptation:**
- Seal mountain passes; become fortress-cities
- Begin detailed record-keeping of the chaos (chronicle of the Scattering)
- Maintain limited trade through heavily guarded routes
- Neutral stance but defend borders ruthlessly

**Elven Withdrawal Accelerates:**
- **High Elves:** Almost completely vanish from mortal view; "diminishing" begins in earnest
- **Mortal Elves:** Retreat into deep forests; abandon cosmopolitan settlements
- Create hidden enclaves with magical wards
- Maintain minimal contact with outside world

**Trade Route Collapse:**
- Long-distance commerce nearly ceases
- Only short-range, highly protected caravans operate
- Caravanserai abandoned or converted to fortifications
- Economic depression across all regions

### Phase 2: Diplomatic Stabilization (~320-400 HB, ~80 years)

**The Mediator Movement:**
- **Dwarven record-keepers** propose first trade protocols based on historical precedent
- **Halfling mediators** emerge as neutral negotiators (non-threatening, ubiquitous, pragmatic)
- **Gnome inventors** create caravanserai defensive systems (early warning, fortified rest stops)
- **Merchant leagues** (multi-species) offer economic incentives: "Trade pays better than raiding"

**Formation of Orc Confederations:**
- First loose tribal alliances with trade charters
- Elder Orcs who remember pre-enslavement oral traditions emerge as leaders
- Gradual adoption of sedentary lifestyles in some regions
- Territorial agreements negotiated through Halfling/Dwarf intermediaries

**Emergence of New Political Structures:**
- **Independent city-states** replace empire (human, multi-species, Goblin)
- **Merchant republics** arise in key trade hubs
- **Dwarf mountain leagues** formalize defensive alliances
- **Orc war-confederacies** establish first recognized borders

**Goblin Opportunism:**
- Form powerful city-states in borderlands
- Profit from chaos through mercenary work, information brokering, smuggling
- Establish "neutral ground" trading posts between hostile territories

**The Wizard's Shadow Work (Hidden):**
- Uses chaos as cover to establish influence networks
- Positions "advisors" in multiple emerging power centers
- Begins cultivating proto-Liches (ambitious mages seeking immortality)
- Studies necromancy in secret; experiments with binding magic on the dead
- Establishes first hidden undead laboratories in remote ruins

**Key Event:**
- **The Truce of Nine Caravans (~370 HB equivalent):** First multi-species trade agreement
  - Negotiated by Halfling League and Dwarf Record-Keepers
  - Establishes protected trade corridors with shared security
  - Creates precedent for future diplomatic structures

**Real-World Parallel:** Hunnic invasions → Migration Period → Early medieval kingdoms forming; post-Roman fragmentation

## Era 2: The Flowering (~400-700 HB equivalent)
**"The Age of Many Voices"** (300 years)

### Cultural Renaissance

**Political Fragmentation Stabilizes:**
- No single hegemon; dozens of independent powers coexist
- Multi-species governance becomes norm in cosmopolitan hubs
- Regional identity stronger than pan-continental politics
- Power balance through trade networks rather than military dominance

**Religious & Philosophical Explosion:**
- Multiple belief systems emerge and spread via trade routes
- Syncretism common; cities host diverse temples and schools
- Theological debates replace theological wars (mostly)
- **Parallels real-world Buddhism/Islam/Christianity spread but with fantasy religions**

**Technology Transfer Accelerates:**
- Paper-making spreads from Eastern human kingdoms
- Advanced mathematics and astronomy shared across cultures
- Alchemical knowledge exchanged; early magical-technological fusion
- Agricultural innovations (irrigation, crop rotation) enable population growth

**Merchant Republics Flourish:**
- Sogdian-equivalent diaspora (possibly Halflings/Gnomes) control long-distance trade
- Banking houses, letters of credit, standardized weights/measures
- Multi-lingual cosmopolitan elite class
- "Merchant princes" rival nobility in wealth and influence

### Orc Integration & Transformation

**From Warriors to Traders:**
- Orc confederations transition to legitimate trading partners
- Some tribes remain nomadic raiders; others settle and found cities
- Inter-species marriages common in trade hubs

**Oral Epic Tradition Emerges:**
- Orcs develop rich storytelling culture preserving liberation history
- **Heroes become mythic figures in Orc oral epics**
- Songs, chants, ritual recitations keep memory alive
- Variants emerge across different confederations

**Cultural Innovations:**
- Distinctive Orc architectural styles (fortified trading posts)
- Unique musical traditions (war-songs adapted to celebration)
- Martial arts as cultural practice rather than pure warfare

### Faction Dynamics by Mid-Era (~550 HB equivalent)

**High Elves:**
- Fully withdrawn from mortal politics; near-mythical status
- Rumors of hidden sanctuaries in unreachable forests/islands
- Occasional individual emerges as advisor/observer then vanishes

**Mortal Elves (Wood, Sea, Earth):**
- Fully isolationist but maintain trade relationships
- Known for magical crafts, rare knowledge, mysterious ways
- Elven ambassadors appear at major diplomatic events then disappear for decades
- Lifespan advantage means individual elves form multi-generational relationships with other cultures

**Dwarves:**
- **Golden Age of Craftsmanship:** Dwarf-made goods set quality standard
- Cities become banking and trade hubs (trusted record-keepers)
- Mountain League formalizes as defensive alliance
- Begin extensive written histories (Chronicle of Ages)

**Goblins:**
- Establish powerful mercantile city-states controlling key chokepoints
- Rival Dwarf influence through pragmatism and ruthlessness
- Develop reputation: "Goblins deliver, no matter what"
- Goblin Free Cities become "neutral ground" for negotiations

**Fauns/Katari:**
- Emerge as cultural innovators (music, art, philosophy centers)
- Faun theatrical traditions spread; Katari poetry revered
- Small populations but outsized cultural influence
- Often serve as cultural ambassadors between larger powers

**Halflings/Gnomes:**
- **Ubiquitous but decentralized:** "Everywhere but nowhere in control"
- Halfling diplomats, Gnome engineers, both species as traders
- No unified Halfling/Gnome state; individuals embedded in all cultures
- Trusted as neutral mediators

**Humans:**
- Fragmented into regional kingdoms, city-states, merchant republics
- Some attempt to recreate empire's glory (all fail)
- Cultural/linguistic diversity increases over generations
- No pan-human identity; regional loyalties dominate

**Firbolg/Fungril/Fairies/Infernis:**
- Occupy ecological/cultural niches
- Small populations; isolated communities or integrated minorities
- Contribute specialized knowledge/skills to cosmopolitan centers

### The Wizard's Long Game (Hidden)

**Establishing Hidden Academies:**
- Poses as different scholars/philosophers across centuries
- Teaches necromancy disguised as "philosophy of death and transformation"
- Recruits talented mages from multiple species
- None realize they're being cultivated as future Liches

**The Lich Cadre Recruitment (~400-600 CE):**
- Identifies 8-12 powerful, ambitious mages across different species:
  - High Elf seeking to prevent "diminishing"
  - Dwarf obsessed with preserving knowledge beyond mortal limits
  - Human warlord's court mage fearing mortality
  - Orc shaman wanting to preserve liberation memory forever
  - Goblin merchant-prince seeking ultimate power
  - Others TBD
- Teaches each Lichdom ritual separately (none aware of others)
- Positions them in geographically dispersed locations

**Stockpiling Resources:**
- Collects corpses from battlefields, crypts, forgotten war-zones
- Establishes hidden laboratories in ruins, caves, remote wilderness
- Experiments with binding magic on the dead (perfecting control mechanisms)
- Creates false identities that "die" and are replaced every 50-80 years

**Strategic Positioning:**
- Places "advisors" in key courts (rotates personas to avoid detection)
- Encourages border conflicts to create fresh corpses
- Subtly steers policy toward instability (never obviously)
- Builds network of unwitting agents (informants, suppliers, guards)

### Key Events

**The Convocation of Eleven Cities (~550 HB equivalent):**
- Multi-species council establishes first pan-regional trade laws
- Creates bureaucratic frameworks for dispute resolution
- Foundation for later political structures
- **Unintended consequence:** Creates institutions Wizard later infiltrates

**The Great Drought (~620 HB equivalent):**
- Decade-long water crisis tests regional cooperation
- Dwarf engineers, Gnome inventors, Elf water-mages collaborate
- Shared infrastructure projects (aqueducts, irrigation) create lasting bonds
- Strengthens inter-species trust and technical knowledge exchange

**The Scholar's Plague (~680 HB equivalent):**
- Disease kills thousands of scholars, clerics, record-keepers across trade routes
- **Hidden truth:** Wizard eliminates researchers investigating ancient necromantic texts
- Blamed on natural disease; no one connects the pattern
- Allows Wizard to "discover" lost knowledge others might have found first

**Real-World Parallel:** Islamic Golden Age + Tang Dynasty innovations + Sogdian merchant networks; Byzantine-Sassanid cultural exchange

## Era 3: The Consolidation (~700-1000 HB equivalent)
**"The Age of Pacts and Suspicions"** (300 years)

### Political Maturation

**Regional Powers Solidify:**
- Loose alliances formalize into recognizable kingdoms/confederations
- Borders stabilize (though disputed); treaty networks develop
- Dynastic continuity emerges (generational rulers)
- Professional bureaucracies replace ad-hoc governance

**Major Power Blocs (~800 HB equivalent):**

**Northern Dwarven League:**
- Formalized alliance of seven mountain kingdoms
- Shared defense, trade coordination, mutual legal recognition
- Chronicle of Ages becomes authoritative historical record
- Banking system enables long-distance credit

**Southern Orc Confederacy:**
- Major trading bloc comprising 12+ tribes/settlements
- Internal tensions persist (warrior vs. merchant factions)
- Oral epic tradition standardizes (canonical hero-songs)
- Emerging written language adapted from human/dwarf scripts

**Eastern Human Dynasties:**
- Three major kingdoms (successor states to original empire)
- Sophisticated bureaucracies, Confucian-style examination systems
- Cultural nostalgia for "imperial golden age" (revisionist history)
- Patronize arts, philosophy, magical research

**Goblin Free Cities:**
- 20+ independent city-states controlling strategic trade chokepoints
- No unified government; each city fiercely autonomous
- "Goblin Code": Shared commercial law without political alliance
- Mercenary companies hired across continent

**Elf Sanctuaries:**
- Fully isolated; exact locations known only to elves
- Maintain trade outposts at sanctuary borders (non-elves not admitted)
- Individual elves serve as ambassadors (rare, mysterious)
- Rumored to possess magical knowledge lost to other cultures

**Halfling/Gnome Networks:**
- Still no unified state; individuals everywhere
- Halfling League (loose commercial association) facilitates trade
- Gnome Guilds (technical societies) share engineering knowledge
- Trusted as neutral parties in negotiations

### Rising Tensions

**Resource Competition:**
- Water rights disputes intensify (irrigation, river access)
- Mountain pass tolls create friction (Dwarf vs. Goblin control)
- Trade monopoly conflicts (who controls what routes)
- Fishing grounds, grazing lands, timber forests contested

**Religious/Cultural Divisions:**
- Theological differences deepen (not violent but politically divisive)
- Cultural chauvinism emerges (species-based prejudices)
- "Golden age" nostalgia fuels territorial claims
- Missionary movements create tension

**Border Skirmishes:**
- Constant low-level conflict (not wars, but raiding, sabotage, assassination)
- Mercenary companies profit; become destabilizing force
- Some conflicts proxy wars between larger powers
- Refugee populations complicate diplomacy

**Economic Stratification:**
- Merchant elite vs. laboring classes
- Urban vs. rural tensions
- "Old money" nobility vs. "new money" merchants
- Species-based economic castes in some regions

### The Wizard's Preparation (Hidden)

**Completing the Lich Cadre (~700-900 CE):**
- All recruited mages undergo Lichdom transformation
- Each positioned in remote, defensible location:
  - Ancient battlefield crypts
  - Mountain fortresses
  - Desert ruins
  - Subterranean complexes
  - Isolated islands
- Each begins raising undead legion independently
- **None know about the others; each believes they're pursuing individual immortality project**

**Building Undead Legions (~800-1000 CE):**
- Stockpile corpses from:
  - Border skirmishes and mercenary conflicts
  - Plague victims and natural disasters
  - Forgotten battlefields from earlier eras
  - Executed criminals and unclaimed dead
- Develop standardized reanimation techniques
- Create command-and-control hierarchies (lesser undead → wights → Lich commanders)
- Total forces: **Tens of thousands** across all locations

**Multi-Persona Manipulation:**
- Operates as "advisor" to several kingdoms simultaneously (different identities):
  - Court mage to Eastern Dynasty
  - Merchant prince in Goblin Free City
  - Scholarly recluse in Dwarf archives
  - Wandering philosopher among humans
- Encourages border conflicts (creates corpses, distracts authorities)
- Steers policy toward instability (subtle, never obvious)
- Builds network of unwitting collaborators

**Infiltrating Institutions:**
- Plants agents in bureaucracies, merchant guilds, religious orders
- Funds archaeological expeditions seeking ancient magical knowledge
- Establishes "benevolent" organizations that serve hidden purposes
- Creates dependencies (kingdoms rely on his "advice")

### Elf Watchers (The Few Who Notice)

**Suspicious Elders:**
- A handful of 300-400 year old mortal elves notice patterns
- Same "advisor" appearing in different courts across centuries
- Unusual longevity for human/dwarf personas
- Report to Elf councils; dismissed as paranoia

**The Debate:**
- Some elves advocate intervention; majority refuse
- "Mortal affairs are not our concern" prevails
- A few individual elves begin covert investigation (isolated, unsupported)
- **These elves may become crucial allies when heroes return**

**High Elf Silence:**
- Those who might know remain withdrawn
- Rare High Elf appearances provide cryptic warnings
- Warnings ignored or misinterpreted
- High Elves do not break their isolationist stance

### Key Events

**The Siege of Kalar's Gate (~780 HB equivalent):**
- Three-year siege ends in negotiated settlement
- Establishes "laws of warfare" (treatment of prisoners, non-combatants)
- Creates precedent for limiting conflicts
- Wizard uses siege as cover to collect thousands of corpses

**The Copper Accord (~850 HB equivalent):**
- Major trade treaty between Dwarves, Goblins, and Orc Confederacy
- Standardizes currency, weights, measures across species
- Reduces trade friction; enables economic growth
- Creates institutions Wizard later infiltrates

**The Vanishing of Khorashar (~950 HB equivalent):**
- Entire city (population ~8,000) disappears overnight
- Buildings intact but all inhabitants gone
- Blamed on magical disaster, planar rift, curse
- **Hidden truth:** Wizard's first test of coordinated Lich-Legion assault
- Entire population killed and reanimated; city becomes secret undead staging ground
- Becomes cautionary tale; no one investigates thoroughly

**Real-World Parallel:** Abbasid fragmentation + Song Dynasty + Turkic migrations + Early Crusades tensions; Byzantine-Sassanid rivalry

## Era 4: The Gathering Storm (~1000-1200 HB equivalent)
**"The Age of Whispers and Warnings"** (200 years)

### Surface Prosperity

**Economic Golden Age:**
- Trade at all-time high; cities wealthy and cosmopolitan
- Long-distance commerce routine; safe routes abundant
- Banking systems sophisticated; credit widely available
- Multiple currencies accepted; exchange rates stable

**Cultural Flourishing:**
- Art, science, philosophy peak across multiple cultures
- Universities, libraries, academies in major cities
- Magical research advances (theoretical and applied)
- Engineering marvels (bridges, aqueducts, fortifications)

**Multi-Species Governance:**
- Mixed-species city councils common
- Inter-marriage normalized in urban centers
- Cosmopolitan elite class transcends species boundaries
- Legal systems recognize multi-species citizenship

**The "Long Peace" Narrative:**
- Generations without major wars
- Border disputes settled through negotiation
- Prosperity creates optimism ("golden age")
- **Feels stable, secure, permanent**

### Undercurrents of Dread

**Mysterious Disappearances:**
- Remote villages vanish (population gone, buildings empty)
- Travelers disappear on well-traveled routes
- Small military patrols fail to return
- Pattern dismissed as banditry, natural disasters, unrelated incidents

**Population Anomalies:**
- **Dwarf census records note discrepancies:** Expected population growth doesn't match reality
- Tax records show missing laborers, farmers, minor officials
- Attributed to migration, record-keeping errors, plague
- **Hidden truth:** Wizard harvesting people systematically**

**Strange Sightings:**
- Witnesses report "walking dead" in remote areas
- Dismissed as superstition, drunken tales, madness
- A few investigators disappear when they pursue leads
- Stories suppressed by authorities (don't want panic)

**Elf Warnings Intensified:**
- Elf ambassadors deliver urgent warnings: "Something ancient stirs"
- Warnings vague, mystical, easily dismissed
- Younger generations ignore; older generations suspicious but inactive
- "Elves always speak in riddles" becomes convenient dismissal

**Dwarf Archivists Troubled:**
- Notice patterns in disappearances (geographic, temporal)
- Cross-reference with ancient records; find disturbing parallels
- Publish scholarly papers; ignored by policymakers
- A few Dwarf investigators go missing

### The Wizard's Final Positioning

**All Lich Commanders Active:**
- 8-12 Liches each command hidden undead legion
- Positioned across continent in strategic locations:
  - Northern mountains (blocks Dwarf reinforcements)
  - Southern deserts (flanks Orc territories)
  - Eastern ruins (near human dynasties)
  - Western coasts (prevents sea escape)
  - Central wastelands (reserve forces)
- Each legion numbers 5,000-15,000 undead
- Total forces: **~100,000+ undead warriors**

**Command and Control Infrastructure:**
- Wizard establishes magical communication network (scrying, sending stones)
- Liches receive sealed orders (to be opened on signal)
- Undead forces drilled in coordinated maneuvers
- Supply caches hidden (weapons, armor, siege equipment)

**Triggering the Gaes (~1180-1200 HB equivalent):**
- Wizard finally locates **threshold to Hero Heaven** via:
  - Ancient texts recovered from ruins
  - Interrogation of captured celestial beings
  - Decades of magical research and divination
- Realizes he needs **Heroes' knowledge/essence** to breach threshold
- Discovers the **fallen teammate's lingering connection** to Hero Heaven
- Enacts **gaes** through necromantic manipulation of that connection

**The Gaes Mechanism:**
- Uses fallen teammate's sacrifice as spiritual anchor
- Twists their noble death into binding compulsion
- Creates paradox: Heroes earned reward but charge incomplete
- Forces expulsion from Hero Heaven back to mortal world
- Heroes experience as betrayal, confusion, devastating doubt

### Key Events

**The Night of Falling Stars (~1050 HB equivalent):**
- Meteor shower interpreted as omen
- Multiple religions claim prophetic significance
- Wizard uses distraction to move major undead forces
- No one notices logistical movements during celestial event

**The Grey Council (~1120 HB equivalent):**
- Emergency meeting of species leaders after disappearances escalate
- Delegates debate action; no consensus reached
- Goblin cities demand investigation; Dwarf League supports
- Human dynasties dismiss as local problems
- Council disbands without resolution
- **Wizard's agents sabotage unity efforts**

**The Scholar's Purge (~1175 HB equivalent):**
- Wave of assassinations targeting researchers of necromancy/ancient magic
- Blamed on religious extremists, political rivalries
- **Hidden truth:** Wizard eliminates those close to discovering his plan
- Key libraries burned; irreplaceable texts lost
- Removes potential counters to his scheme

**The Expulsion (~1190-1200 HB equivalent):**
- **Heroes expelled from Hero Heaven via the gaes**
- Arrive confused, disoriented, self-doubting
- World is utterly transformed (1,000 years changed)
- No human living memory; only legends remain

**Real-World Parallel:** Late medieval prosperity before Mongol invasions; "calm before the storm"; Renaissance humanism emerging

## Era 5: Campaign Present (~1200-1250 HB equivalent)
**"The Age of Reckoning"** (Campaign begins)

### When the Heroes Return

**Immediate Context:**
- World appears prosperous, stable, at peace
- No obvious crisis (Lich-Legion threat not yet revealed)
- Heroes arrive disoriented, expelled, self-doubting
- Must discover: Why expelled? What is the threat? What must they do?

**Memory & Legend:**

**Humans:**
- **No living memory**; heroes are pure myth/legend
- Stories contradict; multiple versions of hero-tales
- Some cultures revere them; others dismiss as fiction
- Scholars debate their historical reality

**Dwarves (~250 year lifespan):**
- **No personal memory**; oldest Dwarves (~230 years) weren't born yet
- Extensive written records in Chronicle of Ages (detailed, accurate)
- Treat heroes as historical figures (academic interest, not reverence)
- Want verification before accepting claims

**Mortal Elves (~350-400 year lifespan):**
- **Handful of 300-350 year old elders** remember heroes' era as children/young adults
- Recall fragmented childhood memories (emotional, not detailed)
- Can verify general history but not specifics
- **Mixed reactions:** Hope, fear, suspicion ("Why were you expelled?")

**High Elves (potentially limitless):**
- **Maybe one or two** knew heroes personally
- Nearly "diminished" from mortal world
- Rare, cryptic appearances (if any)
- Might offer guidance or withhold it

**Orcs (~100 year lifespan):**
- **No living memory**; heroes are **central mythology**
- Liberation epic taught to all Orc children
- Heroes' names spoken before ancestors' names
- **Powerful cultural reverence but also pressure**
- Some Orcs view return as sacred; others as omen of crisis

**Goblins/Halflings/Gnomes (~100-350 years):**
- Pragmatic response: "Prove you're who you say; then we'll talk"
- Stories exist but treated as folklore
- Want to see heroic deeds before believing
- Opportunity for profit/alliance if heroes are real

**Power Balance Overview:**
- **No single hegemon:** Multiple power centers compete and cooperate

### The Lich-Legion Threat (Hidden at Campaign Start)

**Wizard's Position:**
- All pieces in place; awaiting optimal moment
- Heroes' return is unexpected variable (must adapt)
- Liches ready to activate on signal
- Undead legions numbering ~100,000+ hidden across continent

**Campaign Progression (anticipated):**

**Early Campaign:**
- Heroes investigate why they were expelled
- Discover gaes came from fallen teammate
- Begin to suspect Wizard survived

**Mid Campaign:**
- **First Lich-Legion attacks begin** (probing, testing defenses)
- Heroes realize scale of threat
- Must convince factions to unite (difficult; no one believes at first)

**Late Campaign:**
- **Full coordinated assault** across multiple fronts
- Lich commanders revealed
- Heroes face choice: Finish what they started or flee again

**Endgame:**
- Confront Wizard at threshold to Hero Heaven
- Final reckoning with self-doubt and incomplete heroism
- Potential outcomes TBD (multiple endings possible)

### Technology & Culture Snapshot

**Technology Level (~1200 HB equivalent):**
- Advanced metallurgy (Damascus steel equivalent)
- Sophisticated irrigation, agriculture
- Paper, printing (woodblock), advanced mathematics
- Gunpowder exists but **very rare** (experimental, unstable)
- Compass, astrolabe, advanced navigation
- Castle/fortification architecture at peak
- No cannons, no printing press (western equivalent), no firearms as standard weapons

**Magical Integration:**
- Magic commonplace but specialized (requires training/talent)
- Magical items rare, expensive (crafted by masters)
- Healing magic available in cities (temples, academies)
- Enchantments used in infrastructure (wards, lights, preservation)
- Necromancy **forbidden** in most cultures (Wizard's work is secret heresy)

**Cultural Character:**
- Cosmopolitan urban centers vs. traditional rural areas
- Multiple languages; trade lingua franca developing
- Religious diversity; syncretism common
- Art, music, philosophy flourishing
- Literacy rates vary (high among elites, lower among laborers)

**Real-World Parallel:** High Medieval period before gunpowder revolution; Song Dynasty innovation; Islamic Golden Age remnants; pre-Mongol Central Asia

## Key Divergence Points: Fantasy vs. Real History

This section maps where Tarim-Shaiel's timeline **deliberately diverges** from real Silk Road history and why.

| Real Silk Road History | Tarim-Shaiel Fantasy Divergence | Narrative Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Mongols unite steppe tribes → rapid conquest** | **Lich-Legions emerge from hidden places → existential undead threat** | Replaces political conquest with supernatural horror; emphasizes Wizard's patience vs. Mongol speed |
| **Islamic expansion spreads dominant religion** | **Multiple belief systems coexist without theological hegemon** | Prevents religious war; allows diverse spiritual landscapes; fantasy races have different spiritual needs |
| **Sogdian merchants monopolize long-distance trade** | **Multi-species merchant leagues (Halflings/Gnomes/Goblins) share power** | No single species dominates; cooperation required; prevents ethnic essentialism |
| **Nomadic warriors transition to administrators** | **Freed Orcs transition via external mediation (Dwarves/Halflings)** | Highlights trauma of enslavement; Orcs lacked cultural memory; required help to rebuild |
| **Great Wall of China (single defensive line)** | **Dwarf mountain fortifications + caravanserai networks** | Distributed defense; no monolithic structure; reflects decentralized power |
| **Paper/printing spreads gradually** | **Paper common; printing rare (woodblock only)** | Preserves medieval information scarcity; prevents information revolution too early |
| **Gunpowder weaponizes (cannons, firearms)** | **Gunpowder experimental only (fireworks, alchemy)** | Avoids gunpowder revolution; preserves sword/bow/magic combat; keeps medieval warfare |
| **Buddhism spreads via missionaries** | **No single missionary religion dominates** | Allows polytheistic/animistic traditions; fantasy races resist conversion |
| **Dynastic continuity (Han, Tang, etc.)** | **Human kingdoms fragment; no long dynasties** | Reflects empire's collapse; prevents human hegemony; shorter generational memory |
| **Trade declines in 15th century (maritime routes)** | **Trade at peak in campaign present** | World is thriving (not declining); raises stakes for Lich-Legion threat |

### Design Principles to Maintain

**Grounded in Reality:**
- Every fantasy element should have real-world Silk Road parallel or justification
- Geography, trade routes, resources based on actual Central Asian models
- Cultural complexity mirrors real multi-ethnic Silk Road societies

**Multiple Species, No Species Essentialism:**
- Avoid "all Orcs are X" or "Dwarves always Y"
- Each species has internal diversity (cultural, political, individual)
- Mixed-species communities are norm in cosmopolitan areas

**Justified Self-Doubt:**
- Heroes' uncertainty must be **reasonable** given evidence
- 1,000 years creates genuine ambiguity about consequences
- World has moved on; their relevance is questionable

**The Wizard is Patient, Not Omniscient:**
- 1,000-year plan required time, not perfection
- He makes mistakes; heroes' return is unexpected variable
- He adapts rather than controls everything

**Consequences are Cascading, Not Direct:**
- Heroes aren't responsible for 1,000 years of history
- They're responsible for leaving Wizard alive
- Orc chaos was immediate; current world is natural evolution

**Long-Lived Races as Witnesses:**
- Elves/Dwarves provide continuity without human timescales
- Orcs preserve cultural memory through oral tradition
- Each species experiences history differently
