# Peoples & Polities: The Silk Road Network (Present Day)

## Overview

The world operates as a **network of competing/cooperating nation-states and city-states**, with no single hegemon. Trade dependency creates mutual interests despite cultural/religious differences. This section maps the major power centers and peoples that inhabit the world.

---

## The Six Primary Peoples (by race/culture)

### Humans
- **Distribution:** River valleys, oasis cities, coastal settlements
- **Political structure:** Mostly city-states and regional confederations
- **Economic role:** Farmers, administrators, soldiers, merchants
- **Notes:** The "default" political units; no unified human empire post-collapse

### Orcs
- **Distribution:** Steppe regions and semi-nomadic trade networks
- **Political structure:** Tribal confederations with rotating leadership councils
- **Economic role:** Traders, warriors-turned-guards, caravan masters, bridge-builders between peoples
- **Notes:** Recently stabilized (last 50-100 years). Still harbor resentment about the chaos period. Valuable allies but not fully trusted.

### Elves
- **Distribution:** Forest regions, river valleys with dense vegetation (Malabar-like territories)
- **Political structure:** Semi-isolationist enclaves; rare confederations
- **Economic role:** Producers of luxury goods (spices, rare dyes, botanical medicines, silk variants)
- **Notes:** Politically weak but economically leverage massive. Historically meddled in human affairs; now withdrawn. "Elven isolation" is partially choice, partially geography.

### Dwarves
- **Distribution:** Mountain passes and underground networks in Hindu Kush/Pamir analogue regions
- **Political structure:** Independent city-states controlling choke points
- **Economic role:** Toll-takers, engineers, miners of rare metals, craftspeople
- **Notes:** Strategic gatekeepers. Not isolated; they're *necessary*. Old grudges with some polities from pre-stabilization period.

### Goblins
- **Distribution:** Urban diaspora across oasis cities; no territorial base
- **Political structure:** Family/clan networks; merchant guilds
- **Economic role:** Information brokers, money-lenders, urban traders, culture-straddlers
- **Notes:** Operate in the *in-between* spaces. Connected to multiple powers simultaneously.

### Halflings/Gnomes
- **Distribution:** Diaspora across multiple cities (merchant families)
- **Political structure:** Network-based; loyalty to family/guild rather than territory
- **Economic role:** Long-distance merchants, traders of rare goods, information networks
- **Notes:** Essential to the trade network but never fully trusted. Analogous to historical Armenian/Jewish diaspora merchants.

## Historical Tensions (Post-Stabilization)

- [ ] **Orc resentment:** Some Orcs remember the chaos period; some blame the "liberation heroes" for unleashing it

- [ ] **Dwarven aloofness:** Other powers resent Dwarven toll-taking; Dwarves resent dependency
- [ ] **Religious friction:** Multiple faiths coexist; periodic conflicts over sacred sites, trade practices
- [ ] **Memory of empire:** Some older polities long for the stability of unified rule; others fear a return to tyranny
