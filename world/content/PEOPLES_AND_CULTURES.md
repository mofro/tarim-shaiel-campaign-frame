### 2. Species to Detail

**Long-Lived Species:**
- **High Elves:** Potentially limitless (Tolkien-style diminishing)
- **Mortal Elves:** 350-400 years (Wood, Earth, Sea variants)
- **Dwarves:** ~250 years (record-keeper culture)

**Medium-Lived Species:**
- **Firbolg, Fungril, Katari, Infernis:** ~150-350 years

**Human-Lifespan Species:**
- **Humans, Orcs, Halflings, Gnomes, Goblins:** ~60-100 years

**Short-Lived Species:**
- **Fairies, Fauns, others:** ~50-75 years

**Cultural Exchange & Integration:**

- Shared institutions and multi-species governance
- Species-based prejudices and stereotypes (realistic, not essentialist)

**Memory & Oral Tradition:**
- How different lifespans affect historical memory
- Oral vs. written tradition preferences
- How heroes are remembered across species
- Generational transmission of knowledge

**Political Relationships:**
- Alliance patterns and traditional rivalries
- Economic interdependencies
- Shared vs. competing resources
- How species-identity intersects with regional identity

### 4. Species-Specific Responses to Heroes' Return

**High Elves:**
- Maybe 1-2 individuals knew heroes personally
- Cryptic, withdrawn, may offer guidance or silence

**Mortal Elves:**
- Handful of 300+ year elders remember heroes' era as children
- Mix of hope, caution, and suspicion

**Dwarves:**
- No living memory but extensive written records
- Academic interest; demand verification

**Orcs:**
- No living memory but central mythology
- Heroes are core of liberation epic
- Split between reverence and suspicion

**Humans:**
- Pure legend; scholarly debate about historical reality
- Multiple contradictory versions of hero-tales

**Others:**
- Pragmatic responses (Goblins, Halflings, Gnomes)
- Cultural variations based on regional traditions

## Development Notes

**Design Principles:**
- **Avoid species essentialism:** Internal diversity within each species
- **Justify lifespans:** How does longevity affect culture, politics, memory?
- **Grounded in reality:** Base cultural traits on real-world analogs where appropriate
- **Show evolution:** How 1,000 years changed each species' role and self-conception

**Key Questions to Answer:**
- How do long-lived species view short-lived ones (and vice versa)?
- What happens to inter-species children (longevity, culture, identity)?
- How does species memory affect political legitimacy?
- Which species are most/least integrated in cosmopolitan areas?
- How do species-based guilds/organizations function?
