# Historical Frame: The Silk Road World

## Overview

The Tarim-Shaiel world is grounded in real-world Silk Road geography (13th-15th century) with a critical divergence point: an empire that unified the routes through military conquest and ruled for approximately 50-100 years, then collapsed. This collapse mirrors the Mongol Empire's rise and fragmentation into regional khanates, but with a fantasy twist: the collapse was partially engineered by a wizard who survived the heroes' legendary victory and has spent 150+ years planning to access Tarim-Shaiel's power.

---

## Real-World Historical Anchor: The Mongol Empire

**Historical Period:** 13th-14th centuries
- **Rise:** Temüjin unites steppe tribes, becomes Genghis Khan (early 1200s)
- **Expansion:** Rapid conquest across Asia (1206-1368)
- **Pax Mongolica:** Unified trade routes, relative stability (13th-14th centuries)
- **Fragmentation:** Splits into competing khanates (Golden Horde, Chagatai, Ilkhanate, Yuan Dynasty)
- **Collapse:** Regional powers become independent; trade continues as negotiated exchange, not enforced tribute

**Why this matters:** The Mongol model demonstrates that nomadic warriors CAN become administrators and traders, that empires can fragment without disappearing, and that post-imperial politics can be MORE complex and interesting than monolithic rule.

## Fantasy Parallel: The Hero Era & Aftermath

### Timeline Skeleton (13th-15th Century Equivalents)

**[HERO ERA] ~1250s-1280s: The Legendary Victory**
- A human-led empire (name TBD) expands aggressively across the Silk Road
- Empire relies on enslaved/conscripted Orc tribes as military backbone
- A wizard (name TBD) serves the empire covertly, maintaining magical control systems (primarily binding spells on Orcs)
- **The Heroes arise:** A legendary party performs the heroic deeds that will become myth
  - They liberate enslaved Orcs by breaking binding spells
  - They defeat the empire's leadership/military
  - A team member (name TBD, charismatic and upbeat) dies in the final push—a consequential sacrifice
  - The team member's death becomes the rallying cry that secures victory
- **The Wizard's gambit:** Survives the heroes' assault; realizes he was underestimated; retreats to plan
- **The Empire's collapse:** Falls into fragmentation; regional powers begin negotiating

**[POST-HERO CHAOS] ~1280s-1350s: The Orc Bloodshed Period (~70 years)**
- Freed Orcs, suddenly autonomous after generations of subjugation, erupt into violence
- Decades of raids, territorial conflicts, and uncontrolled warfare
- Significant casualties on all sides; trade routes become dangerous
- Gradually, other polities (Dwarves, some human city-states, merchant leagues) band together to impose *diplomatic* constraints
  - Establish trade protocols
  - Offer economic incentives for stability over raiding
  - Provide mediators/sponsors for Orc confederations to find grounding
- **Wizard's influence (behind the scenes):** Uses chaos to consolidate power, gain allies, plan his assault on Hero Heaven

**[STABILIZATION] ~1350s-1400s: The Merchant Era (~50-100 years)**
- Orc confederations transition from raiders to traders (hard-won, fragile)
- Regional powers (Dwarven kingdoms, Elf enclaves, human city-states, Orc confederations, merchant diasporas) establish mutual dependency
- Trade flows normalize; cultural/religious tensions persist but are managed through negotiation
- Multiple power centers; no single hegemon

**[PRESENT DAY] ~1450s: The Campaign Begins**
- The world is ~150-200 years post-heroes
- Current Orc integration is still relatively recent (within living memory for long-lived races)
- The Wizard has finally positioned himself to threaten Hero Heaven
- The Heroes are expelled via gaes from the fallen teammate
- They begin investigating: Why? What happened? What must they do?

## Notes for Lore Keeper

This skeleton deliberately leaves blanks for you to fill in as the world develops. The structure is: **Empire → Collapse → Chaos → Stabilization → Present**. This mirrors real-world Mongol history but allows for fantasy-specific details to emerge organically.

Key constraint: Everything must feel *grounded* in the Silk Road's actual geography and culture, even where we diverge into fantasy.
