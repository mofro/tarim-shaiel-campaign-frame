## GEOGRAPHIC FEATURES & INFRASTRUCTURE

### Mountain Passes & Barriers
- **Tian Shan Mountains** - North boundary of Tarim Basin
- **Kunlun Mountains** - South boundary of Tarim Basin
- **Taklamakan Desert** - Central to Tarim Basin; major navigation hazard
- **Gobi Desert** - North of routes; nomadic zone
- **Himalayas** - Southern barrier
- **Pamir Mountains** - Tajikistan region; Castle Karon/Kala-i Kukhna (38.45, 70.85) - "Machu Picchu of Tajikistan"

### Major Regions
- **Tarim Basin** - Heart of Silk Road; surrounded by mountains
- **Sogdania** - Persian-influenced; primary merchant network (Sogdian merchants dominated 4th-8th centuries)
- **Fergana Valley** - Oasis network; secondary hub
- **Gansu Corridor** - Connection from Tarim to Chang'an
- **Steppe** - North; nomadic peoples; horse source

## TRADE PATTERNS & FLOW DYNAMICS

### Primary East-West Goods Exchange
**Eastbound (to China):**
- Horses (critical for military)
- Jade (Khotan specialty)
- Precious metals (Persian, Central Asian)
- Textiles & carpets
- Spices & incense

**Westbound (from China):**
- Silk (luxury good + imperial currency for paying officials/soldiers)
- Paper (8th c. onward; major technology)
- Porcelain
- Tea & sugar

**Central Asian Exports:**
- Porcelain, jade, bronze
- Wool, carpets, rugs
- Luxury textiles

**Central Asian Imports:**
- Jewels, ivory
- Spices
- Glass & luxury goods
- Metals

### Trade Organization
- **Sogdian merchant networks** - Dominant long-distance traders; established trading colonies in major cities
- **Caravan logistics** - Small groups of local merchants traveled to next oasis; chain of exchanges
- **Caravanserai** - Safe lodging stations; credit provision
- **Commercial innovation** - Bills of exchange, banking houses, paper money

## NOTES FOR FANTASY ADAPTATION

- **Mongol Destruction Events**: Many cities were destroyed then rebuilt (Bukhara, Samarkand, Merv). This creates narrative opportunities for "ruined glory" or "phoenix rising" themes.
- **Sogdian Merchant Networks**: These operated independently of imperial control—useful for neutral/merchant-republic factions in Tarim-Shaiel.
- **Religious Coexistence**: Cities hosted Buddhist, Christian, Islamic, and Zoroastrian communities simultaneously—valuable for multicultural settlement design.
- **Oasis Vulnerability**: All major cities depend on water sources in deserts/basins. Seasonal/water control could create political leverage points.
- **Strategic Choke Points**: Gansu Corridor, mountain passes, and desert waypoints create natural conflict zones or tribute collection points.
- **Commerce Over State**: Long-distance trade functioned more through merchant networks than imperial directives—useful for emergent, decentralized power structures.
