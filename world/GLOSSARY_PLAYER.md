# Player Glossary — Tarim-Shaiel

**Player-facing reference.** Defines terms as a player character in the world of Tarim-Shaiel would encounter or use them. No cosmological architecture, no GM-layer truths.

For GM-layer and mechanical vocabulary, see `mechanics/GLOSSARY_GM.md`.

---

## The Chain-Breakers Order
A multi-species community anchored in Orcish tradition, dispersed across the continent. They hold oral histories, practical lore about gaes-completion, and a deep conviction that certain unfinished business from a thousand years ago still requires resolution. They have been keeping faith with something for a very long time.

---

## Dead Zones
Geographic areas where magic fails, preservation fails, or — in the worst cases — where cause and effect seem to operate by unfamiliar rules. Trade networks have been mapping them for decades. Their distribution is not random.

---

## The Liberation
The act performed by the heroes — now legendary across every culture on the continent — roughly a thousand years ago. In every tradition that remembers it, the liberation is understood as correct. The questions that remain are not about whether it was right, but about what it left unfinished.

---

## The Scholar's Purge
*(Player-accessible name — full scope is GM-layer knowledge)*
A period, beginning roughly a thousand years ago, during which scholars with specific areas of knowledge began disappearing at unusual rates. Dwarven archives record the anomaly empirically. The Scholar's Remnant are the survivors and their descendants.

---

## The Scholar's Remnant
Scattered communities of scholars who preserve fragments of knowledge from before a systematic purge. They are cautious, well-hidden, and acutely aware that what they carry has made them targets.

---

## The Silence
*(Orcish term — also used colloquially in Steppe communities)*
The degradation of ancestor communication that Orcish shamans have been experiencing for centuries. Specific spirits become unreachable; certain ritual sites go quiet. Experienced as a profound wrongness — the dead withdrawing from the living without explanation.

---

## Tarim-Shaiel
The world. Named in the old tongue; the meaning is debated by scholars. The continent spans the known Silk Road corridors, the Dwarven mountain confederations, the Orcish Steppe, and the Elven highland enclaves.

---

## Tools *(hero artifacts)*
The objects carried by legendary heroes — not weapons or instruments in the ordinary sense, but artifacts that seem to carry memory, personality, and occasionally resistance. In legend, the heroes' tools are described as extensions of their will. Those who have encountered heroes directly report something stranger: the tools appear to have their own.
