# Player Glossary — Tarim-Shaiel

**Player-facing reference.** Defines terms as a player character in the world of Tarim-Shaiel would encounter or use them. No cosmological architecture, no GM-layer truths.

For GM-layer and mechanical vocabulary, see `mechanics/GLOSSARY_GM.md`.

---

## The Chain-Breakers Order
A multi-species community anchored in Orcish tradition, dispersed across the continent. They hold oral histories, practical lore about gaes-completion, and a deep conviction that certain unfinished business from a thousand years ago still requires resolution. They have been keeping faith with something for a very long time.

---

## Dead Zones
Geographic areas where magic fails, preservation fails, or — in the worst cases — where cause and effect seem to operate by unfamiliar rules. Trade networks have been mapping them for decades. Their distribution is not random.

---

## The Liberation
The act performed by the heroes — now legendary across every culture on the continent — roughly a thousand years ago. In every tradition that remembers it, the liberation is understood as correct. The questions that remain are not about whether it was right, but about what it left unfinished.

---

## The Scholar's Purge
*(Player-accessible name — full scope is GM-layer knowledge)*
A period, beginning roughly a thousand years ago, during which scholars with specific areas of knowledge began disappearing at unusual rates. Dwarven archives record the anomaly empirically. The Scholar's Remnant are the survivors and their descendants.

---

## The Scholar's Remnant
Scattered communities of scholars who preserve fragments of knowledge from before a systematic purge. They are cautious, well-hidden, and acutely aware that what they carry has made them targets.

---

## The Silence
*(Orcish term — also used colloquially in Steppe communities)*
The degradation of ancestor communication that Orcish shamans have been experiencing for centuries. Specific spirits become unreachable; certain ritual sites go quiet. Experienced as a profound wrongness — the dead withdrawing from the living without explanation.

---

## Tarim-Shaiel
The world. Named in the old tongue; the meaning is debated by scholars. The continent spans the known Silk Road corridors, the Dwarven mountain confederations, the Orcish Steppe, and the Elven highland enclaves.

---

## Tools *(hero artifacts)*
The objects carried by legendary heroes — not weapons or instruments in the ordinary sense, but artifacts that seem to carry memory, personality, and occasionally resistance. In legend, the heroes' tools are described as extensions of their will. Those who have encountered heroes directly report something stranger: the tools appear to have their own.

---

## The Ancestors *(Orcish cosmological term)*
The spirits of Orcish dead who have always served as counsel, witnesses, and navigational anchors for the living — accessible through shamanic practice at specific ritual sites. The gradual silencing of the Ancestors (see: *The Silence*) is understood within Orcish culture as the most consequential spiritual event in living memory. Other traditions have analogous concepts under different names; the Orcish term has spread because the shamans who use it have been speaking about the phenomenon the longest.

---

## The Celestial Peak
*(player-accessible name — significance and location are GM-layer)*
A place — or the idea of a place — that appears in the traditions of every major culture on the continent, under different names and with different meanings attached to it. Shamans speak of it as a point where the sky meets something older. Scholars debate whether it is geographic or cosmological. Merchants have heard stories from travellers who claim to have seen it and will not describe it clearly. Every faction with cosmological awareness has some interest in it. Why they want it, and what they would do with it, they do not agree on.

---

## The Dwarven Archivists
A scholarly institution within the Dwarven Mountain Confederations that has maintained meticulous longitudinal records for centuries — trade data, seismic patterns, demographic anomalies, disappearance records. Not a political body; a record-keeping function that has outlasted the institutions it serves. The Archivists are known to hold data that doesn't fit any available framework. Scholars who want to understand anomalous patterns tend to find their way to mountain archives eventually.

---

## The Eastern Imperial Dominion
The great empire at the eastern terminus of the Silk Road — politically dominant in its own sphere, ancient in its institutions, and distantly aware of the western corridor's troubles without understanding their full significance. Trade from the Dominion flows through the Jade Gate. Its relationship to the Elven Highland Enclaves is long-established and not entirely understood by either party's subordinates.

---

## The Free Cities
*(also: Goblin Free Cities)*
City-state markets that operate as neutral ground — places where people who cannot safely meet elsewhere can conduct business. The Free Cities are governed by civic councils and run by Goblin traders and administrators with a deep professional investment in neutrality. They are clearinghouses for news, unusual goods, and unusual customers. If something is happening on the continent, someone in a Free City has noticed.

---

## The Gaes
*(also: the incomplete charge; the unfinished work)*
The obligation carried by the legendary heroes — the structural shape of something they left undone when they were taken to their rest. Not a curse; the heroes were not punished. What they left behind remains unresolved, and the world has been living with the consequences. Most cultures on the continent are aware that something was left unfinished. The Chain-Breakers Order holds the clearest description of what completion would require.

---

## The Gnome Guilds
Technical societies operating across every major trade hub — the engineers, cartographers, and precision-instrument makers of the Silk Road. The Guilds maintain caravanserai networks and the infrastructure that makes long-distance trade reliable. Their charm failure rate data across trade zones is the most geographically granular record of the dead zone problem available. They have not published this as a crisis report; they have treated it as an engineering problem.

---

## Hero Heaven
*(also: the rest the heroes earned; paradise; Svarga in some traditions)*
The cosmological reward that, in every tradition, the heroes of the liberation were understood to have earned — a genuine rest, not merely death. In legend, they were taken there. The questions circulating in scholarly and shamanic communities are not about whether they went, but about whether they stayed, whether they can return, and whether what they left undone matters enough to call them back.

---

## The Human Empire
The empire that existed across the Silk Road corridor roughly a thousand years ago — not a faded golden age in every tradition's telling, but a specific political structure whose collapse the liberation caused. Its successor factions (remnants of its administrative apparatus, its cultural institutions) are still present and still claim various degrees of legitimacy. The Empire's relationship to the Wizard and the Lich Cadre is contested; different factions tell the story differently.

---

## The Jade Gate
The strategic passage between the Western Silk Road and the Eastern Imperial Dominion — administratively controlled by the Eastern Gateway Council, politically contested by every power with ambitions in both directions. The Jade Gate is a physical location and a metaphor: what passes through it, what is held back, and who controls the threshold are questions that have driven wars.

---

## The Lich-Legion
The military force associated with the Wizard — not a conventional army, but something that has been assembling across multiple regions for generations. Its commanders are reported to be undead of significant power. Most people with detailed knowledge of the Lich-Legion's scope do not share it openly; most people with only general knowledge underestimate it. The Lich-Legion's presence in a region tends to be recognized after the fact.

---

## Peoples of the Nine Roads
A diaspora-compact — a community without fixed territory that maintains identity through shared practice, mutual obligation, and a network of private channels across every major trade route. The Nine Roads refer to the original corridors; the Peoples have long since spread beyond them. They are the continent's connective tissue for information that moves between people who cannot meet officially.

---

## Svarga
*(scholarly and shamanic usage; see also: Hero Heaven)*
The term used in Vedic-tradition cosmology for the celestial rest earned by heroes of exceptional merit — a genuine paradise, distinct from afterlife states that carry obligation. Scholars of multiple traditions use it when discussing the heroes' destination after the liberation, though there is disagreement about its nature, location, and whether the heroes' return would represent failure, completion, or something the cosmological framework doesn't have a word for yet.

---

## Vestiges
The artifacts carried by the heroes — the objects that legend describes as extensions of their will and that those who have encountered the heroes directly describe as something more unsettling. In scholarly usage, "vestige" implies something left behind, a remnant of a greater state. In the Chain-Breakers tradition, vestiges are not remnants but ambassadors: the aspect of a hero that was never quite contained in a body, given an object to work through. Both accounts agree on one thing: the vestiges are not inert. They are not waiting. They are doing something, in the direction of something, and whether that aligns with their bearer's intentions is not guaranteed.

---

## The Wizard
The primary figure opposing the heroes' charge — a being of genuine power who has been operating across the continent for centuries under different identities and through institutional proxies. Not a simple conqueror; the Wizard's goal is cosmological in scale rather than territorial. The cultures that have encountered the Wizard's agents disagree sharply about his nature, intentions, and whether his plan — whatever it is — represents salvation or catastrophe. He has been keeping the unfinished work unfinished. Whether that was a choice, a calculation, or something else is the question every faction with knowledge of him is trying to answer.

---

## The Wrongness
*(colloquial; used across multiple cultural contexts)*
The quality of places where the world has stopped working correctly — not simply where magic fails, but where the underlying texture of cause and effect has become unreliable. The Wrongness is what dead zones become when they progress past the stage of mere absence. Trade networks have been noting zones of wrongness for decades; shamans of multiple traditions have been cataloguing them longer. No common framework for understanding them has been established. The distribution is not random.
