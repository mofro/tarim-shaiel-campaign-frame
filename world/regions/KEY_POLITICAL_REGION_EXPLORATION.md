I have mapped the key political regions and empires along the Silk Road as they existed around 1200 AD.

Since precise historical borders from 1200 AD are not available as a standard dataset, I have mapped the **capitals and major power centers** of the empires that dominated the Silk Road during this era.

The map includes the following historical powers:

| Region / Empire | Key Cities Mapped | Historical Context (~1200 AD) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Song Dynasty (Southern)** | **Hangzhou** (Lin'an) | The economic and cultural powerhouse of China, though having lost the north to the Jin. |
| **Jin Dynasty** | **Beijing** (Zhongdu) | Ruled by the Jurchens, controlling northern China and the eastern steppe. |
| **Western Xia (Xi Xia)** | **Yinchuan**, **Dunhuang** | A Tangut empire controlling the vital Hexi Corridor, the gateway to the Silk Road. |
| **Qara Khitai (Western Liao)** | **Tokmok** (Balasagun), **Kashgar** | A Khitan empire in Central Asia that facilitated East-West trade before the Mongol conquest. |
| **Khwarazmian Empire** | **Köneürgench** (Urgench), **Samarkand**, **Merv** | A massive Persianate empire dominating Central Asia and Iran just before the Mongol invasion. |
| **Mongol Empire** | **Kharkhorin** (Karakorum) | The rising power in the north; Genghis Khan (Temüjin) would unite the tribes by 1206. |
| **Ghurid Dynasty** | **Herat** | A Persianate dynasty ruling parts of modern-day Afghanistan, Iran, and Northern India. |
| **Abbasid Caliphate** | **Baghdad** | The spiritual heart of the Islamic world, though its political power had waned compared to earlier centuries. |
| **Sultanate of Rum** | **Konya** | A Seljuk Turkish state in Anatolia, marking the western leg of the overland Silk Road. |
| **Byzantine Empire** | **Istanbul** (Constantinople) | The historic endpoint of the Silk Road in the West, controlling the Bosphorus. |

I also included **Xi'an (Chang'an)**, the traditional eastern terminus of the Silk Road. Note that the "Silk Road" geometry itself was not mapped as a single line, as it was a complex network of changing routes rather than a single road.


To represent the shifting political landscape along the Silk Road from 1100 to 1400 AD, I have created four separate time-period layers.

Since precise historical border polygons are not available, I have mapped the **Capitals and Centers of Power** as proxies for these empires. You can toggle each layer on and off in your Google Earth table of contents to see how power shifted over the centuries.

### **1100 AD: The Pre-Mongol Era**
*   **Kaifeng (Song Dynasty):** The economic engine of East Asia.
*   **Isfahan (Seljuk Empire):** The heart of the Great Seljuk state.
*   **Constantinople (Byzantine Empire):** The western anchor of the trade networks.
*   **Ghazni (Ghaznavids):** Connecting the Persianate world with India.
*   **Yinchuan (Western Xia):** Controlling the vital Hexi Corridor.

### **1200 AD: The Eve of the Mongol Conquests**
*   **Hangzhou (Southern Song):** The new Song capital after the loss of the north.
*   **Zhongdu/Beijing (Jin Dynasty):** The Jurchen power in the north.
*   **Urgench (Khwarazmian Empire):** A massive empire in Central Asia about to face Genghis Khan.
*   **Herat (Ghurid Dynasty):** A major center in Khorasan.
*   **Konya (Sultanate of Rum):** The Seljuk presence in Anatolia.

### **1300 AD: Pax Mongolica**
*   **Khanbaliq/Beijing (Yuan Dynasty):** The capital of the Great Khan (Kublai).
*   **Tabriz (Ilkhanate):** The Mongol capital in Persia.
*   **Sarai Batu (Golden Horde):** The capital on the Volga, linking Russia and the Steppe.
*   **Almaliq (Chagatai Khanate):** The center of the Central Asian Mongol khanate.
*   **Delhi (Delhi Sultanate):** A major power in India holding off the Mongols.
*   **Cairo (Mamluk Sultanate):** The Islamic power that defeated the Mongols at Ain Jalut.

### **1400 AD: The Timurid & Ming Era**
*   **Samarkand (Timurid Empire):** The brilliant capital built by Tamerlane (Timur).
*   **Nanjing (Ming Dynasty):** The restored Han Chinese capital.
*   **Bursa & Edirne (Ottoman Empire):** The rising Turkish power encircling Constantinople.
*   **Herat (Timurid Empire):** The cultural capital under Shah Rukh.
*   **Cairo (Mamluk Sultanate):** Continuing as a major trade hub.
