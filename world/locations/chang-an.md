# Chang'an (Xi'an)

The glorious eastern terminus of the Silk Road. An imperial capital of legendary proportions, surrounded by massive walls. This is where the journey ends and fortunes are made or lost.

## Historical Basis

**Central China** – Major imperial capital during the Han and Tang dynasties. A center of power, culture, and commerce. Completely redesigned and fortified in 1370.

## Key Features

- **Massive Fortifications** – 12-mile perimeter walls; heavily garrisoned
- **Imperial Court** – Seat of imperial power and bureaucracy
- **Cultural Center** – Buddhist temples, Daoist shrines, imperial palaces
- **Merchant District** – Where Silk Road goods are sold and valued

## Factions

- Imperial Government (absolute authority)
- Merchant Guilds (economic power)
- Buddhist Communities (cultural/spiritual influence)
- Daoist Orders (spiritual influence)

## Resources

- Silk (primary imperial monopoly)
- Fine porcelain
- Jade goods
- Imperial authority and legitimacy

## Trade

All Silk Road goods ultimately flow to Chang'an's markets. Merchants who reach here with exotic goods can command premium prices from the imperial court and wealthy nobles.

## Challenges

- Strict imperial regulations and taxation
- Bureaucratic complexity
- Merchant competition
- Political intrigue
