## Session 0 & 1 Opening Campaign Structure (Session 2026-01-08)

### Narrative Architecture: LOCKED

**Session 0 Structure Resolved:**
- **Framework:** Secret god-tier snippets during individual awakenings + convergence + GM-imposed recognition scene
- **Why this works:** Solves player-to-character investment without collapsing dual-truth system; creates emotional cliffhanger without explaining mystery
- **Key Insight:** Mystery works best on two levels — group mysteries (shared) and personal secrets (private)
- **Player-facing opacity:** Pitch states historical facts (heroes emerged 200 years ago, they earned paradise, the world lives with consequences); does NOT hint that players are those heroes
- **Archetype descriptions:** Describe psychological patterns + current role function; NO hints about fallen godhood or past power

**Four-Axis Flashback Trigger System (NEW):**
1. **Solo triggers** — Personal power growth, resonant events, crisis moments
2. **Power progression** — Mechanical thresholds tied to character advancement
3. **Interaction triggers** — Shared memory unlocked when 2+ characters work in concert
4. **Environmental/event triggers** — Returning to significant locations, moral echoes

**Event-Centric Design (Game-Changer):**
- Avoids combinatorial explosion (6 factorial pair-specific flashbacks)
- Pre-scripts 8-12 critical "shared memory events" that work with any character pairing
- Triggers emerge organically from actual party dynamics, not mechanical rules
- Different pairings experiencing same event may emphasize different aspects

**Session 0 Pacing (Verified Executable):**
- Segment 0: Opening framework (10 min)
- Segment 1: Round 1 awakenings — Bridge + Seeker parallel (25 min)
- Segment 2: Round 2 awakenings — Warrior + Sacrificer parallel (25 min)
- Segment 3: Round 3 — Breaker solo/sequential (30-35 min, high-tension)
- Segment 4: Visionary crisis pulling toward convergence (20 min)
- Segment 5: Convergence & caravan boarding (20 min)
- Segment 6: Campfire recognition climax (10 min, GM-imposed narration)
- Segment 7: Closing & notes (5 min)
- **Total: ~145 minutes actual play; 4.5-5 hours with natural pacing**

**Secret Snippet Architecture:**
- Delivered during individual awakenings at moment of archetype's core crisis
- Sensory + emotional register (NOT explanatory)
- Personal (player only; private)
- Earned through crisis (not gift-wrapped)
- Triggers player-to-character investment without revealing cosmic truth

**Hero Heaven Vision Specificity (Hero Heaven Moment):**
- Emotionally specific (players feel the meaning: we were together, we were powerful)
- Visually vague (NO explanation of what they were doing or why they were together)
- Sensory but not explanatory (see/feel architecture of care, hear voices, experience serenity)
- Preserves traitor possibility (same vision interpreted differently by different characters)
- Preserves dual-truth (storyteller knows what Hero Heaven WAS; players only know it MATTERED)

**Decision: HYBRID PARALLEL + SEQUENTIAL PACING**
- Parallel scenarios (Bridge+Seeker, Warrior+Sacrificer) allow all players engaged without long waits
- Breaker sequential (high-tension scenario deserves full table attention)
- Visionary crisis PULLS toward convergence (elegant mechanical solution)
- Natural convergence at caravan within 5-10 minutes

## Player Pitch & Archetype Development (Session 2026-01-08)

### Core Pitch (Less Transparent - LOCKED)
> "You're traveling west on a merchant caravan with little memory of the lives you lived before. Something feels wrong—an absence you can't quite name. Worse: you keep recognizing kinship in the strangers around you. They feel *familiar* in a way that makes no sense. And something, somewhere east, calls to you even as you travel away from it."

**Why this works:**
- States facts without hinting at mystery
- No poeticizing or explanation
- Just: what's happening and how it feels
- Players can read this and speculate without spoilers
- Emotional tone established without revealing content

### Emotional Hook (Full Transparency - For Reference)
> "This is a game about the weight of power and the cost of heroism. About waking up to discover that your greatest deeds echo still, 200 years later. About confronting the doubt that haunts gods—the fear that despite everything you sacrificed, everything you toppled, something essential was still left undone. And about finding kinship with others carrying the same impossible burden."

**Note:** This is the emotional TRUTH of the campaign, but held back from pitch to preserve player discovery.

### Archetype Descriptions (No Past Leakage - LOCKED)

**BREAKER:** "You solve problems through direct action. When something stands in the way, you move it. You see barriers—physical, social, conceptual—and you understand how to dismantle them. Others deliberate; you act. This serves you well. Mostly."

**SEEKER:** "You ask questions others don't think to ask. You notice patterns, connections, details that slip past normal sight. You're driven to understand things. You follow threads to their ends, even when the path is unclear. Especially then."

**BRIDGE:** "You understand people in ways that surprise them. You find common ground where none seemed to exist. You're the one who listens, who connects, who holds things together through presence alone. Others rely on you to make sense of each other."

**SACRIFICER:** "You take care of people. It's not a choice you make—it's what you do. You notice what others need before they do. You give time, energy, presence. You sustain. The cost of that is something you've learned to carry."

**WARRIOR:** "You stand between others and harm. You're steady. Reliable. When danger arrives, you're the one people look to. You're strong, and you know how to use it. You also know how to hold it back when necessary."

**VISIONARY:** "You see what needs to happen and you make it happen. You act when others are still deciding. You trust your instincts, and they usually serve you well. You lead not through authority but through conviction and momentum."

**Design Philosophy:** Describe the person NOW (psychological patterns + role function), not their past. No hints about fallen godhood, power loss, or cosmic mystery. Players discover this through play.

## Shared Memory Events Architecture (Session 2026-01-08 Continued)

### Core Decision: 5 Thematic Events (NOT 8-12)

**Rationale:** Quality over quantity. 5 events with rich narrative imagery beats 12 skeleton outlines. Each event reveals canonical truth(s) asymmetrically through emotional + informational blend.

**Thematic Grouping from 9 Aspirational Pairings:**

**EVENT 1: THE WARRIOR'S CHOICE**
- Thematic core: Method of engagement (restraint/aggression/diplomacy)
- Pairings: Warrior+Bridge, Warrior+Breaker, Seeker+Warrior
- Each pairing emphasizes different aspect of same scene

**EVENT 2: THE QUESTION OF SIGHT**
- Thematic core: What we know vs. what we trust vs. what's actually true
- Pairings: Seeker+Bridge, Visionary+Bridge, Seeker+Visionary
- Bridge questioning certainty across different knowledge types

**EVENT 3: THE WEIGHT OF CHOICE**
- Thematic core: Action vs. inaction; cost vs. purpose alignment
- Pairings: Breaker+Sacrificer (opposed), Breaker+Sacrificer (shared), Visionary+Sacrificer
- Tension between impulse and consequence

**EVENT 4: ASPIRATIONS UNDONE**
- Thematic core: The gap between what they fought for and what emerged; failure vs. unavoidable change
- Pairings: Any characters discovering the unintended consequences of their victory
- Reveals ambiguity about whether they chose right

**EVENT 5: THE WIZARD'S SHADOW**
- Thematic core: What they suspect about the Wizard's goals (and why they're wrong)
- Pairings: Any characters working together to piece together Wizard's suspected intention
- Plants false certainty; preserves mystery

**Implementation Note:** Each event drafted with:
- Abstract trigger (flexible, narrative-based)
- Sensory anchor + specific scene imagery
- Canonical truths revealed (asymmetric)
- Multiple pairing variations with distinct emphasis
- GM delivery notes

**Narrative Imagery Template Created:** `/narrative/Shared_Memory_Narrative_Template.md`
- Ensures consistent sensory richness across all memories
- Provides structure for drafting full scenes (400-600 words each)
- Reusable for additional events later if needed

## Cosmological Architecture — Session 2026-03-08

### Decision 1: The Mythic Ecosystem — LOCKED

**The Mythic Ecosystem is confirmed as a multi-dimensional biome.**

- Warrens are distinct dimensional spaces ("worlds" or "planes") — not merely channels or metaphors for power flow
- Each Warren has its own nature: specific types of power, affinities, and entities occupy, suffuse, and constitute them
- The system functions as a **biome**: consistent ecological rules of interaction, opposition, and balance govern it — not designed, not aware, but rule-governed
- **"Power draws power"** is a core mechanical principle — concentrated power attracts more pressure; it bends the ecosystem toward it
- Affinities are not interchangeable — people, creatures, and things attuned to specific channels cannot simply swap allegiances
- The Threshold is a porous container of the underlying chaotic adjacent structures — a pressure regulator, not merely a door
- **Player-facing design principle:** This architecture is largely invisible to players. They encounter it only through practical effects (dead zones, tool behavior, shamanic degradation, etc.). No exhaustive cosmological exposition required or intended.
- **Attuned creatures (flavor):** Most terrestrial magical creatures (dragons, ghosts, etc.) are naturally attuned to the biome. Their magical properties "just work." This does not require documentation or player-facing explanation — it gives the world internal consistency without generating exhaustive linkage lists.

### Decisions 2 & 3: The Nature of the Cosmic Threat — LOCKED
*(Revises the "geological/tectonic sleeping entity" proposal in STORY_ARC_SYNTHESIS.md)*

**The threat is not a single mindless geological force. It is multiple liminal consciousnesses at celestial scale, held in suspension by cosmological equilibrium — the "Held Breath".**

- These beings are not imprisoned — they are held *back* by the balance of the mythic ecosystem
- They are not actively malevolent — they are not awake enough to have intent in any mortal-recognizable sense
- They are not purely mindless either — they *can* wake, and that changes everything
- **"Held Breath"** is the canonical term: the ecosystem holds these beings at the threshold of manifestation
- **Celestial beings** (god-scale) are intricately tied to the ecosystem's structure — not flavor, foundational
- **The true source of danger:** Human meddling — specifically the Wizard's assault on the Threshold with necromantic force at scale — risks tipping cosmic scales just enough to begin waking these consciousnesses. The horror is their *weight*, not malice or indifference
- The threat is awakening, not containment breach of a mindless flood

**⚠️ STORY_ARC_SYNTHESIS.md update required:** "Sleeping Entity" sections (Part 1) need revision — replace singular geological entity with plural liminal consciousnesses; adopt "Held Breath" as canonical term; reframe threat as awakening rather than flood-through-cracks.

### Decision 5: Heroes' Charms Strain the Ecosystem — LOCKED
*(Includes R/H/K reframe and beacon mechanic)*

**Yes — Charm use strains the ecosystem. Scaled by tier. Expressed as beacon, not immediate cost.**

**Layer 1 — Mechanical cost is sufficient at low tiers.**
Foundation Charm costs (FP/Hope) are game balance. Players need no cosmological explanation at that scale. The story significance is invisible early on — by design.

**Layer 2 — Power as beacon, scaling with tier.**
This is the "power draws power" principle (Decision 1) expressing itself narratively throughout the campaign:
- Foundation tier: barely registers cosmologically
- Specialization tier: visible to attuned observers (Wizard's scrying network, sensitive shamans, High Elf elders)
- Mastery tier / heroes in concert: cosmologically loud — visible to Warren-level entities; potentially registers as disturbance to the Held Breath
- The awakening "race" (Wizard's agents vs. ecosystem immune response) is the first instance of this principle — it runs as escalating pressure through all three acts
- Players experience this as increasing attention, not as a mechanical tax

**Layer 3 — R/H/K reframed as Warren allegiance, not hero loyalty.**
The tools are ambassadors of their respective Warrens. Their R/H/K behaviors reflect Warren interests, which may or may not align with the hero's immediate goals:
- **Resist** — the Warren protecting its investment, or protecting itself from what the hero is about to do with its power. Not the tool being protective of the hero.
- **Hunger** — a Warren (or its denizens) calling for its energy to be used, for reasons the hero may not understand or agree with.
- **Know** — Warren-mediated revelation: what the Warren *wants* the hero to know. May be true, partial, or strategically framed. Not neutral truth.

**Endgame implication:** Tools are the heroes' interface to the cosmological layer — the only mechanism by which mortals can touch the ecosystem to repair or reinforce it. Constructive or destructive depending on choices made throughout the campaign.

**Compatibility confirmed:** All three existing awakening narratives audited (Warrior, Seeker, Breaker). None conflict. CORE_CAMPAIGN_NARRATIVE.md R/H/K ambiguity was already load-bearing — this reframe answers those questions. TOOL_EVOLUTION_FRAMEWORK's player-facing "your power" framing remains valid as experienced truth; Warren-as-source is the cosmological layer beneath it.

**⚠️ TOOL_EVOLUTION_FRAMEWORK.md — minor GM note recommended:** Flag in "Tether to mortal world" section: why does the Warren keep its heroes alive? Not a conflict — a design question to hold for later.

### Decision 6: Liberation's Ecosystem Side Effects — LOCKED
*(Revised from original "ecosystem damage" framing)*

**The liberation was unequivocally right. The framing of "ecosystem channel damage" is dropped.**

What actually happened: the liberation was a *massive beacon event*. By the "power draws power" principle, shattering thousands of binding spells simultaneously rang through the Warrens like a bell. The consequences are not structural damage — they are **attention and disrupted agendas**:

- Warren denizens were displaced or agitated by the severing of thousands of forced connections
- Celestial entities with their own plans noticed — and some of those plans relied on the binding spells remaining intact
- The heroes may have angered a trickster entity, wrecked a schemer's long game, or triggered interest from forces they didn't know were watching
- The incomplete charge isn't "repair the plumbing" — it's "reckon with what you woke, and whose plans you crossed, and what those parties have been doing for 1,000 years while you were in Hero Heaven"

**The player doubt drama survives completely intact.** "Did we cause harm by liberating them?" is still rich, ambiguous, emotionally resonant. The answer is just no longer "you cracked the ecosystem." It's "you drew attention you didn't account for, and some of that attention has been patient."

**⚠️ liberation_aftermath.md update required:** The existing framing of ecosystem damage needs to be replaced with Warren disturbance / interested parties framing. The timeline fix (200 → 1,000 years) remains separately needed.

### Decision 4: Wizard's Awareness — LOCKED 2026-03-17

**The Wizard is Options B+C combined: tragic hubris seeded by cosmic manipulation.**

- **Option B (tragic hubris):** The Wizard *knows* about the ecosystem risks and the Held Breath. He does not act in ignorance — he acts in overconfidence. He believes he is the one person who understands the situation well enough to act on it.
- **Option C (cosmic manipulation):** The entity's 1,000-year subtle influence has shaped his conviction that he *can* manage what he's about to wake. His certainty is not purely his own — it was cultivated.
- **Synthesis:** He is overconfident and unaware of the true risk. He is wrong in the specific way that matters: the very thing he believes he has under control has been grooming his certainty for a millennium.

**Narrative implications:**
- The Wizard is not a villain through ignorance (A) and not a pure puppet (straight C) — he has genuine agency and genuine belief in his own competence
- Players who piece together his plan will find it *almost* coherent — which makes it more unsettling than cartoonish evil
- Event 5 (The Wizard's Shadow) should plant false certainty about his goals; the B+C truth is GM-layer only
- His motivation for breaching the Threshold remains a separate writing task (Distinctions / Inciting Incident)

**Status:** LOCKED. Motivation specifics (why he wants to breach) to be developed in campaign frame writing sessions.

### Decision 7: Threshold as Ecosystem Node — LOCKED

**Hero Heaven is adjacent to the Warren structure — not a Warren itself, but directly wired into the infrastructure.**

- The Threshold is where those connections are most concentrated and most exposed
- Breaching it is not subtle accumulation of ecosystem stress — it causes **immediate, unmistakable consequences**
- The Wizard's assault doesn't slowly weaken something. It kicks a load-bearing wall. The results are visible, fast, and undeniable.
- This makes the endgame dramatically legible — players will *see* it break, not infer it from distributed data

**Narrative implication:** The Wizard's plan shifts from "worrying long-term threat" to "if he does this, something breaks *right now* in ways everyone can feel." Raises urgency without requiring the players to have done all the cosmological homework first.

**⚠️ HERO_HEAVEN_THRESHOLD.md update required:** Extend with this framing — Threshold as concentrated ecosystem wiring, breach = immediate consequence, not just "cracking containment over time."

### Decision 8: Three-Layer Revelation Structure — LOCKED
*(Largely resolved by Decisions 1–7)*

The nested story arc shape is confirmed:

- **Layer 1 (early campaign):** Stop the Wizard — surface quest, discovered through investigation
- **Layer 2 (mid-late campaign):** The Wizard's Threshold breach causes immediate cosmological consequences — the Held Breath stirs, Warren structure destabilizes, the world *feels* it
- **Layer 3 (endgame):** Stopping the Wizard is necessary but not sufficient — the heroes must also reckon with the Warren-level agendas and entities disturbed by the liberation 1,000 years ago, and address what was already stirring before the Wizard ever made his move

**Integration note:** Layer 3 ties Decision 6 (liberation disturbance) directly into the endgame. The heroes' incomplete charge is not just "stop the Wizard" and not just "repair damage" — it's "account for everything your power has touched, including what you woke a thousand years ago." The tools (as Warren ambassadors, Decision 5) are the mechanism by which they can act at this scale.
