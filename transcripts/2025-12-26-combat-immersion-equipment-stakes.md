## THEO TTRPG: Combat Flow at Different Power Levels

**GURPS COMBAT (Granular, Tactical, Equipment-Heavy)**

Session 2 (Low Power, ~100 points):
- Equipment MATTERS mechanically (sword vs. axe, armor rating, HP pool)
- Combat is turn-based, tactical
- Equipment choices from creation have REAL stakes now
- Immersion comes from tactical uncertainty + consequence visibility
- Death is possible; survival is uncertain

Session 5 (Mid Power, ~150 points):
- Equipment still matters but warrior is superior
- Combat is faster (warrior wins more rolls)
- Immersion: "I'm growing competent. Equipment shows advancement."
- Victory is likely; death unlikely

Session 8+ (High Power, ~200+ points):
- Equipment is present but warrior is overwhelming
- Combat is nearly automatic
- PROBLEM: Tactical immersion is GONE
- Immersion shifts to narrative: "What's the cost of victory?"

**DAGGERHEART DUALITY DICE (Lightweight, Emotional, Narrative)**

Session 2 (Few Charms):
- Equipment is flavor only (doesn't mechanically change roll)
- Roll 2d12, interpret result, narrate consequence
- Immersion from Hope/Fear tension, not equipment weight
- PROBLEM: Equipment feels divorced from mechanics
- Sword vs. spear doesn't matter; shield doesn't protect

Session 5 (Several Charms):
- Same mechanical experience as Session 2
- Immersion from Charms + Hope/Fear, not escalation
- Equipment still divorced from mechanics

Session 8+ (Many Charms):
- Same dice resolution as Session 2 and 5
- PROBLEM: Duality Dice don't create escalation feeling
- A Session 8 hero rolls same dice as Session 2

**EXALTED CHARMS (Divine Power Recovery, Mechanically Expressive)**

Session 2 (2-3 Charms):
- Equipment matters somewhat (armor provides basic protection)
- Charms provide modest bonus (+2 to hit)
- Combat has tension; victory uncertain
- Immersion: "Charms feel special. I'm touching divinity."
- STAKES: Tactical—"Can I win? Do I have essence left?"

Session 5 (4-5 Charms):
- Equipment matters less; hero clearly superior
- Charms provide significant bonuses
- Enemy struggles to land hits
- Immersion: "Charms feel POWERFUL. I'm remembering divinity."
- STAKES shift: "What am I willing to do? How many Charms will I burn?"

Session 8+ (6+ powerful Charms):
- Equipment barely matters
- Charms resolve actions (often automatic success)
- Victory is assumed
- Immersion: "Hero is god-like. Stakes are entirely narrative."
- PROBLEM: Combat is no longer a challenge

## MYTHWEAVER: What "Immersion" Actually Means at Each Stage

Immersion is NOT constant. It changes at each power level:

**Session 2 Immersion:** Tactical + Equipment consequence
- "Will I survive?"
- "My armor choice is saving my life"
- Equipment creates concrete, visible stakes

**Session 5 Immersion:** Power fantasy + Strategic choice
- "I'm becoming capable"
- "Which Charm should I use?"
- Equipment is fading; Charms are real mechanical anchor

**Session 8+ Immersion:** Narrative + Moral consequence
- "What does using this power cost me?"
- "Should I do this?"
- Equipment is pure flavor

**THE PROBLEM:** If you use GURPS equipment weight at Session 2, then switch to narrative stakes at Session 8, players feel abandonment. The immersion they built collapses.

---

## LORE KEEPER: The Real Issue

You need:
1. Equipment is mechanical (not flavor) at low power
2. Power scales visibly (Session 8 hero rolls differently than Session 2)
3. Charms are additive (Charm bonus + base capability)
4. It's lightweight enough (doesn't overshadow narrative)

**Daggerheart's Duality Dice can't do #1, #2, or #5 simultaneously.**

## MYTHWEAVER: The Layering Architecture

Possible three-layer structure:

```
Layer 1: Duality Dice (Hope/Fear) as BASE resolution
         - Roll 2d12 (Hope/Fear)
         - Outcome determines success/partial/failure
         - Hope/Fear axis connects directly to R/H/K choices

Layer 2: Equipment Modifiers (SEPARATE from Duality Dice)
         - Armor: Provides damage reduction
         - Weapons: Provide damage bonus or special effect
         - Adds tactical weight without complicating roll

Layer 3: Charms (Exalted-style) as POWER escalation
         - Charm provides bonus to Duality Dice roll OR modifies outcome
         - Charm activation might cost Hope (R/H/K feedback loop)
         - Higher Charms feel like power growth
```

**Result:** Equipment matters mechanically (Session 2). Charms layer on for power (Session 5+). Hope/Fear tied to R/H/K throughout.

## THEO TTRPG: GURPS vs. Daggerheart Philosophy

**GURPS says:**
"Every point you spend on a character shows up in resolution."
- Higher DEX affects initiative and defense rolls directly
- Weapon Skill affects hit chance directly
- Equipment bonus affects damage directly
- Every advantage changes resolution numbers
- **Result:** Character building choices have mechanical weight in EVERY roll

**Daggerheart says:**
"Resolution is simple. Complexity comes from what you can DO, not how you roll."
- 2d12 is always the roll, regardless of skill
- Your Charms (what you can do) change outcomes, not base skill
- **Result:** Character building matters through abilities, not granular resolution modifiers

**FUNDAMENTAL INCOMPATIBILITY:** You can't have both cleanly.

## THEO TTRPG: The Three Paths

**Path A: Full GURPS for Low-Power, Transition Later**
- Sessions 1-4: GURPS granular combat (30+ minutes per combat)
- Sessions 5+: Transition to Charm-based (Exalted-style)
- PROBLEM: Transition is jarring. Players feel abandoned from Session 2's weight.
- BENEFIT: Early immersion is STRONG. Stakes are undeniable.

**Path B: GURPS Throughout, Charms Layer On**
- Sessions 1-8: GURPS stays base, Charms add bonuses on top
- Combat gets increasingly complex (GURPS + Charms both matter)
- PROBLEM: Sessions 8+ combats could take an hour
- BENEFIT: Equipment always matters. Smooth scaling. Consistency.

**Path C: Light Resolution Throughout, Find Stakes Elsewhere**
- Sessions 1-8: Duality Dice (or similar light) stays base
- Equipment provides damage reduction/bonuses, not roll modifiers
- Charms provide roll bonuses or special effects
- Combat is fast (5-10 minutes)
- PROBLEM: Equipment feels less consequential
- BENEFIT: Consistent. Fast. Scales smoothly. Stakes from R/H/K, Hope, tool agency.

## THEO TTRPG: The Solution (Respecting Transparency)

**Honest Version:**

In Session Zero, tell players transparently:
```
"You have standard attributes (everyone STR 10, DEX 10, etc.).
You roll 2d12 (Hope/Fear) to resolve actions.
Your attributes provide narrative flavor and small bonuses in specific situations.
Charms, when recovered, provide mechanical bonuses.
You don't start with skills; you discover your capabilities through play."

But you don't explain the implications.

Players naturally assume: "High DEX helps me roll better on 2d12."

Through play, they discover: "Actually, Duality Dice outcome is what matters. My DEX is mostly narrative."

**This is NOT deception. This is letting players discover implications through play.**

## CHARACTER CREATION: The Bounded Choice System

**Step 1: Choose Archetype**
- Breaker, Bridge, Seeker, Sacrificer, Warrior, Visionary

**Step 2: Standard Attributes (Everyone the same)**
- STR 10, DEX 10, CON 10, INT 10, WIS 10, CHR 10
- (Baseline. Everyone starts equal.)

**Step 3: Pick TWO Attributes to Augment**
- Raise each by 1-3 points
- DEX could go from 10 → 11, 12, or 13
- Archetype guidance (not requirements):
  - Breaker: STR (strength) + WIS (understanding what to break)
  - Warrior: STR + DEX
  - Bridge: CHR (charisma) + WIS (wisdom)
  - Seeker: INT (intelligence) + WIS (perception)

**Step 4: Define Aspirational R/H/K**
- What do you think you Resist?
- What do you think you Hunger for?
- What do you think you Know?

**Step 5: That's it. No skills. No equipment list. No other mechanics.**

## HOW IT WORKS IN PRACTICE

### **SESSION 2: Low Power Combat (No Charms Yet)**

**Setup:**
```
Hero (Warrior archetype): STR 12, DEX 10
Brigand opponent: STR 10, DEX 10
```

**Combat Round:**

```
HERO: "I attack the brigand with my sword."

GM: "Alright. Roll for this action."

HERO: Rolls 2d12 (Hope/Fear)
      Result: 8 Hope, 7 Fear (Success)

GM: "You land a solid hit. Your sword connects.
     Because you have STR 12, that hit is particularly forceful.
     Roll damage: d6 base + 1 (strength advantage) = 5 damage.
     The brigand staggers back, bleeding."

BRIGAND's turn:
     Rolls to attack hero.
     Result: 9 Fear, 8 Hope (Partial success)

GM: "The brigand swings at you. It's a glancing blow, but connects.
     You take 4 damage.
     [Because you only have DEX 10 (no bonus), you can't dodge effectively]"
```

**What player learns:**
- My STR 12 made my attack better (+1 damage)
- My DEX 10 (no bonus) meant I couldn't reduce damage
- Attributes matter, but they're small bonuses (not game-changing)

---

### **SESSION 5: Mid-Power Combat (4 Charms Recovered)**

**Setup:**
```
Hero: STR 12, DEX 10, + 4 Charms
Charms include: "Warrior's Strike" (+2 to attack roll), "Iron Skin" (damage reduction)
Threat: Multiple brigands (escalated)
```

**Combat Round:**

```
HERO: "I'm using Warrior's Strike to attack the first brigand."

GM: "Alright. Roll with the Charm bonus."

HERO: Rolls 2d12
      Result: 9 Hope, 6 Fear (Strong success)

GM: "Your Charm guides your strike. It's devastating.
     Roll damage: d6 base + 2 (Charm bonus) + 1 (STR bonus) = 7 damage.
     [Notice: Charm added more than STR did]
     The brigand goes down."

BRIGANDS attack:
     Multiple brigands hit hero.
     Hero takes 8 damage total.

HERO: "Can I use Iron Skin to reduce that?"

GM: "Yes. Iron Skin absorbs 4 damage. You take 4 damage.
     [Your DEX 10 would have reduced it by 1. Your Charm reduced it by 4.]"
```

**What player learns:**
- My STR 12 is still relevant (+1 damage)
- My DEX 10 is barely noticeable compared to Charms
- Charms are doing the heavy lifting now
- Attributes feel less important

---

### **SESSION 8: High Power Combat (8 Charms)**

**Setup:**
```
Hero: STR 12, DEX 10, + 8 Charms (including powerful celestial abilities)
Threat: Major antagonist
```

**Combat Round:**

```
HERO: "I activate Celestial Wrath to strike at them."

GM: "Roll with the Charm."

HERO: Rolls 2d12
      Result: 10 Hope, 5 Fear (Strong success)

GM: "Your celestial power flows through you. The strike is nearly unstoppable.
     Roll damage: d6 + 3 (Celestial Wrath Tier 3) + 1 (STR 12) = 10 damage.
     [The STR bonus is cosmically irrelevant at this point]"

ANTAGONIST attacks:
     Rolls to hit hero.
     Result: Partial success.

HERO: "Do I activate Iron Skin?"

GM: "You could. It would absorb 6 damage. Or you could just take the hit."

HERO: "Just take it. I'm a god now. A few scratches don't matter."
```

**What player realizes:**
- My STR 12 is basically flavor
- My DEX 10 was never the point
- Celestial power is what matters
- I'm not a skilled warrior. I'm remembering divinity.

```

- The attribute you chose says something about who you thought you were
- Over time, it becomes less relevant
- By the end, it's purely characterization
- But it was honest the whole time
