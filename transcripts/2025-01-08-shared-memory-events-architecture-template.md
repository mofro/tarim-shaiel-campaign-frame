**KEY INSIGHTS FROM THIS DESIGN:**

1. **Thematic grouping accommodates player uncertainty** — Since you don't know which archetypes players will choose, grouping by THEME (not by pair) means events work flexibly

2. **Narrative triggers are more powerful than mechanical ones** — A trigger like "When character resists their tool's whispered impulse and chooses restraint" works with any Warrior + any other archetype

3. **Asymmetric emotional/informational balance is stronger** — Events don't need to be 50/50 emotion and info. Some are 70/30 emotional, others 30/70 informational. Each tells the full story across the 5 events.

4. **Specific narrative imagery is essential** — You correctly noted that abstract triggers are worthless without concrete scenes. 400-600 word imagery per event is the real work.

5. **Different pairings should see different aspects of same scene** — Warrior+Bridge notices what dialogue accomplishes. Warrior+Breaker notices the moment of choosing not to break. Seeker+Warrior notices the information the Warrior was working with.
